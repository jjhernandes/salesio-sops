The 3 Levers
LEVER 01
Show Rate — Your Biggest Problem
Currently 39%  ·  Industry standard 70–75%
You booked 179 calls. Only 70 showed up. That means 109 people who were interested enough to book — just didn't show. At a 47% close rate, those 109 no-shows represent roughly 51 closes that never happened.

This is almost entirely a pre-call sequence problem. People need confirmation, a reason to show, and social proof between booking and the call. Right now there's a gap.

LEVER 03
Offer Rate — Two Valid Approaches
Current offer rate: 83%  ·  Pick the philosophy that fits your game
On roughly 12 of your 70 calls, no offer was made. There are two legitimate ways to think about this — and they lead to different strategies. Pick the one that matches how you want to operate.
Calls taken70
Calls where no offer was made (~17%)~12 calls
If those 12 get an offer at 47% close rate5–6 additional closes
Revenue upside if you offer everyone+$17K–$21K
Option A — Offer Everyone
Always make an offer. Let them say no — don't say no for them. Even a bad fit gets to hear the offer and DQ themselves.

Upside: 5–6 more closes/month, $17K–$21K more revenue.
Downside: More time on calls that won't close. Energy goes to low-probability prospects.
Option B — Protect Your Time
Only offer people who are genuinely qualified. DQ fast, hard, and early — and protect your energy for the calls that matter.

Upside: Higher energy on high-quality calls. Close rate on those calls goes up.
Downside: Leaves money on the table — some of those 12 would have closed.
Either approach works. What doesn't work is being unclear about which one you're running. If you're doing Option B, your DQ criteria need to be sharp and consistent — not just gut feel on a bad day. If you're doing Option A, track why you didn't offer on each no-offer call and see if the reasons hold up.

Exactly What to Do
01
Build a pre-call sequence. Confirmation the moment they book. A check-in 24 hours before. A reminder 1 hour out. Each message should include a reason to show — a testimonial, a result, a quick "here's what we'll cover." Most no-shows happen because people forget or lose momentum. Kill that gap.
02
Default to the $5.8K offer. Every call. $2.8K becomes the step-down if they can't do $5.8K — not the starting point. The data confirms: you close the premium offer at a higher rate. Stop anchoring low.
03
Decide which offer rate philosophy you're running — and commit to it. If you're offering everyone, track the 12 no-offer calls and see what you're leaving behind. If you're protecting your time, tighten your DQ criteria so the decision is sharp and consistent, not a gut call that varies day to day.
