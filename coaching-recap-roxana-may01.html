The First Thing to Understand

Sales is not what you think it is. Throw out the old idea.

Most people think sales is transactional — get on a call, say the right things, take someone's money. That's wrong.

Sales is transformational. You're communicating a specific way to influence a specific decision. You're eliciting change inside someone. The money is just the currency of that change.

"The way you are, most authentically, is the best way to show up."

JJ, on sales identity

What makes great salespeople great isn't what they say. It's self-management and self-recognition. That's what this program builds.

Clarity Systems, Not Goals

There's a difference between a goal and a clarity system. Goals are "I want to do X in Y time." A clarity system is execution made so clear that a 5-year-old could follow it step by step.

If a 5-year-old can't explain your next move, it's not clear enough yet.

The example: Andrew, 18 years old, making $10K/month as a setter in Spain. His goal was $25-30K/month so he could travel. The bottleneck? He needed to become a closer, but no closer roles were open for 6 months. So the first step was simple: speak to his sales manager at Tuesday's 1-on-1 to get on their radar. That's it. That's a clarity system.

It sounds simple. That's the point. When you're clear, the next step is obvious.

Identity Before Income

This is the most important concept from today — and it applies to everything you'll learn.

Most People Think

Hit the income first. Then change. "Once I'm making $30K a month, I'll feel like myself."

How It Actually Works

Become the person first. The income is a byproduct of who you become. You don't earn your way into identity.

Once you land a role and you're selling a $10K offer at 10% commission, the math is simple — you know exactly how many closes you need. But right now, the work is on becoming the kind of closer who gets there. That's what these 12 weeks are.

Setter vs. Closer

You asked. Here's the breakdown.

Appointment Setter

Qualifies and books leads onto calls. Confirms appointments. Gets paid ~5% commission. That person who called you before this call? That was Faris — a setter.

Closer

Takes the booked call and closes the deal. Collects the cash. Gets paid ~10% commission. Higher income ceiling. Usually where setters grow into.

No wrong answer on which to start with. Most people start as setters and move to closing once they understand the full sales process. We'll figure out which makes the most sense for you as you go through the program.
