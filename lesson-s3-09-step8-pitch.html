Step 8 — The Pitch

Connect every feature to what they said. Nothing goes in the pitch that wasn't in their discovery. Nothing.

The Core Rule

Every element of your pitch must connect back to something the prospect told you. If you can't trace it to a specific thing they said — leave it out of this pitch.

This is the entire difference between a pitch that feels like it was built for this person and one that sounds like you're reading from a brochure. One is a mirror. The other is a monologue. People close on mirrors.

What This Means Practically

You know your product cold. You know every feature, every component, every proof point. The amateur pitches all of them. The elite rep pitches the three or four that directly address what the prospect just told you they need — and connects them explicitly to what was said.

How to Connect Features to Pain

The Structure

Every feature or component should follow this pattern: name what it is, then immediately reference what the prospect said that makes it relevant.

Feature DumpConnected Feature
"We do weekly 1-on-1 coaching sessions with JJ and the coaching staff." "The weekly 1-on-1s — you said you've never had someone actually watching your calls and giving you real feedback. That's what these are for. Not generic advice — they look at your specific calls and tell you exactly what's off."
"We have a proprietary job board with 60-80 vetted companies hiring every week." "You mentioned you're stuck on an offer that doesn't match your skills and you want out. The job board has 60-80 companies actively hiring right now — and we guarantee placement within the program. That piece you said was non-negotiable — it's solved."
"We teach the psychology of sales, not scripts." "You said you've tried scripts and they make you sound robotic. What we teach is the opposite — the psychological reason people buy, so you can have a real conversation instead of reciting lines. That's the specific thing you said wasn't working."

Pitch Length — How Long Is Too Long

The Rule

A well-built pitch for this offer is 5-8 minutes. 10 minutes is the ceiling. Beyond that, you've shifted from presenting to overwhelming — and overwhelmed prospects default to "I need to think about it" because the amount of information requires more processing time than they want to give on the call.

If your pitch is going over 10 minutes, you're feature dumping. You've run out of things from the discovery and you're adding components because you know they're good, not because this specific prospect needs them. Cut it. The prospect doesn't need to know everything you offer — they need to know the things that answer the questions they already have.

The Discipline

Before the pitch, run through what you know from the discovery. Identify the top 3-4 things the prospect told you they need. Build the pitch around those. Everything else either gets one sentence or gets cut entirely. Your pitch is not a tour of the program — it's a targeted answer to their specific situation.

What Feature Dumping Sounds Like

Feature dumping is when a rep presents every component of the offer in sequence, without connecting any of them to what the prospect said. It sounds like a tour. The prospect's eyes glaze over. They start thinking about other things. Interest fades not because the offer isn't good — but because none of it sounds personal to them.

  • ✗
    Listing features with "we also have..." transitions — each one feels additive instead of relevant
  • ✗
    Not referencing anything the prospect said during discovery — the pitch sounds pre-built
  • ✗
    Going past 10 minutes without a check-in — prospect is no longer tracking
  • ✗
    Pitching features the prospect never indicated they cared about — it's noise

Call Exchange — Feature Dump vs. Connected Pitch

VERSION 1 — Feature Dump
Rep
"So the program is 12 weeks. You get weekly 1-on-1s, group coaching calls — we do Mock Call Monday, Setting Mastery, Coffee Chat, Sales Psychology, B2B Strategy, Inner Circle Advanced, and Call Review. You also get the WhatsApp community, the job board with 60-80 companies per week, and access to quarterly in-person events in Orlando. Plus the money-back guarantee and guaranteed job placement."
That's everything. In 20 seconds. None of it is connected to what this person said. They're hearing a product description, not a solution to their problem. Interest is low. Objections will be high.
VERSION 2 — Connected Pitch
Rep
"Okay, so based on what you told me — you said the core issue is that you're not converting under pressure, specifically at price, and you've never had anyone actually watch your calls and tell you what's wrong. That's the thing I want to show you how we address specifically."
Starts with a callback to discovery. Prospect thinks: "they were listening."
Rep
"The weekly 1-on-1s — that's someone watching your actual calls. Not your neighbor, not a YouTube coach — someone who closes at a high level themselves, going through your recordings and telling you exactly where your frame breaks and what to do differently. That's what you said was missing."
First feature, directly connected to what they said they needed. Prospect is nodding, not zoning out.
Rep
"You also said you want to get onto a better offer — the job board is 60-80 companies every week, vetted, actively hiring. And we guarantee placement within the program. That's not 'maybe you'll find something' — that's a guarantee."
Second feature, directly connected to the stated desire. Every sentence earns its place because it came from the discovery. This prospect is engaged.
