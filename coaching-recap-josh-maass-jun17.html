The Pierre Call — What We Covered
01
When the Prospect Is Already Problem-Aware — Don't Over-Dig
Where it happened in the call
Pierre told you upfront: he invested money in a program that did nothing, made barely $200/month after everything, and is ready to dump them. He is not unaware of his problem — he has been living it.

A lot of reps burn time digging into pain that has already been fully surfaced. When the prospect does your job for you like this, the work shifts immediately to solution awareness — why this, why now, why us, and what makes it different this time.
The Rule
If they can articulate their own problem clearly and with emotion, stop digging. Move to: why are they still looking? What would make them confident this is different?
02
You Never Kill the Resistance — So It Came Back Later
The skepticism moment
Pierre said it directly: "Now I'm very suspicious of a lot of companies." You acknowledged it — "as you should be" — and kept moving. That is a natural instinct and not wrong. But it left the resistance alive.

Unaddressed resistance does not disappear. It sits there. It quietly builds. And it shows back up at the end of the call as the reason they don't move forward. When you hear skepticism this specific, you need to stop and cut it before moving into solution.
The Rule
Two conditions required before moving to solution: (1) the problem is clear, and (2) there is no resistance. If both aren't true, you don't move forward yet.
The Question That Cuts It
"Hey, that makes total sense — I'd be the same way. When you're making this decision to work with us or another company, what are you looking for that would make that suspicion go away? Like, how are you actually gauging this decision?"
03
How Are You Gauging This Decision — The Most Powerful Question in the Conversation
What nobody else asks
This is not just for resistance. Any time you're in a conversation where the prospect is comparing options, sitting on the fence, or coming from a bad past experience — this is the question.

Most people do not know how they make decisions. When you ask them to articulate it, you challenge their own perception of their process — and you get a roadmap. If they have four questions, you know the four questions. If it's a vibe check, you know it's about certainty and presence. Either way, you are no longer guessing what the sale requires.
The Question
"How are you gauging this decision? Like, what gives you the certainty that you're choosing the right company this time around — is it specific answers you're looking for, or more of a feeling?"
The Real Truth Behind This
Nobody is buying the product. They are buying certainty. They want the "secret sauce" — something that makes them feel like this time is fundamentally different. The secret sauce is a new perspective: a way of looking at their situation they had never seen before. That is all you are giving them.
04
Go Deeper, Not Lateral — Stay in the Pocket
The most common mistake in your calls right now
When Pierre answered a question, you acknowledged it and moved to the next topic. That is a lateral move — you scratched the surface and kept going. The real information is always one layer deeper than what they first say.

"I don't quit" is not an answer. It's a sentence. The answer is: what does not quitting mean for him specifically? What would he look back on in 6 months and feel proud of? Going deeper on that one thread gives you more to work with than asking five separate questions ever will.
The Rule
Work in batches of clarity. This makes sense. This doesn't make sense. This makes sense. This doesn't. Every time something doesn't make sense — go deeper. Do not move laterally until you have full clarity on what you are already working with.
The Question That Should Have Been Asked
"I get that — you've been through it and you're still here. So given that bad experience, what is it about us specifically that made you feel like this one was worth a conversation?"
The Process Framework — Where These Moments Fit
Problem Awareness → Resistance Check → Solution Awareness → Pitch
You'll see this fully in Week 3. Here's how today's call maps to it.
1
Problem Awareness
How well does the prospect understand their own problem? Pierre: fully aware. Save the time you would have spent digging and redirect it to solution.
2
Resistance Check
Is there any skepticism, past damage, or unresolved tension? If yes — stop and address it. Don't move forward with resistance alive in the room.
3
Solution Awareness
Does the prospect believe a solution actually exists — and that you might be it? Build this through their own words, not your pitch. Ask what would make this feel different.
4
Pitch
You only get here when the first three are fully locked in. If you're here and you feel uncertainty — go back. The problem is upstream, not in the close.
