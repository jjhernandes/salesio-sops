Concept 1 — The Scale of Consciousness
From the book Power vs. Force (and expanded in Letting Go), every person operates from a default emotional baseline — a level they naturally return to when nothing external is pushing them up or pulling them down. This is not mindset or meditation. This is just how you operate, and it shapes everything: how you show up on calls, how you respond to rejection, how you connect with people.

The levels from highest to lowest:
Love / Joy
Everybody gravitates to this person. Easy to talk to. Sees the good in everyone.
Reason / Acceptance
Very practical. Focused on understanding why things happen. Logical, grounded.
Neutrality / Willingness
Neutral about outcomes. Willing to take steps. Doesn't over-attach or over-react.
Your Baseline
Courage
Willing to take action even when uncertain. A step above neutral.
Pride / Anger / Desire / Fear
Most sales reps land here. Afraid of losing their job, angry at their results, desiring money, prideful about being right.
Grief / Apathy / Guilt / Shame
Usually temporary. After a breakup, a bad week, an embarrassing moment. Nobody stays here long — and you should not stay here.
Your baseline is Neutrality and Willingness — that is a strong place to start. You don't hold onto things. You bounce back fast. And you're already moving toward something instead of away from something.
Concept 2 — What Moves You Up and Down
Knowing your baseline is step one. Knowing what elevates you and what drops you is how you start managing your state deliberately — so you are not at the mercy of circumstance on a sales call.
What Brings You Up
Seeing growth.
Not money specifically — growth. Momentum. Feeling like things are moving. When you see progress happening, you operate at your best.
What Can Bring You Down
Things out of your control.
You acknowledged this yourself (truck example). The key — you already bounce back fast. That is the skill. Keep building that.
As we work together, we will discover more of what moves you up and down in a sales context specifically — interviews that go sideways, calls where you feel lost, weeks where you're on fire. We will track all of it.
Concept 3 — Push vs. Pull Motivation
Two types of motivation exist. Most people operate from push — they're running from something. Proving people wrong, escaping a situation, trying to show they are worth something. That fuel burns out.

Pull motivation is different. You are so moved by a future you actually want that it pulls you toward it regardless of circumstance. It doesn't need a reason. It doesn't need external pressure.
Your pull is retiring your mom. That is not a goal — that is an identity. Everything you are doing is rooted in that. That kind of pull is rare and it will carry you further than money ever will.
You have some push too — the environment you grew up in, what you have seen, what you don't want to become. That is fine. Push can serve you. Just make sure it's the pull that leads.
Concept 4 — Your Environment
Your environment is the makeup of your reality. It does not matter if your roommates are bad people — if they are not operating at the level you want to operate at, being around them all day has an effect on you whether you notice it or not.

This is not about cutting people off. It is about being intentional with the space you are in when you are working.
Create a space you control. Where you work, what surrounds you when you are on calls, what you consume. Sales is the only industry where the more you work on it, the more it works on you. That means your environment matters more here than almost anywhere else.
"Reality doesn't matter. Your perception of reality does."
Concept 5 — Time, Attention, and Energy
Where your time and attention go is where your energy goes. And where your energy goes determines what you actually have capacity to do in the world.

This is not about hustle. It's about being honest with yourself. Are you thinking about debt all day? Your job? Freedom? A person? Whatever occupies the most mental real estate is what your energy is already funding — whether you wanted it to or not.
You said your time and attention mostly goes to work — and that even though you dislike your job, you still want to perform well in it. That says a lot about your character. It also means the thought of something better is already feeding you, not draining you. That is a strong signal.
