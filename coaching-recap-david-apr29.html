The Bottlenecks — In Order
01
No consistent process. Every call is different. Some of them hit. When they miss, you don't know why, because there's no baseline to compare against. You can't improve what you can't measure, and you can't measure what you never built.
02
Follow-up is broken. You admitted it. Leads no-show, you send the demo video, they don't watch it, you can't get them back. There's no follow-up sequence — just one-off attempts. This is a pipeline leak that's costing you closes every week.
03
Financial qualification is inconsistent. The Social Security lady, the course junkie, the bankruptcy case — you're getting them to the call and finding out they can't pay once they're there. Front-end qualification needs to be sharper before you invest a call in them.
04
The demo video is de-selling your sets. Experienced investors show up expecting a lender list. The video emphasizes coaching and mentorship — which immediately turns them off. This is a marketing problem, not just a sales problem, but you can compensate for it in how you frame the call before they watch it.
05
No KPI tracking. You don't have a personal dashboard. The sales manager tracks it, but you can't see it clearly, and you can't act on what you can't see in real time. JJ sent you a tracking sheet — use it.
The Real Pattern — Thinking vs. Doing

This came up unprompted, and it's important enough to name directly. You're a high-level thinker. You caught the first-principles framework faster than anyone JJ has run it with. You can reverse-engineer complex problems in real time. That's a genuine asset.

But it comes with a tax. The hardest part for you isn't the action — it's getting started. Once you're in flow, you're in flow. The gap is the moment between knowing what to do and doing it.

The Loop You're In
You think about what you need to do. You understand it completely. You know exactly why it matters. Then you don't do it — not because you're lazy, but because the activation energy to start is higher than it should be.

Sleep is the clearest example: wake up at 7, feel fine, tell yourself you need more, go back to sleep, wake up at 10. The decision happens before you're fully conscious — and you lose the day.
How You Break It
You don't fix this with more motivation. You fix it with external constraints that make the action automatic.

— Go to bed at 10pm. Not midnight. 10pm. The morning problem is a night problem.
— Book your first call for 8am. Now you have a reason to be up.
— Reduce the friction on everything. The videographer next door is JJ's example. What's yours?
"The task isn't hard. Getting yourself to start the task is. Once that behavior breaks once — you systemize it. That's it. That's the whole game."
What's Being Built for You
From the calls + scripts you sent
You sent your script variations and bad calls to jj@sales.io. From those, JJ will build:

1. A consolidated call framework — not a script to memorize, but a structure you run every call through. Takes your best instincts and puts them in a repeatable sequence so when you're off, you have something to fall back on.

2. A follow-up process — a specific sequence for no-shows, demo video non-watchers, and soft "I'll think about it" ends. Each stage has a clear action and a clear timeline.

3. A front-end qualification filter — a set of 2-3 questions that catches the unqualified (Social Security leads, course junkies, bankruptcy cases) before they consume a call slot.

4. A demo video reframe — language you use before they watch it that sets expectations so experienced investors aren't blindsided by the coaching component. You can't change the video, but you can change what they think they're about to watch.
