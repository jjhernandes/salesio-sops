Baseline

The Sales.io Framework — 12 Steps

"Never pitch just to pitch. You only present once you have earned the right to present. A robust discovery phase prevents forced pitches and objections."
— Sales.io Core Rule · JJ Hernandez

This is the framework every ESI call is built on. Every phase in the ESI 10-phase script is an application of one or more of these steps to the immigrant e-commerce market. Know this framework cold — it tells you WHY each phase exists and what you're trying to accomplish inside it.

1
Step One
Intro — Respect First, Likability Later
Goal: Establish control, confirm time, open appropriately
"Hey [Name], good to meet you. I know you booked a call about [context]. Do you still have the full [time] for today?"

Lead with calm authority, not impression-seeking. Optional opener: "Before we dive in — what motivated you to book the call?"

2
Step Two
Current Reality
Goal: Understand their present situation and approach
  • "What are you currently doing for [sales / income / business]?"
  • "How long have you been doing that?"
  • "What made you go that route?"
  • "What's working?" · "What's not working?"

Progress from surface-level to deeper inquiry gradually. Don't rush past this to get to pain.

3
Step Three
Problem Awareness
Goal: Develop complete understanding of real problems and their impact
  • "What do you feel is the biggest issue with where things are right now?"
  • "What have you tried to fix it?" · "Why do you think that hasn't worked yet?"
  • "What is this costing you right now?" · "How long has this been going on?"
If they say things are fine: "I hear you — but if everything was exactly where you wanted it, why are we on this call?"
4
Step Four
Solution Awareness
Goal: Assess how they think about solutions — gaps often emerge here
  • "How do you know what the right solution would even look like?"
  • "How are you deciding who or what to trust?"
  • "What would need to be true for you to feel confident moving forward?"
  • "What do you feel is missing from your current approach?"

This step is often skipped — and it's where closers leave the most on the table. Their answer to "what would need to be true" becomes your closing criteria. Hit every point they name before you pitch.

5
Step Five
Future Pace
Goal: Establish the gap between current state and desired future
  • "If this was fully solved, what would be different?"
  • "What would that allow you to do?"
  • "What would life look like 3–6 months from now if this changed?"
  • "What would make this worth it for you?"

Keep their answers grounded and specific. Vague future pace = vague buying motivation = a close that depends on your technique, not their desire.

6
Step Six
Consequence / Cost of Inaction
Goal: Illuminate the cost of staying the same
  • "What happens if this stays the same for the next 3–6 months?"
  • "What does that cost you — not just financially, but in time, health, family?"
  • "What are you worried could happen if this doesn't get fixed?"
  • "Is staying where you are actually an option for you?"

Deploy only when the emotional foundation exists. A prospect who hasn't verbalized pain yet will hear these questions as pressure. A prospect who has verbalized pain will hear them as clarity.

7
Step Seven
Transition to Pitch
Goal: Summarize what you heard and earn the right to present
"Based on everything you told me, I do think this could be a really strong fit. The main thing I'm hearing is [their problem], and that's creating [their consequence]. Let me walk you through exactly how we'd help you solve that."

The pitch should feel inevitable — not random. If you have to force the transition, discovery isn't done.

8
Step Eight
Pitch
Goal: Connect the offering directly to their problems and stated criteria
  • Restate their problem → explain the solving mechanism → detail how it works → connect each feature to what they said they wanted
  • Check-ins throughout: "Does that make sense?" / "Do you see how that would help with [their specific problem]?"

"Do not feature dump. Every part of the pitch should connect to something they already said." If a feature doesn't connect to their discovery, cut it or come back to it in Q&A.

9
Step Nine
Confirm Fit
Goal: Establish agreement before discussing investment
"Based on everything we covered — do you feel like this is the right fit for what you're trying to solve?"

If yes: "Why do you feel that way?" — make them articulate it. This is a verbal commitment and becomes your closing ammunition. Never reveal price before this step.

10
Step Ten
Price Reveal
Goal: State the investment calmly, once, without over-explaining
"Cool. So the investment for the program is [$X]. Is that reachable for you?"

No drama, no apology, no speed. Say it once and wait. The first one who talks after the price is said is the one who loses.

11
Step Eleven
Objection Handling
Goal: Address concerns with understanding, not argumentation
Energy shifts: "[Name], I feel like the energy shifted a little. Am I wrong, or is there something I'm not seeing?"
Cost raised: "I hear you. Is it truly that the money is impossible — or is it more that you're not fully confident this will work for you yet?"
Deliberation: "Of course. What specifically do you feel like you need to think through?"

Create psychological safety and curiosity. Never argue. The real objection is almost never the stated one.

12
Step Twelve
Close
Goal: Facilitate commitment to their own stated objectives
"Based on what you told me, this is the exact problem you said you wanted to solve. You also said staying where you are isn't really an option. So if the only thing left is getting started — let's take care of that now."

They commit to transformation, not merely a program. The close is not you asking them to buy. It is you reflecting their own words back and asking if they meant them.

Foundation

The Invisible Layer — What the Script Can't Give You

The Four Levels of Communication

What you say accounts for 7% of whether you close. How you say it — tonality, pacing, energy — is 38%. Your intention matters more. But the variable that drives everything else is how you feel about yourself on that call. A closer who genuinely believes they are handing someone a life-changing opportunity will outperform a closer reading a perfect script from a state of desperation every single time.

No Neutral Influence

From the first second of the call, you are either moving this person up or pulling them down. There is no neutral. Your state transfers. If you are anxious about making a quota, they feel it without knowing what it is. If you are genuinely curious about their life and detached from the outcome, they feel that too. The prospect's state at the close is a reflection of your state throughout the call.

Connection Is What People Actually Buy

The program, the pillars, the WhatsApp group — these are proof points. But the decision to buy is almost always a decision about the person on the other side of the call. They are asking: does this person actually understand my life? Do I trust them? Do I feel seen? If the answer is yes to all three, every objection becomes manageable. If it's no, no financing structure will close them.

Protocol
2-Minute Pre-Call Check

Before every call, answer these three out loud:

  • Do I genuinely believe this program can change this person's life? (If no — review the Wins channel first.)
  • Am I curious about this specific person, or am I running a script? (If script — re-read their questionnaire like a novel.)
  • Does this sale change anything for me emotionally? (If yes — reframe: "It changes nothing for me whether they do this. But it changes everything for them.")
Foundation

PACE Personality Typing

ICP (A/B/C) tells you about their situation. PACE tells you about their personality — how they process the pitch, what moves them, and how to handle their specific objections. Type them in the first 5 minutes by listening to HOW they speak, not just what they say. Most prospects are a blend — pick the dominant trait.

P
Practical
Signals: "How does this work exactly?" Asks for specifics before committing. Skeptical of vague claims.
Adjust: Lead with the math ($333/day = 3 perfumes). Show the step-by-step of the credit sequence. Don't rush to emotion — they need to understand the mechanism first.
A
Action
Signals: "I'm ready to move." Fast talker, impatient, competitive. Hates being left behind.
Adjust: Short discovery. Show who's already winning. Use time urgency. Skip the mindset pillar, double down on business results. Close early and direct.
S
Social
Signals: Talks about family constantly. "My wife says…" Asks about the community. Needs to belong.
Adjust: Emphasize the 400-student community, the Wins channel, the private group. Show a real private 1-on-1 chat. Family angle is the close. Mental vaccine is critical — their family will try to talk them out of it.
E
Emotional
Signals: Tells their full story unprompted. Goes deep on pain — depression, loneliness, exhaustion. Says "I just want a different life."
Adjust: Slow down. Don't pitch over their pain — go into it. Use their exact words in the mirror recap. The TikTok pitch (3 things they said they valued most) is most powerful here.
Phase 01

Pre-Call Prep

1
Before the Call
Sales.io: Prep
5 min before

Read the questionnaire. Classify ICP (A/B/C). Read setter + confirmer context in Airtable. Pick 2 success stories. Tabs open: Wins channel, platform, checkout link. Camera on, quiet room.

Morning confirmation (mandatory): Send to all same-day leads. Confirms attendance and plants authority frame before the call.

Sales.io Add
Pre-Classify PACE + Identity Type from the Questionnaire

Before the call, scan HOW they answered the questionnaire — not just what they said. Short, direct answers = A or P. Long emotional paragraphs = E. Repeated mentions of family = S.

Also identify their likely identity type: historical (past failure or success they reference), role (leads with job title or expertise), social (mentions family or community). Match your proof case to their identity, not just their job title.

Phase 02

Opening + Frame

2
0–3 min
Sales.io: Step 1
Rapport → Frame → Commitment

Rapport (60–90 sec): Find one real connection point. "Living the dream or not yet?" plants the pain seed in second 30.

"Look, [NAME]. This call is not a sales call. It's an audit of your life here in the United States. I want to understand where you are, what's holding you back, and where you want to go. The only thing I ask: at the end, you give me a yes or a no. Can I count on that?"

Wait for the verbal yes. Do not continue without it. Confirm time: "Do you have 45 minutes to an hour, calm, no interruptions?"

Sales.io — Step 1
Lead with Calm Authority, Not Impression-Seeking

The Sales.io frame: respect first, likability later. The opener is not about being liked — it is about establishing control of the conversation before discovery begins. The "audit not a sale" frame is the ESI version of this. It only lands if your voice sounds like someone who doesn't need them to say yes.

Listen for their PACE type in their first 30–60 words. Adjust tone before discovery begins.

Phase 03

Early Qualification

3
3–8 min
Sales.io: Steps 2 + 4
Reality · Capital · Decision Readiness

Confirm questionnaire, credit situation, banking, family structure, available capital. Get the money question done before minute 10.

"When you booked, you said if we show you a system to build $25K+ in credit and an e-commerce business at $10K/month, you'd have [$X] to invest. That money — is it in your bank account, in cash, on a card? And if at the end of this call you see this is exactly what you want, would there be any problem making that transfer on this call?"
Sales.io — Steps 2 + 4
Current Reality + Solution Awareness Run Together Here

Step 2 (Current Reality): Status, job, years, credit, bank. This is surface-level fact-finding — don't skip it, but don't linger. Move quickly to depth.

Step 4 (Solution Awareness): The money question is solution awareness in disguise. You're not just finding out if they have capital — you're finding out how they make decisions. "If you saw this was right for you, is there anything that would stop you from moving today?" surfaces the real blockers before they become objections.

Add: "What would need to be true for you to feel fully confident moving forward?" — write down their answer. Hit every criteria they name before you close.

Phase 04

Discovery + Pain

4
8–20 min
Sales.io: Steps 2, 3, 4, 5, 6
The heart of the call

This phase is where the sale happens. Run the pain ladder in order: Situation → Dream → Trigger → Brake → Attempts → Consequence → Motive. Do not move to the pitch until they have said out loud (1) what hurts, (2) what it's costing them, and (3) why today.

Sales.io — Steps 2–6

Sales.io Framework
Cognitive Dissonance — Widen the Identity-Reality Gap

Every prospect has a self-image (hard worker, good parent, someone who figured it out when they came here). Then there is their reality: trading time for money, same place they were 3 years ago.

Your job is to widen the gap between those two things until staying the same feels more uncomfortable than changing. The ESI "wasted advantage confrontation" is already doing this — apply it across all prospect types, not just citizens.

"You're clearly someone who came here and worked harder than most. And yet — you're still in the same spot. How does that sit with someone like you?"

Then wait. The discomfort of that gap is the decision.

Sales.io Add
Active Listening — Repeat Their Life Better Than They Told It

The most powerful move in discovery isn't a question. It's a reflection. When you give their life back to them with more clarity than they had when they were living it, you become the most trusted person they've spoken to in years.

"So what I'm hearing is — you didn't fail, you got hit by something external and didn't have a system to absorb it. That taught you that depending on one income, one job, one person saying yes to you every week is the most dangerous position you can be in. Is that right?"

When they say "exactly" — you have earned the authority to prescribe. Not before.

Phase 05

Mirror Recap + "You Qualify"

5
20–23 min
Sales.io: Step 7
Reflect → Confirm → Permission to Pitch

Repeat their life back in their exact words. Wait for the yes. Then: "You qualify." Use their exact words — if they said "estancado," say "estancado."

"Let me make sure I understood you, [NAME]. You've been [X] years here. You work [X] hours in [JOB]. What's held you back most is [THEIR WORDS]. What you want is [THEIR WORDS]. And you're tired of [THEIR WORDS], because if nothing changes, in 3 years you'll be exactly where you are today. Am I right so far? ... Perfect. [NAME], you qualify to work with us. Let me explain how this works."
Sales.io — Step 7
This Is the Transition — Earn the Right Before You Pitch

The Sales.io transition script: "Based on everything you told me, I do think this could be a really strong fit. The main thing I'm hearing is [problem], and that's creating [consequence]. Let me walk you through how we'd solve that."

The ESI version ("you qualify") is more powerful for this market because it flips the frame — they're being accepted, not sold to. Both achieve the same goal: the pitch should feel inevitable, not random. If you have to force this transition, discovery isn't done. Go back.

After the recap, bridge the identity gap: "The only thing that's been missing is the system. Everything else — the work ethic, the reason you're on this call — that's already there. That's why you qualify."

Phase 06

The Pitch — 4 Pillars

6
23–33 min
Sales.io: Step 8
Mindset · Legal · Credit · Business

Four pillars, each tied to what THEY said in Phase 4. Not a course — the school nobody built for the immigrant. Sell the transformation: trading hours for a paycheck → operating the U.S. system like an owner.

Sales.io — Step 8
"Do Not Feature Dump. Every Part of the Pitch Must Connect to Something They Already Said."

Before presenting each pillar, anchor it explicitly to something from their discovery. This is not optional — it is the entire difference between a pitch that lands and one they forget.

Run the Heaven and Hell structure for each pillar:

StepWhat You're DoingExample
Hell (Current)Anchor their specific pain from Phase 4"You told me you've been doing this 7 years and you're still in the same spot."
Heaven (Future)Paint the specific future they described"The life you want — off the job site, a business that pays while you sleep — that's not a fantasy. That's what Güero built."
GapName what's between them and that life"The only thing between you and Güero is the system he had and you didn't."
Pillar as BridgePresent the pillar as the solution to THEIR specific gap"That's what Pillar 3 — the credit system — does. It's the engine Güero used to go from no credit to $80K available."

Check in throughout: "Does that make sense?" / "Do you see how that connects to what you told me you want?" Don't wait until the end to verify comprehension.

Sales.io Add
TikTok Pitch — Only Hit the 3–4 Things That Matter to Them

You found their top motivators in Phase 4. The pitch is where you use them. You do not need to present all 4 pillars equally. If their top motivators are "get off the job site" and "provide for my family," spend 70% of the pitch on the business and mindset pillars — because those connect to what they care about. Credit and legal are supporting evidence.

The closer who pitches only what matters to this specific person sounds like they read the prospect's mind. That closes more than covering all bases.

Phase 07

Live Social Proof

7
33–38 min
Sales.io: Step 8 (continued)
Show the mirror case

Match the case to their profile. Share screen: Wins channel. Read 2–3 wins from today out loud with names and amounts. Recency beats size — a $360 win from today is more credible than a $100K screenshot from last year.

Sales.io Add
Match the Case to Their Identity, Not Just Their Job
  • Historical identity (defines self by past failure/struggle): Use the before-and-after story — "Güero wasn't always where he is. Here's where he started."
  • Role identity (leads with expertise or job title): Use Johnny — already selling $5K/month alone, joined anyway, Porsche in 2 months. "You already know how to work. This is about working with the right engine."
  • Social identity (motivated by family or community): Use a case that mentions family results. Make the community itself part of the proof.
  • Emotional identity (raw before-and-after): Use the most human story you have. Carmen. The father who cried. The story that feels like a movie.
Phase 08

Future Pace + Price

8
38–42 min
Sales.io: Steps 5, 9, 10
Future Pace → Confirm Fit → Price Once

Put them 6 months into the future. Make them say out loud what changes. Then confirm fit before revealing price. Then say price once, calmly. Shut up.

"The mentorship has a value of $4,000 for 6 complete months. And if you do it in a single payment, it stays at $3,500 — simply for taking action. So tell me, [NAME]: are we doing this, or do you prefer to stay where you are?"
Sales.io — Step 9
Confirm Fit Verbally Before You Say the Price

Sales.io Step 9 is the most skipped step in this entire framework — and the most costly to skip. Before price, ask:

"Based on everything we covered — do you feel like this is the right fit for what you're trying to solve?"

If yes: "Why do you feel that way?" — make them articulate it. This verbal commitment is your ammunition at the close. When they later say "let me think about it," you say: "Ten minutes ago you told me this was the right fit and here's why. What changed?"

If they can't confirm fit — you are not ready to reveal price. Go back to Phase 4.

Sales.io Framework
The Emotional Contract — Sign It Before You Say the Price

Logic signs off on the emotional contract that was already signed. If you get logical objections at the price — "let me think about it," "it's expensive," "I need to check" — the emotional contract was not signed in Phase 4. This is not a close problem. It is a discovery problem.

Before saying the price, run this internal check:

  • Did they verbalize their pain in their own words?
  • Did they say what their life looks like if nothing changes?
  • Did they answer "why today" with genuine feeling?
  • Did they verbally confirm fit (Step 9)?

If any of those are no — go back with a question, not forward to price. You never propose to someone you don't know is going to say yes.

Phase 09

Close + Payment Captured Live

9
42–55 min
Sales.io: Steps 11, 12
Objections → Close → Payment

Route A (SSN): ClarityPay financing — 12 months 0%. Walk them through live. Route B: card, Splitit, Zelle. Route C: payment ladder ($3,500 → $1,700×2 → $1,200×3 → minimum entry). Nobody leaves with a verbal yes only. Some payment captured on every call.

Sales.io — Steps 11 + 12
The Close Is Not a New Question — It's a Reflection of Their Own Words

Sales.io Step 12: "Based on what you told me, this is the exact problem you said you wanted to solve. You also said staying where you are isn't really an option. So if the only thing left is getting started — let's take care of that now."

They are not committing to a program. They are committing to the transformation they described in their own words in Phase 4. The close is not you asking them to buy. It is you asking if they meant what they said.

Sales.io — Step 11
Read the Energy — Then Name It

The Sales.io energy-check is one of the most powerful tools in this entire document. The moment you feel the prospect pull back — even slightly — name it before it becomes an objection:

"[NAME], I feel like the energy shifted a little bit there. Am I wrong, or is there something I'm not seeing?"

This does two things: it shows you're paying attention (builds trust), and it surfaces the real hesitation before it calcifies into a wall. The energy check is cheaper than any objection reframe.

On cost: "I hear you. Is it truly that the money is impossible — or is it more that you're not fully confident this will work for you yet?" Almost always the second. When they admit it, you're back in discovery, not fighting price.

Sales.io Add
Three Behavioral Yeses Before the Payment Method

"Are you going to be on the live calls? Are you going to apply what we give you? Are you going to ask in the groups when you're stuck?" — three yeses. Do this before you present the payment options, not after the price yes. When they commit to the behavior first, the payment is the administrative step of confirming a commitment they already made. They're paying for their own word — not for your product.

Phase 10

Post-Close + Mental Vaccine

10
55–60 min
Sales.io: Beyond the 12 Steps
Group · Onboarding · Vaccine · Celebrate

Congratulate with meaning. Build WhatsApp group live on the call. Tag Valentina/Omar. First 3 messages. Assign first tasks. Schedule onboarding. Do not hang up until they confirm the group is on their phone.

"One last thing. When you hang up, your head is going to start talking: 'Did I do the right thing? What if it doesn't work?' That is NORMAL. It's your old mind trying to keep you comfortable — the same mind that kept you [THEIR SITUATION] for [X] years. When that voice shows up: open the WhatsApp group, read the wins, and remember WHY you did this today."
Sales.io Add
Frame the Close as an Identity Decision, Not a Purchase

Add this layer to the mental vaccine. Buyer's remorse is not about money — it is about the gap between who they've been and who they just decided to become. The vaccine needs to address the identity shift, not just the fear of losing money.

"You didn't make a purchase today. You made a choice about who you are. The person who crossed a border and worked this hard deserves more than just surviving. When the old voice shows up — remember that's the version of you that got you to today. And you got on this call because you wanted more than that."

This framing dramatically reduces refund requests and ghosting in the first 48 hours because it ties the commitment to identity, not to a product they could return.

Playbook

Objection Playbook

The Meta-Rule — Sales.io

If you are getting logical objections at the close, the emotional contract was not signed in Phase 4. Don't fight the objection. Go back with a question: "Before I answer that — when you think about where you are right now versus where you said you wanted to be, how does that sit with you?" This is more powerful than any reframe. Objections evaporate when the emotional contract is signed.

"I don't have the money"
Real: fear or unclear priority — the money usually exists
Sales.io: "I hear you. Is it truly that the money is impossible — or is it more that you're not fully confident this will work for you yet?" Almost always the second. When they admit it, you're back in discovery.
"How much DO you have available today?" → enter with that + schedule the rest. If SSN: "You don't even need cash — let's check what ClarityPay approves you for. You pay nothing to check."
Money aside: "If this were free, would you do it? Why?" Let them sell themselves. Then: "And if you had the $3,500 in the bank, would you do it? Then the only thing between you and this is the money. Should money be the limit on what you want for your life?"
"Let me think about it"
Real: unspoken doubt / emotional contract not signed
Sales.io Step 11: "Of course. What specifically do you feel like you need to think through?" Surface the real objection — handle THAT, not the stall.
Then: "At the start you told me you'd give me a yes or a no. With everything you've seen — what does your gut say?"
If they still defer: "This isn't about Wednesday. It's about the way you've been making decisions — and that way of deciding is what brought you exactly here. What do you think will happen if you keep deciding the same way?"
"I need to talk to my wife / husband"
Real: seeking permission / social identity / fear of owning the decision
"I respect that. Let me ask: if your car broke down tomorrow and you needed $2,000 to get to work — do you ask permission, or do you solve it? Because it's a necessity. Is building a different future for your family a necessity or optional?"
Sales.io add: "The best way to have that conversation is not with an idea — it's with a decision already made. Imagine telling her: 'I already started, and here's the plan to give you more.'"
"It's expensive" / "Is it worth $4,000?"
Real: haven't weighed cost vs. cost of inaction
Sales.io: "I hear you. Is it truly that the money is impossible, or is it more that you're not fully confident this will work?" Then the three doors:
Door 1 (worst case in history — still have the LLC, $25K credit, and the knowledge). Door 2 (most likely — $5K–$10K/month business). Door 3 (don't invest — December same as today). "Of the three doors — which one is actually expensive?"
"Bryan paid $25,000 to two mentors last month — at his level. Why do you think the people who earn the most are the ones who pay the most to learn?"
"What if it's a scam?" / "I was scammed before"
Real: legitimate fear from experience — never take offense
"I'm glad you said it. If I were you, I'd have the same distrust. What would you like me to show you to break that barrier?"
Then show: Bryan's Instagram from 6 years ago (dates matter). Live WhatsApp group. Private 1-on-1 chat. Offer their phone number to a student with their same profile. "Would a scam give you all that?"
Sales.io energy check: "I feel like there's something still sitting there. Am I wrong?" Name it. Don't talk past the doubt.
Framework

The Three Identity Types

Every prospect operates from one dominant identity. Knowing which one shapes every line of discovery, pitch, and objection handling. Spot it in the first 5 minutes — it's almost always in the first story they tell you.

Historical Identity — "Back in my day…" / "I used to…" / "I tried this before and…"

They define themselves by past experiences — a past success, a past failure, a past version of themselves. The sale is about releasing them from a narrative they've been carrying.

Discovery angle: "That situation taught you that doing it alone doesn't work. The question is whether you're going to let that be the end of the story — or the beginning of a different one."

Most common type. Everyone has some version of it. Listen for it in every call.

Role Identity — "I've been doing this 15 years" / "I already know about credit" / "I've been selling on my own"

They lead with expertise or credentials. They protect their identity against being sold to by someone who might know less. Validate the expertise first — never challenge it directly. Then create the gap between having the skill and using it for themselves.

"You know more than most people I talk to. The question I want you to answer for yourself is: what is all that knowledge building for you right now?"

Social Identity — "My family…" / "People I know said…" / "I want to be part of something"

They mention family and community constantly. They make decisions collectively. They need to belong. Sell them into the community, not just the program. Show the private group live. Make the WhatsApp group feel like a family joining a family.

"Imagine the call you make to your family when you close your first sale. What does that call feel like?"

Detachment + Frame

What Detachment Actually Means

Detachment is not not caring. It is caring about the prospect's outcome more than your own commission. When you genuinely want what's best for them — and you're prepared to tell them this isn't for them if it isn't — you become the only salesperson they've ever spoken to who wasn't trying to sell them something. That is the most powerful position on the call.

I'd Rather Lose the Sale Than Lose the Frame

The moment you drop the price without being asked, accept "let me think about it" without a question, or apologize for the cost — you've lost the frame. And once the frame is gone, you can only beg. Never beg. Never discount unprompted. Never panic when an objection lands. The closer who stays in the frame even when pushed is the one they'll respect enough to buy from.

Never
  • Pitch before the pain is verbalized
  • Say the price before confirming fit (Step 9)
  • Lower the price unprompted
  • Accept "let me think about it" without finding the real objection
  • Promise specific income or timelines
  • Show panic when a card declines
  • Let them hang up without a captured payment
  • Talk more than 40% of the call
  • Mock fear, religion, or past failures
Always
  • Run Steps 1–9 before price — in order
  • Get yes/no commitment in minute 2
  • Know available money before minute 10
  • Ask what they need to feel confident (Step 4)
  • Confirm fit verbally before price (Step 9)
  • Read the energy and name it when it shifts
  • Use their exact words in recap and close
  • Apply the mental vaccine with identity language
  • Capture some payment on the call — always
