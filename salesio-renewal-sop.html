Renewal Process

Step-by-step on how to get a student to renew. This starts at Week 10, and it is not a pitch — it is a conversation with someone who already knows what the program does.

1
Week 10
Identify & Prep
Pull the renewal list from the Client Hub every Monday

At week 10, the student enters the renewal window. Before you send anything or say anything, do a 60-second review of their file so you go in knowing what to say.

Before you reach out — know this
What week are they on? (Hub shows this)
What are their wins? Close rate improvement, income increase, calls landed
What are their struggles? Where are they still stuck?
Have they landed a role or are they still looking?
What is their payment status? Any balance due?
What tier makes sense for them? Core, Growth, or Elite?
💡
The renewal conversation is won before you send the doc. If you know what they care about and what's still unresolved, you already know how to position it.
2
Week 10 — same day as identification
Send the Renewal Offer Doc
WhatsApp DM only. No Slack. No email.

Send the renewal offer link with a short, personal message. The doc does the heavy lifting — your job is just to make it feel like a natural next step, not a sales pitch.

What to send
Hey [name], we're coming up on week 10 — wanted to loop you in on what Year Two looks like for people who stay in. Pulled this together so you can look at the options. No pressure, just want you to have it before the end of the program. [link to salesio-renewal-offer.html]
✓
Short. Personal. No hype language. They already know what the program does — this is not a cold pitch. It is a door you're opening.
✕
Don't say "expires soon" or "limited spots" unless it's true. This student has been with you — they'll know if it's fake urgency and it will kill trust.
After sending

Go to the Client Hub and mark their renewal doc as sent (the ✉ DOC? tag in the table). This keeps the pop-up tracker accurate.

3
Week 11 — 2 to 3 days after sending the doc
Follow-Up Call
If no response, follow up via WhatsApp. If they responded, book a call.

The follow-up is a check-in, not a chase. Most people who are renewing already know they want to. They just need the conversation to make it real.

If no reply yet — follow-up message
Hey [name] — wanted to check in on that Year Two doc. Take a look when you get a chance and let me know if you have questions. Happy to hop on a call and walk through which option makes sense for where you're at.
On the call — what to cover
Acknowledge where they are — "You've come a long way since week 1. What's the one thing that changed the most for you?"
Name what's unresolved — "What's still the thing you want to crack before the end of the program?"
Ask about the future — "Where do you want to be income-wise in the next 12 months?"
Bridge to renewal — "This is exactly what Year Two is for. You're not starting over — you're compounding what you already built."
Walk through the right tier — based on their goals and finances, which one fits?
Ask for the decision — "Does this make sense for where you're going?" Let them close themselves.
💡
The best renewals happen when the student talks themselves into it. Ask more than you say. The person who needs the sale will always lose — approach this with no attachment to the outcome.
4
On the call or over WhatsApp
Handle Objections
These are the five that come up every time

These aren't objections — they're questions. Treat them that way and the conversation stays collaborative instead of defensive.

"I need to think about it."
"Of course. What's the part you're thinking through — is it the investment, the timing, or are you unsure if it makes sense for where you're at right now?" Get specific. Vague objections live in silence.
"I can't afford it right now."
"Tell me more — is that a cash flow thing or is the investment not feeling worth it based on what you've seen so far?" If it's cash flow, walk them through the $500/month option. If it's value, that's a different conversation — go back to what's unresolved for them.
"I think I can do it on my own from here."
"That's great — it means you've built real confidence. The question I'd ask is: what's the cost of going slower on your own versus compounding at the pace you've been on?" Don't push. Plant the seed and let them sit with it.
"What's different about Year Two vs. what I already went through?"
"Year One built the foundation — how you think, how you communicate, how you show up on calls. Year Two is the application. We go deeper on identity under pressure, frame control at higher price points, and the consistency piece that takes people from $10K months to $30K months. Plus you're in the room with people who've already done it."
"Can I just do month-to-month or pay as I go?"
"We don't do month-to-month — the reason is that the people who get the best results are the ones who commit to the full year. A 30-day mentality produces 30-day results. The three tiers have payment options, but they're all 12-month commitments." Hold the frame. Don't negotiate the structure.
5
Week 11 or 12
Close It
One clear ask. No chasing after this.

When the conversation has covered their goals, their unresolved pieces, and the right tier — ask once, directly. Then stop talking.

Based on everything we just talked about, it sounds like [Growth / Elite] makes the most sense for you. Do you want to lock in the alumni rate and keep the momentum going?
✓
After you ask — be quiet. The next person to speak loses. Let them answer. Do not fill the silence.
If they say no or not now

"Totally fine. You know where to find us. If it ever makes sense in the future, the door's open."

That's it. No guilt, no follow-up pressure. Update their status in the Hub to "Completed." If they change their mind later, they'll come back. The ones you push out of the frame don't come back.

If they say yes

Get the payment processed before the call ends. Don't end the call on "I'll send the link." Open the checkout, walk them through it, and confirm payment received. Verbal yes + closed payment = done deal. Verbal yes alone closes at ~40%.

6
Immediately after payment
After They Say Yes
Confirm, update the hub, welcome them back properly

The handoff matters. How you treat the 10 minutes after they pay sets the tone for the next 12 months.

Confirm payment received and send receipt
Update Client Hub: status → Renewed, new start date, payment type and amount
Send a personal WhatsApp message (not a template — actually personal)
Book their first Year Two 1-on-1 before the call ends
Add them to any Elite/Year Two channels if tier changed
Log the renewal in the decision log if anything notable happened
[Name] — pumped to have you in for Year Two. We're going to build on everything you've already done and push to a new ceiling. Your next call is booked — see you [date].
💡
The welcome message should feel like the start of something, not the end of a sales process. One sentence that connects to where they're going — not a recap of what they bought.
