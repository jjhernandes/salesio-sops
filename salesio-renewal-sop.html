Renewal Process

How to get a student to renew, start to finish. Starts at Week 10. It's not a pitch — it's a conversation with someone who already knows what the program does.

1
Week 10
Identify & Prep
Pull the renewal list from the Client Hub every Monday

At week 10, the student enters the renewal window. Do a 60-second file review before you reach out — you should already know what to say.

What week are they on, and what's their payment status?
What are their wins — close rate, income, calls landed?
What's still unresolved? Have they landed a role?
Which tier fits — Core, Growth, or Elite?
💡
The renewal is won before you send the doc. Know what they care about and you already know how to position it.
2
Week 10 — 11
Send & Follow Up
WhatsApp only. No Slack. No email.

Send the offer doc with a short personal note. If no reply in 2-3 days, follow up — this is a check-in, not a chase.

Hey [name], we're coming up on week 10 — wanted to loop you in on what Year Two looks like for people who stay in. Pulled this together so you can look at the options. No pressure, just want you to have it before the end of the program. [link to salesio-renewal-offer.html]
Hey [name] — wanted to check in on that Year Two doc. Take a look when you get a chance and let me know if you have questions. Happy to hop on a call and walk through which option makes sense for where you're at.
On the call — what to cover
Acknowledge where they are — what changed the most for them?
Name what's still unresolved — what do they want to crack before the program ends?
Bridge to renewal — they're compounding, not starting over
Walk the right tier, ask for the decision — then let them close themselves
💡
The best renewals happen when the student talks themselves into it. The person who needs the sale will always lose — no attachment to the outcome.

After sending: mark their renewal doc as sent in the Client Hub (the ✉ DOC? tag). Keeps the pop-up tracker accurate.

3
Week 11 — 12
Objections & Close
The three that come up almost every time

These aren't objections, they're questions. Treat them that way and the conversation stays collaborative.

"I need to think about it."
"Of course. What's the part you're thinking through — the investment, the timing, or whether it's right for where you're at?" Get specific. Vague objections live in silence.
"I can't afford it right now."
"Is that a cash flow thing, or is the investment not feeling worth it based on what you've seen?" Cash flow → walk them through the $500/month option. Value → go back to what's unresolved for them.
"Can I just do month-to-month?"
"We don't — the people who get the best results commit to the full year. A 30-day mentality produces 30-day results." Hold the frame. Don't negotiate the structure.
When it's time to close
Based on everything we just talked about, it sounds like [Growth / Elite] makes the most sense for you. Do you want to lock in the alumni rate and keep the momentum going?
✓
After you ask — be quiet. The next person to speak loses. Do not fill the silence.
If no, or not now

"Totally fine. You know where to find us." No guilt, no follow-up pressure. Update status to "Completed" in the Hub.

If yes

Get payment processed before the call ends. Verbal yes + closed payment = done deal. Verbal yes alone closes at ~40%.

4
Immediately after payment
After They Say Yes
How you treat the next 10 minutes sets the tone for the next 12 months
Confirm payment, send receipt, update Hub status → Renewed
Send a personal welcome message — not a template
Book their first Year Two 1-on-1 before the call ends
Add to Elite/Year Two channels if tier changed
[Name] — pumped to have you in for Year Two. We're going to build on everything you've already done and push to a new ceiling. Your next call is booked — see you [date].
💡
The welcome should feel like the start of something, not the end of a sales process — one line about where they're going, not a recap of what they bought.
