Behavioral Magnets

What you radiate is not what you intend to radiate. The gap between those two things is exactly where most reps lose deals.

What a Behavioral Magnet Is

The Concept

A behavioral magnet is the signal you broadcast — the cumulative output of your internal state, your body language, your vocal tone, your pacing, and your energy. Prospects don't consciously read these signals. They feel them. And they respond to them the same way a compass responds to a magnetic field: automatically, without analyzing why.

You don't choose what you radiate. Your internal state chooses it for you. This is why two reps can say the exact same words and get completely different results. The words aren't what's doing the work.

The gap that kills close rates: Most reps think they're radiating confidence and warmth. What they're actually radiating is performance and need. The difference is invisible to them and completely obvious to the prospect.

The Magnets — What You Radiate and What It Attracts

These aren't personality types. They're states. You can move between them — and most reps cycle through several of them in a single call.

Radiates
Need — "I want this sale"
Prospects feel this as pressure. It triggers their defense mechanisms. They become harder to reach, more guarded, more skeptical of everything you say. The needier you are, the more they question whether you actually believe in what you're selling — or whether you're just selling.

Attracts: resistance, deflection, stall tactics, "I need to think about it"
Radiates
Performance — "Watch me close"
This is the rep who's trying too hard. Over-enthusiastic opener, too many rapport-building questions, a pitch that sounds rehearsed because it is. Prospects can feel when someone is running a script. It feels transactional. You stop being a person and start being a function.

Attracts: surface-level answers, guardedness, "I've heard this before" energy
Radiates
Competence — "I know what I'm doing"
Better than need and performance, but still not the highest level. Competence radiates authority and knowledge — but it can come across as clinical. Prospects trust you intellectually but don't feel emotionally connected. They can respect your process and still not move.

Attracts: logical engagement but not emotional commitment — lots of "that makes sense" without closing
Radiates
Curiosity — "I actually want to understand you"
This is where deals start to open up. Real curiosity — not performed curiosity, not pacing and leading, actual interest in what the person in front of you is experiencing — creates safety. When prospects feel genuinely understood, they open up. And when they open up, they give you everything you need to help them make the decision.

Attracts: real answers, emotional disclosure, trust, forward momentum
Radiates
Certainty — "I don't need you to say yes"
The highest-converting magnet. Certainty isn't arrogance — it's a grounded belief in the value of what you're offering, combined with a complete absence of need for the prospect to agree. This reads as authority. Prospects instinctively trust people who don't need anything from them. It signals that you're operating from truth, not from agenda.

Attracts: respect, reduced resistance, prospects who sell themselves

The Radiate Gap

You cannot close the gap through technique. You can't practice a "certainty voice" in front of a mirror and expect it to hold under real pressure. The magnet you radiate is downstream from who you actually are in that moment. Technique changes what you say. Identity changes what you radiate.

Situation What Most Reps Radiate What Elite Reps Radiate
Opening the call Forced enthusiasm ("So excited to connect!") — performance Calm, unhurried warmth — genuine interest in who this person is
Stating the price Slight vocal hesitation, immediate pivot to payment plans — need States the number once, holds silence — certainty
Hearing an objection Defensive body language, faster speech — anxiety Brief pause, genuine curiosity about what's underneath it
End of call, no decision Over-accommodating, scheduling five follow-ups — desperation Clear, calm — "Here's what I'd recommend. You decide."

The Self-Audit

Pull up your last 3 call recordings. Don't listen for what you said. Listen for what you radiated. Ask yourself:

  • ? When the prospect went quiet, did your pace change? (Speeding up = need radiating)
  • ? How did you state the price? Did your voice lift or soften at the end? (Vocal uncertainty = doubt radiating)
  • ? When they pushed back, did you immediately counter or did you get curious? (Countering fast = performance)
  • ? Were you asking questions you actually wanted the answer to, or questions designed to move them forward? (Designed questions = agenda radiating)
  • ? At the end of the call, did you feel relieved when they said yes, or was the outcome genuinely secondary to whether it was the right fit? (Relief = you needed it)

How to Shift Your Magnet

The Only Real Lever

You can't will yourself into radiating certainty. But you can build the internal conditions that produce it. That's what this entire section is about. The behavioral magnet is the output. Identity is the input. Chapters 1-5 built the foundation. Chapter 6 is where you see it in action.

In the Short Term — On the Call

When you catch yourself radiating need or performance: slow down. Not as a technique — as a reset. A genuine pause. Internally ask: what is actually true about this person right now? What do I actually want to know about them? That question shifts your orientation from selling to understanding. And understanding is what certainty radiates from.

One reframe that changes everything: Walk into every call with this as your only goal — understand this person's situation clearly enough to tell them honestly whether this is right for them. Not to close them. To understand them well enough to give them an honest answer. When that's genuinely your goal, what you radiate changes completely.

The Same Call — Two Different Magnets

Rep radiating NEED — the prospect feels it
Rep
"Hey Marcus, so excited to connect today — I feel like this is going to be a great call. So real quick, tell me a little bit about where you're at with your sales career right now."
"Super excited" + "real quick" + leading with an agenda — broadcasting performance and hurry. Prospect hears: this person needs something from me.
Prospect
"Yeah, so I've been closing for about a year, doing okay I guess, making like 6k a month..."
Vague, surface level. Not opening up. Mirroring the guarded energy coming at them.
Same rep radiating CURIOSITY + CERTAINTY
Rep
"Marcus — good to meet you. What's going on for you right now that made you want to hop on this call?"
No performance. No agenda-pushing. Actually wants to know the answer. Calm. Unhurried. Magnetic.
Prospect
"Honestly I've been in sales for a year and I feel like I know the technical stuff but there's something missing. I close okay but I'm not where I thought I'd be. I can't figure out if it's the offer or if it's me."
Specific, honest, emotionally real. This is the conversation that leads to a close. It only happened because of what the rep radiated before saying a word about the program.
