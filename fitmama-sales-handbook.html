Setter Role

Setting SOP

The Setter's Job

Qualify leads from paid ads via DM. Get curious, build a real connection, identify if they're a genuine fit for FitMama, and get them to book a strategy call. You are not pitching the program — you are qualifying whether they deserve the conversation.

1

Initial DM Engagement

Respond to every lead within 5 minutes if possible. Start with curiosity, not a pitch. Ask a genuine question about where they're at with their health. Your tone should be warm and sisterly — not salesy.

Opener

"Hey [name]! Saw you came through — what's been your biggest struggle with staying consistent lately?"

2

Qualify the Fit

Before sending anyone to an application, confirm they meet the basic profile. This protects the closer's time and keeps quality high.

  • Are they a Filipina mom (or at least Filipino background)?
  • Are they serious about making a change — or just browsing?
  • Do they have a goal or a health concern they want to address?
  • Are they in a position to invest? ($5K is not a trivial spend — make sure they can act.)
3

Send Application Link

Once qualified, send them to the application: fitmamacoaching.com/apply-for-vip-coaching

Bridge to Application

"Before I get you on a call with Khalil, I want to make sure we can actually help you. We have a quick intake form that takes 3 minutes — just want to make sure we can customize the right plan for you. Can I send it over?"

4

Route Through LeadFi

Once the application is submitted, it goes through the LeadFi system. Confirm receipt and log the lead in GoHighLevel immediately. Move them to "Application Received" stage.

5

Book the Call

Get them booked within 24 hours of application submission. Use GHL scheduling or your team's booking link. Confirm via DM and send them the pre-call homework: VSL + Confirmation Page.

Booking Confirm

"You're all set for [day] at [time]! Before your call, watch this short video — it'll help Khalil customize the strategy session just for you. [VSL link]. See you then!"

6

Pre-Call Reminder

Send a reminder 24 hours before and again 1 hour before the call. Confirm they watched the VSL. If they haven't, resend it and don't let them skip it.

Book Rate Benchmark: 3%

Current benchmark is 3% of leads booked. This is the floor, not the ceiling. As you learn the DM flow and how to qualify faster, this number should climb. Track your own book rate weekly and bring it to your check-ins.

Closer Role

Call Framework

Study Khalil's Calls First

Before you run your first live call, you need to watch Khalil close. He has call recordings available — use them. His script is also available for reference. But don't copy the words. Understand why he says what he says. Study his pace, his curiosity, how he handles objections, when he pitches.

Access the Script

Khalil's full call script: View Script Document →

1

Open — Set the Frame

This is a strategy session, not a sales call. Lead with that framing. Your goal in the first 2 minutes is to make her feel heard, not sold to. Confirm she watched the VSL. Get her relaxed.

Frame Setter

"Before we dive in — did you get a chance to watch the video? Good. So today's call is really just about figuring out where you're at, where you want to be, and whether what we do even makes sense for you. There's no pressure — I'd rather be honest with you about fit than have you join something that isn't right."

2

Discovery — Find the Real Problem

Most women who get on this call have a surface-level problem (weight, energy) but a deeper problem underneath (identity, visibility, feeling worthy of time for themselves). Your job is to find that real problem. Ask about her life, not just her health goals.

  • What's your life like right now — walk me through a typical week?
  • When did you first notice this becoming a real issue for you?
  • What have you tried before? What happened?
  • What would it mean for your family if you finally got this handled?
  • If nothing changes, what does the next year look like for you?
3

Identify the Gap

Get her to articulate where she is now vs. where she wants to be. The gap is the product. She should feel the distance between those two realities before you ever mention price or program details.

Gap Question

"So on a scale of 1–10, where are you right now with your health and how you feel in your body? And where do you want to be? What does a 10 actually look like for you?"

4

Present the Program — Match to Her Pain

Don't run the full pitch. Connect specific program elements to what she told you. "You mentioned you struggle with rice at family events — here's exactly how we handle that." Personalized beats comprehensive every time.

Personalized Pitch Bridge

"Based on everything you just shared — especially [specific thing she said] — here's what I think would actually work for you..."

5

Handle Objections — Don't Fold

The most common objections:

  • Price: "I get it — $5K is real money. But what have you spent trying to figure this out on your own? And what's staying stuck actually costing you?"
  • Need to think about it: "What specifically do you need to think through? Let's think through it together right now."
  • Need to ask husband: "Is he supportive of you investing in your health? What does he typically say when you bring things like this up?"
  • Timing: "What's making now the wrong time? Because you've been saying that for [X] — when is the right time going to arrive?"
6

Pitch the Right Offer

Default to $5K flagship. Upsell to annual ($8–9K) if: 30+ lbs to lose, health complications, or strong commitment signals. Ask about budget capacity naturally — don't assume they can't afford more.

Upsell Pivot

"Given everything you shared — especially [health issue or goal complexity] — I actually want to talk to you about our annual program first. The reason is [specific reason matching her situation]. Here's the difference..."

7

Close — Ask for the Decision

Don't wait for her to say yes. After presenting the offer and handling objections, ask directly. A non-close is a disservice — if she's a fit, help her make the decision she already wants to make.

Close

"So based on everything we talked about — are you ready to get started today?"

One-Call Close — That's the Standard

This is a one-call close model. Don't leave calls with "I'll send you some info" or "think it over and get back to me." Make the decision happen on the call or know exactly what's in the way before you hang up.

    Culture & Identity

    You Represent This Brand on Every Call

    Every woman who gets on a FitMama strategy session is trusting this brand with something deeply personal. Her body. Her health. Her time. Her kids' household. You don't just sell a program — you represent whether this community is worth joining. Carry that into every conversation.

    01

    Curiosity over Scripts

    Khalil does not close at 40% because of his script. He closes because he actually wants to understand every woman before he pitches anything. Be genuinely curious. The woman on the call can tell when you're asking a question because you care vs. because it's next in your script.

    02

    Cultural Sensitivity is Mandatory

    FitMama's entire differentiator is cultural fit. You don't have to be Filipina, but you have to understand and respect the cultural context. Learn the foods (rice, adobo, sinigang, lechon). Understand the mindset around family, food guilt, generational patterns. Never dismiss or minimize the cultural factors that shape her relationship with health.

    03

    High Standards — Not High Pressure

    Khalil's standard is one-call close and 40% close rate. But he gets there through connection and conviction, not pressure. Never guilt a prospect into buying. Never manufacture urgency that doesn't exist. If she says no, find out why — but don't bully her into yes.

    04

    Ownership Over Everything

    Your numbers are your responsibility. If your close rate is low, own it and bring recordings. If a lead went cold, review your follow-up. No blame on leads, the offer, or the market. The reps who grow fastest here are the ones who take full ownership, fix fast, and stay coachable.

    05

    The Goal is Her Success, Not Your Commission

    If you sell someone who is not a fit, it damages the community. Khalil has built a 97% Filipino, tight-knit tribe where people get results and stay. A bad sale doesn't just lose a client — it breaks trust in a community where everyone knows everyone. Sell the right people the right program.
