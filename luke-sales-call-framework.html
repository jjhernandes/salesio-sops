Sales Call Framework — Luke MacNaughton

The 5-Step Call

Built from our May 8 session — keep this simple, keep it clean

The one rule above all others: Clarity creates decisions. Confusion creates indecision. If you're confused at any point, they're confused. Stop. Ask. Clarify. Then move forward.

The Framework

01
Connection
Intro + Frame

The sale can be lost in the first 2-5 minutes. This is not small talk. Your job here is to create openness — make them feel safe enough to tell you the truth. Set the frame so they know what this call is and what it isn't.

  • What made you reach out now, after following for a while?
  • What would make this call a win for you today?

Read the room in the first 5 minutes. Are they pain-driven or excitement-driven? Adjust your energy accordingly. Don't try to create pain in someone who's already excited, and don't try to pump up someone who wants to be taken seriously.

↓
02
Problem Awareness
Current State

Find out where they actually are — not where they think they are. Your goal is to make them more aware of the real problem, not just gather information. When they say a vague word, stop and define it. That's where the real pain lives.

  • What's going on with [the thing they mentioned]?
  • How long has that been the case?
  • What have you already tried?

The probe rule: if you hear a charged word — "stuck," "frustrated," "bind," "struggling" — do not move on. Ask: "When you say [word], what do you mean by that?" If you can't define it in your mind, they can't feel it. And if they can't feel it, they won't buy.

↓
03
Cognitive Dissonance
Future Pace + Consequence

This is where you create the gap — the separation between where they are and where they want to be. Current state is the hell. Future state is the heaven. The gap is the reason they need to do something now.

The consequence is what happens if they stay where they are. You're not scaring them — you're making them feel the cost of inaction honestly. If the gap is real, they'll feel it.

  • Where do you want to be in 6 months / 12 months?
  • If nothing changes — what does that look like for you?
  • Why does this matter to you now?
↓
04
The Pitch
Connect the One Thing

Don't pitch everything. Find the one thing that carries the most emotion for this specific person — the thing that most directly bridges their current state to their future state — and connect it to them.

People buy on emotion and compensate logically. Your pitch is not a features list. It's a mirror of their pain and desire, with your solution as the bridge.

Before you pitch: do you know the one thing this person needs to feel? If not, go back and probe more. Pitching before you've found that one thing is why pitches miss.

↓
05
Close
Ask + Handle

If you've done the first four steps well, the close is a natural next step — not a pivot. Ask simply. If they hesitate, go back to what's unclear. Every objection is a confusion. Resolve the confusion and you resolve the objection.

  • Does this make sense for where you're at?
  • What would need to be true for this to be a yes for you?
  • What's holding you back right now?

Advanced Techniques — Use When Natural, Not On a Script

Sticky Notes — Pull These In If They Fit

  • Impact expansion: after finding pain, expand it to other areas of their life — how is this affecting your relationships, confidence, finances, etc.? Use when they seem emotionally open and you want to deepen the gap.
  • Change point: "Was there one moment that made you finally want to do something about this, or was it building over time?" — use to find the emotional trigger event and amplify it.
  • Moral elevation: connect their goal to something bigger than themselves — family, legacy, who they're becoming. Use after goals, when the emotional door is open.
  • Identity question: "If you actually hit that goal — how does that change how you see yourself?" — future paces identity, not just circumstances. Use once goals are clear.
  • Laugh interrupt: when someone laughs at their own pain — "why is that funny?" — bring it back. They're deflecting. That's the real pain.

Principles to Run On

  • Clarity creates decisions. Confusion creates indecision. If you're confused, they're confused.
  • If you can't define it in your mind, they can't feel it. Ask what they mean before you move on.
  • The sale can be lost in the first 2-5 minutes. Connection is not small talk — it's the foundation.
  • People make decisions based on emotion and compensate logically. Make them feel something first.
  • Find the one thing. Don't pitch everything — find the one thing that carries the most emotion and connect them to it.
  • Too much framework = friction in your brain = not present. A tool that makes you second-guess yourself is not a tool, it's a liability.
  • Advanced techniques are suggestions, not gospel. Know them. Don't run them on a script.
  • The behaviors you allow in the first 6 minutes will run through the whole call. Set the standard early.
