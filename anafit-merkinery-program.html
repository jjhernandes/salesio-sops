Why We're Starting Here
Two Days, On Purpose
You're at one day a week right now, and the goal is five. Jumping straight there is exactly how last time went — pain and low energy from the Vitamin D and B12 deficiency built up faster than the program did. Two days a week is the pace that actually holds while your body catches up, and we add a third once these two feel easy, not effortful.
Built around your assessment. Squats, hip hinge, core, and overhead mobility all came back strong — that's the foundation we're building on. Push strength and single-leg stability need more time, so they're trained carefully rather than pushed hard.
Training Structure
Your Two Days
Gym access plus some home equipment, ideally after work. Every session opens with a slow warm-up and closes with a few minutes of stretching for your legs and thighs — that tightness gets tended to every single time, not just when it's bothering you.
Day 1
Squat Foundation + Core
~40 min
Bodyweight Squat (to parallel)3 × 10
Glute Bridge3 × 12
Seated or Band Row3 × 10
Bird Dog3 × 8 each side
Plank3 × 20–30 sec
Squats are the priority movement of this entire program — take the time to get the depth and back position right before adding any weight. Bird Dog is here specifically to start rebuilding single-leg stability without loading the knee the way a lunge would.
Day 2
Push Rebuild + Full Body
~40 min
Incline or Wall Push-Up3 × max reps
Goblet Squat (light) or Bodyweight Squat3 × 10
Band Pull-Apart3 × 12
Dead Bug3 × 10 each side
Side Plank2 × 15–20 sec each side
Push-ups start from wherever you actually land that day — 1, 2, 5, it doesn't matter, the number moving up over the coming weeks is the entire point. Incline (hands elevated on a bench or step) makes it achievable without forcing bad form.
Not doing right now: single-leg lunges. Given the shakiness and knee discomfort, they're paused until balance and strength are built underneath the pattern. If any exercise ever feels sharp, wrong, or aggravates your back or hips, stop and message Ana before continuing.
