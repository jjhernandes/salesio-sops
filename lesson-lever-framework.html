The Lever Framework

Not every problem deserves equal attention. One broken stage causes problems in every stage after it. Find the constraint — fix the constraint.

The Core Idea
Your funnel has one place where it breaks. Everything upstream of that point is working fine. Everything downstream is suffering because of it. That's your lever.

In this example, everything before Show is healthy. Dials are up, connections are happening, appointments are booking — but calls aren't happening. That's not a closing problem. That's a confirmation and commitment problem. Fixing your close rate here won't change anything.

The Principles
⛓
One broken lever causes problems downstream
The issue you see at the end of the funnel rarely starts there. Most closers think they have a closing problem. Usually it's a discovery problem. Trace it back.
🎯
Fix the constraint — everything else improves
A system is only as strong as its weakest point. You don't need to improve every stage. Find the one that's killing your output and fix that one. The downstream numbers move automatically.
🔍
Where your funnel loses the most — that's your lever
Look at the drop-off between each stage. Where's the biggest gap? If 80% of appointments show but only 30% of offers close — your lever is the close, not the show. Follow the numbers.
⚠️
Pulling 10 levers at once means pulling none of them hard enough
Trying to fix everything at once is how you improve nothing. When your focus is split across 10 things, you give each one 10% effort. One thing with 100% effort is how skill compounds.
The Rule
One thing. Done right. For 4 weeks.
That's how skill compounds. Not by learning more — by going deeper on the one thing that actually moves your number. Every lesson in Phase 2 connects back to your lever.
