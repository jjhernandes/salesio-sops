03

Core Principles Introduced

Question Quality
  • The quality of your questions dictates the quality of their responses — and the questions you ask reveal more about you than anything you can say directly
  • Always defer to a question over a statement. More questions, fewer statements, throughout the entire process
  • Challenge subjective language every time — "tough," "hard," "frustrated" can mean completely different things to different people
  • Ask questions you genuinely want to know the answer to — that authenticity creates real trust faster than any tactical question
  • Safety questions ("so what's your goal?", "how would that make you feel?") lower your authority and make you sound like every other rep. Eliminate them
Frame & Presence
  • Respect before likability — earn the authority first, the connection follows. Not the other way around
  • Nothing else should matter in the world during the call. Full presence. That's what creates the environment where people share things they've never shared with anyone
  • Trust or distrust is decided in the first 2-3 minutes — the call environment is set before you even get into discovery
Cost of Not Buying
  • "What happens if nothing changes?" only creates real impact if emotional tension is already present — the question lands when they're already feeling it, not before
  • The goal is to surface what's already there — not manufacture emotions. There's a real scratch they need to fix. Pull it out
