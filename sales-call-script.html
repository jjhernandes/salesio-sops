Sales.io — Full Call Framework

Sales Call Script

The full 12-step framework, start to close. Not lines to recite — a map. Discovery does the heavy lifting; the pitch and close just collect what discovery already built.

Core Rule

Never pitch just to pitch. You only present once you have earned the right to present.

A strong close is created in discovery. If discovery is weak, objections become harder, the pitch feels forced, and the prospect will not feel clear enough to decide.

Intro→ Current Reality→ Problem Awareness→ Solution Awareness→ Future Pace→ Consequence→ Transition→ Pitch→ Confirm Fit→ Price Reveal→ Objections→ Close
1
Intro: Respect First, Likability Later
Goal — Establish control, confirm time, and open the conversation without overdoing rapport.
Script

"Hey [Name], good to meet you. I know you booked a call about [reason/context]. Do you still have the full [time] for today?"

💡
Key Principle — Do not try to impress them. Do not try to become their best friend. Lead the call with calm authority.
2
Current Reality
Goal — Understand what they are currently doing and where they are right now.
Questions

"Give me some context. What are you currently doing for [sales/business/career/problem]?"

"How long have you been doing that?"

"What made you go that route?"

"What's working?"

"What's not working?"

💡
Key Principle — Start surface-level, then go deeper. Do not move on too fast.
3
Problem Awareness
Goal — Help them become fully aware of the real problem and its impact.
Questions

"What do you feel is the biggest issue with where things are right now?"

"What have you tried to fix it?"

"Why do you think that has not worked yet?"

"What is this costing you right now?"

"How long has this been going on?"

If They Say "Things Are Fine"

"I hear you, but if everything was exactly where you wanted it, why are we on this call?"

💡
Key Principle — Do not accept surface-level answers like "things are fine" if they booked a call. There is always a reason.
4
Solution Awareness
Goal — Understand whether they know what the right solution actually looks like.
Questions

"How do you know what the right solution would even look like?"

"How are you deciding who or what to trust?"

"What would need to be true for you to feel confident moving forward?"

"What do you feel is missing from your current approach?"

💡
Key Principle — This is where they often realize they have a bigger gap than they thought.
5
Future Pace
Goal — Create the gap between where they are now and where they want to be.
Questions

"If this was fully solved, what would be different?"

"What would that allow you to do?"

"What would life/business look like 3–6 months from now if this changed?"

"What would make this worth it for you?"

💡
Key Principle — Do not get cheesy or overly emotional. Make it feel natural.
6
Consequence / Cost of Inaction
Goal — Help them see what happens if nothing changes.
Questions

"What happens if this stays the same for the next 3–6 months?"

"What does that cost you?"

"What are you worried could happen if this does not get fixed?"

"Is staying where you are actually an option?"

💡
Key Principle — Only ask these if the emotion is already there. If discovery was strong, you do not need to overdo this.
7
Transition to Pitch
Goal — Summarize their situation and earn permission to present.
Script

"Based on everything you told me, I do think this could be a really strong fit. The main thing I'm hearing is [problem], and that is creating [pain/consequence]. Let me walk you through exactly how we would help you solve that."

💡
Key Principle — The pitch should feel like the obvious next step, not a random presentation.
8
Pitch
Goal — Connect the offer directly to their problems, goals, and decision criteria.
Structure
1Restate their current problem.
2Explain the mechanism that solves it.
3Show how the program/product works.
4Tie each feature back to their desired outcome.
5Check for understanding throughout.
Check-Ins

"Does that make sense?"

"Do you see how that would help with [specific problem]?"

"Are you with me so far?"

💡
Key Principle — Do not feature dump. Every part of the pitch should connect to something they already said.
9
Confirm Fit
Goal — Make sure they agree before discussing price.
Script

"Based on everything we covered, do you feel like this is the right fit for what you're trying to solve?"

If Yes

"Why do you feel that way?"

💡
Key Principle — Get them to verbalize the decision before price.
10
Price Reveal
Script

"Cool. So the investment for the program is [$X]. Is that reachable for you?"

Alternative

"The program is [$X] for [timeframe]. Is that something you can do?"

💡
Key Principle — Say the price calmly. Do not over-explain. Do not get weird.
11
Objection Handling
Goal — Do not argue. Make them feel safe and understood.
If Energy Shifts

"[Name], I feel like the energy shifted a little bit. Am I wrong, or is there something I'm not seeing?"

If They Say Money

"I hear you. Is it truly that the money is impossible, or is it more that you're not fully confident this will work for you yet?"

If They Say "I Need to Think About It"

"Of course. What specifically do you feel like you need to think through?"

💡
Key Principle — The objection stage should feel like home. Stay calm, comfortable, and curious.
12
Close
Goal — Help them commit to the decision they already said they wanted.
Script

"Based on what you told me, this is the exact problem you said you wanted to solve. You also said staying where you are is not really an option. So if the only thing left is getting started, let's take care of that now."

💡
Key Principle — They are not just committing to the program. They are committing to the new version of themselves or their business.
