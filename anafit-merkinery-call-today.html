Today's Call, Start to Finish

Everything from the assessment and intake is already known — this call is where it becomes a real conversation. Follow it top to bottom, but let it breathe. This is Merkinery, not a checklist.

~30–40 minutes
Text is her preferred contact — confirm call method
Assessment + intake already reviewed
1
Open Warm
~3 min
She's 50, works full days, then home to her son. Keep it light.
Say
"Hey Merkinery! So happy we're finally talking."
"I read through everything — your assessment, your goals, all of it."
"Today's just a real conversation, nothing formal. Sound good?"
2
Reflect Her Goal Back
~5 min
Show her she was heard before anything else.
Say
"One thing stuck with me — you said you wake up in pain a lot."
"And you want your leg strength back."
"Tell me more — what does a bad pain day actually look like?"
💗
Let her talk here. This is the moment she feels actually seen, not processed.
3
Walk Through the Assessment
~7 min
Lead with strengths first.
Say
"Good news first — squat, hip hinge, core, overhead mobility? All solid."
"That's a real foundation after time off."
"Two spots need work: push strength, and single-leg balance felt shaky, some knee discomfort."
"Does that match how it felt to you?"
"When your knee feels off on that lunge — sharp pain, or more like it just doesn't feel stable?"
4
Name the Health Context
~5 min
The most important moment of the call.
Say
"I saw your Vitamin D and B12 were really low last time you tried this."
"Back and legs hurting a lot, at the same time."
"That's not you failing — your body just didn't have what it needed."
"So this time, we're not rushing the pace."
⚕️
Stay in your lane here. Acknowledge the deficiencies and the bulged disc as real context for pacing — don't diagnose or give medical advice. She's already on Vitamin D, B12, and regular vitamins for this.
5
Explain the Lunge Call
~3 min
She asked for this directly — show her it's already happening.
Say
"You told me you want your form to be right — especially lunges."
"So for now, single-leg lunges are out completely."
"We build the stability underneath first."
"Not forever — just not where we start."
6
Present Phase 1
~5 min
Name the honest gap between 1 day and 5 days now.
Say
"I know the goal's five days a week eventually — we'll get there."
"But you're at one day right now."
"Jump too far too fast and you lose momentum — that's kind of what happened last time."
"So Phase 1: two sessions a week. Squats, planks, core."
"We build from a base that actually holds."
7
Talk Nutrition — Light Touch
~5 min
Not a rulebook — she's never tracked, and there's a binge-restrict pattern.
Say
"Nothing about your eating needs an overhaul."
"Water's already great, alcohol's barely a factor."
"The one thing you flagged: late-night snacking."
"You said that's what would make you want to quit."
"So let's actually talk about what's happening in those moments — not rules, just real talk."
⚠️
Do not prescribe restriction here. Given the binge-restrict pattern, framing this as "cut out snacking" risks triggering the exact cycle she's trying to break. Keep it structural and curious, not punitive.
8
Name the Emotional Pattern
~5 min
Builds trust. Not a therapy session.
Say
"You mentioned — when you're overwhelmed, especially something with your son — that's when eating and staying home take over."
"I'm not fixing that today."
"But on those days — message me. Before you skip. Not after."
💗
Her own words on the hard days: "There will be better days. Soon I will get back to the gym." If the conversation gets heavy, this is a good line to reflect back to her — she already believes it.
9
Confirm Logistics
~3 min
Make it a shared plan, not an assumption.
Say
"You said maybe after work."
"Still realistic with your son's schedule? Or is there a better window?"
10
Close & Confirm Commitment
~3 min
Hand her commitment back to her.
Say
"You rated your commitment a 10 out of 10."
"I believe you — and I'm matching it."
"Program's ready. Weekly check-ins in the Hub."
"Text me anytime, especially on the hard days."
"Sound good?"
📋
Send after the call: her Phase 1 program, and confirm her first session day/time before you hang up.
