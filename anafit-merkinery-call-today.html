Today's Call, Start to Finish

Everything from the assessment and intake is already known — this call is where it becomes a real conversation. Follow it top to bottom, but let it breathe. This is Merkinery, not a checklist.

~30–40 minutes
Text is her preferred contact — confirm call method
Assessment + intake already reviewed
1
Open Warm
~3 min
Light and human first. She's 50, works full days as a paralegal, then goes home to her son — she's not looking for a lecture, she's looking for someone in her corner.
Say
"Hi Merkinery! So happy to finally get to talk. I read through everything you sent — your assessment, your goals, all of it. I want today to just be a real conversation, not a formal thing. Sound good?"
2
Reflect Her Goal Back
~5 min
Show her she was heard before anything else happens. Her goal is specific — legs, strength, the pain, and getting back to the gym 5 days a week eventually.
Say
"What stood out most to me is you said you wake up in pain a lot, and you want your leg strength back. That's real, and that's exactly what we're going to build toward. Tell me a little more — what does a bad pain day actually look like for you?"
💗
Let her talk here. This is the moment she feels actually seen, not processed.
3
Walk Through the Assessment
~7 min
Keep it collaborative, not a report card. Lead with what's strong — it builds confidence before naming what needs work.
Say
"Good news first — your squat, hip hinge, core, and overhead mobility are all solid. That's a real foundation, and it's not nothing after time off. Two spots need more attention: your push strength, and single-leg balance felt shaky with some knee discomfort. Does that match how it felt to you?"
"When your knee feels off on that lunge motion, is it sharp, or more like it doesn't feel stable?"
4
Name the Health Context, Gently
~5 min
This is the most important moment of the call. Her last attempt stopped because of real pain while her Vitamin D and B12 were very low — not because she gave up. Naming that out loud removes shame from the room.
Say
"I saw you mentioned your Vitamin D and B12 were really low last time you tried this, and your back and legs were hurting a lot — that's not you failing, that's your body not having what it needed. That's exactly why we're not going to rush the pace this time."
⚕️
Stay in your lane here. Acknowledge the deficiencies and the bulged disc as real context for pacing — don't diagnose or give medical advice. She's already on Vitamin D, B12, and regular vitamins for this.
5
Explain the Lunge Call
~3 min
She specifically asked to be taught correct form — leg position on lunges, back position, rep counts. This is where you show her that request is already being taken seriously.
Say
"You told me you want to make sure your form is right, especially on lunges — so for now, we're going to leave single-leg lunges out completely and build the stability and strength underneath that pattern first. It's not off the table forever, it's just not where we start."
6
Present Phase 1
~5 min
She's currently training once a week. Her 3-month vision is five days. Naming the honest bridge between those two numbers now prevents disappointment later — and protects her from the exact crash that derailed her before.
Say
"I know the goal is five days a week eventually, and we'll absolutely get there. But you're at one day right now, and the fastest way to lose momentum is to jump too far too fast — that's kind of what happened last time. So Phase 1 starts at two sessions a week, built around squats, planks, and core, and we build up from a base that actually holds."
7
Talk Nutrition — Light Touch
~5 min
She's never tracked before, and a binge-restrict pattern is present — so this is not the moment to hand her a rulebook. Name the one real challenge she already identified herself: late snacking.
Say
"Nothing about your eating needs a big overhaul right now. Your water intake is already great, alcohol's barely a factor. The one thing you flagged yourself is the late-night snacking — and you actually said that's the thing that would make you want to quit. So let's talk about that specifically, not as a rule to follow, just what's actually happening in those moments."
⚠️
Do not prescribe restriction here. Given the binge-restrict pattern, framing this as "cut out snacking" risks triggering the exact cycle she's trying to break. Keep it structural and curious, not punitive.
8
Name the Emotional Pattern, Carefully
~5 min
She already told you the trigger — anxiety from a call about her son, then eating and staying home. This isn't a therapy session, but naming it builds trust and shapes how you support her.
Say
"You mentioned that when you get overwhelmed — especially if something happens with your son — that's when eating and staying home take over. I'm not here to fix that in one conversation, but I want you to know: on those days, message me. Before you skip, not after."
💗
Her own words on the hard days: "There will be better days. Soon I will get back to the gym." If the conversation gets heavy, this is a good line to reflect back to her — she already believes it.
9
Confirm Logistics
~3 min
She works 9–5 as a paralegal, then home to her son with no outside childcare. Confirm the realistic training window out loud so it's a shared plan, not an assumption.
Say
"You mentioned maybe after work — does that still feel realistic with your son's schedule, or is there a better window we should build around instead?"
10
Close & Confirm Commitment
~3 min
She rated her commitment 10/10 on intake. Close by handing that back to her as a shared agreement, not a test.
Say
"You told me your commitment is a 10 out of 10 — I believe you, and I'm matching that. Your program's ready to go. Weekly check-ins in the Hub, and text me directly anytime, especially on the hard days. Sound good?"
📋
Send after the call: her Phase 1 program, and confirm her first session day/time before you hang up.
