How It Works

01
Someone Reports
A sales rep, setter, or closer submits a company they had a bad experience with. We collect the name, the violation type, and what happened — as specifically as possible.
02
We Verify
The Sales.io team reviews every submission. We reach out to the reporter, gather screenshots if available, and cross-reference with other reports before publishing anything publicly.
03
It's Published
Verified entries go live on this registry. Each listing shows the violation type, verification level, and a summary of what happened — so you can make an informed decision before joining any offer.
Verification

What Each Badge Means

REPORTED
1+ submission received — under active review
We've received a report about this company but have not yet spoken to the reporter in detail or gathered supporting evidence. Treat as a flag, not a verdict. We are actively investigating.
VERIFIED
Staff spoke directly to a victim — core claim confirmed
A member of the Sales.io team has spoken to the reporting party, reviewed available documentation, and confirmed the core claim. One verified source minimum. Take this seriously before joining this offer.
CONFIRMED
Multiple independent reports + documented evidence
Three or more independent reports verified, and/or documented evidence (screenshots, contracts, payment records) has been reviewed by the Sales.io team. This company has a documented pattern of harm. Do not join without extreme caution.
Protection

Before You Join Any Offer — Run This Checklist

1
Search this registry first. Takes 10 seconds. If the company is here, you have your answer.
2
Get the commission structure in writing before the first call. A company that won't put comp in writing before you work a shift is already showing you who they are.
3
Talk to a current closer — not a testimonial, a real conversation. Ask what their last 3 paychecks looked like and whether commissions were paid on time. A great offer has nothing to hide.
4
Verify the offer is doing real volume. "We do $1M/month" is easy to say. Ask for a recent lead sheet, call booking rate, or setter metrics. If they refuse, it's a ghost offer.
5
Ask who owns the offer and what their track record is. Search the owner's name. Find LinkedIn, YouTube, or real social proof that they run a real business with real students getting results.
6
Never close under a 60-day probation with no pay. Training periods are normal. Unpaid probation for a month or more with no minimum is not a sales role — it is free labor.
7
If something feels off, report it here. You might not be the first, and you definitely won't be the last. Protect the person who's about to take your spot.
