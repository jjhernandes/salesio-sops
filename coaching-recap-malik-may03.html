Fitness Roles — What You Need to Know

You can't just look at follower count and assume someone has a sales team worth working for. This will waste your time.

What People Think

Big following = big offer = great commission opportunity. So you DM them and try to get on their team.

Reality

Follower count tells you nothing. Some people with 2M+ followers make $200K/month. Some "small" accounts do $5M/month. You'd never know.

Andy Elliott looks like the king of sales to someone who doesn't know the space. When you learn more, you realize the style doesn't match how people actually buy. Some people making $390K-$1M+/month on fitness offers have almost no public following. Don't let optics drive your target list.

The job board solves all of this. Revenue is vetted. Offers are confirmed active. You can filter by niche, experience level, and role type. Fitness roles at the 0-3 months experience level exist right now — you could apply today.

The Bridge Play — Car Sales

While you build the skill over the next few weeks, there's a real option to start making more money now and proving the skill in the real world: car sales in Orlando.

Car sales isn't the destination. It's the fastest way to get real reps, make more money than Amazon, and prove to yourself the skills actually work — before you land the fitness role.

JJ called Ian (works at a dealership in Orlando) on the spot. Here's what Ian said:

Intel from Ian — What to Look For
  • Check the pay plan first — some dealerships price their inventory at wholesale and make gross almost impossible
  • Find a dealership that prices above what they own the car for — even after discounting, you make gross
  • Look for back-end pay (finance office %) — even if front commission is thin, finance adds $500+ per deal you'd otherwise miss
  • Toyota: loyal customer base, consistent, easier to sell. Recommend if you're choosing luxury vs. volume
  • Doesn't matter what brand you're selling as much as the pay plan — don't let the logo fool you

Ian is also sending specific questions to bring into dealership interviews — insider language that signals you know the space even if you've never sold a car. Use those questions. They make hiring managers stop and take you seriously.

How to Win the Interview

Hiring managers make decisions in two ways: look and feel. If you look the part and make them feel good about you, you get the role. That's it.

Don't Say This

"I don't have sales experience, but I'm a hard worker and a fast learner."

Nobody hires on work ethic alone. They need to know they can trust you with their leads.

Say This Instead

"Technically, I've been selling my whole life — everything is communication. I've studied sales and understand that we're influencing decisions. That's what I bring."

And when you use Ian's questions — "What's the OTE?" and "How are leads sourced?" — the interviewer's brain shifts. They stop thinking about your resume and start thinking: this person understands the space. That question doesn't come from nowhere. JJ will help you prep the full list.
