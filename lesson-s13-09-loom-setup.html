Loom Setup

Framing, lighting, audio, background. The video is your first impression — before you've said a word, they've already made a judgment. Get the setup right or the content doesn't matter.

Setup Is Credibility Before You Open Your Mouth

A grainy webcam shot with bad lighting signals low effort. That's the opposite of what a high-ticket closer should project. The prospect sees your video before they hear your pitch. If the visual quality reads as "I don't care about details," your application is done before you speak.

This doesn't require expensive gear. It requires attention. A $0 setup done well beats a $500 setup done carelessly.

The principle: Your Loom setup should communicate the same thing your call demeanor communicates — calm, prepared, intentional, professional. Nothing in the frame should work against that message.

The Four Setup Elements

📷
Framing
Floor: Camera at eye level. Not looking up at a laptop camera, not looking down from a monitor. Eye level. Your face should fill 55-65% of the frame — enough to see your expression clearly, not so close it's uncomfortable.
Pro level: Camera 18-24 inches from your face. Slight angle (not dead-on) adds depth. Rule of thirds if you want to be precise — eyes in the upper third of the frame.
💡
Lighting
Floor: Natural light source facing you. Sit facing a window — not with the window behind you (that makes you a silhouette). If no window, a lamp in front of you works. Warm light preferred over harsh overhead fluorescent.
Pro level: A ring light or soft box in front of you, slightly above eye level. $40-80 on Amazon. This alone upgrades the quality of your video significantly. Diffused light is more flattering than direct light.
🎙️
Audio
Floor: Wired earbuds with a microphone. Not laptop mic. The built-in laptop mic picks up keyboard, room echo, and background noise. Even the mic that comes with Apple EarPods is dramatically better than a laptop mic.
Pro level: A USB condenser mic ($60-100) or wireless lavalier clip-on mic. Clean audio signals care and professionalism — it's often the first thing prospects notice (consciously or not).
🧱
Background
Floor: A clean, uncluttered wall or a tidy room. No laundry in the background. No chaos. A plain white or neutral wall is completely fine — clean is professional.
Pro level: A tasteful background with intentional elements — a plant, a shelf with a few books, a Sales.io branded element. Something that says "I exist in a real workspace" without being distracting. Avoid virtual backgrounds — they look artificial on most setups.

Setup Mistakes That Kill Applications

The mistakeWhy it hurts
Looking up at the camera (laptop flat on desk)Makes you look small, the visual is unflattering, signals low effort
Window or light source behind youYour face goes dark, details disappear — you become unreadable
Using the laptop microphoneEchoey, hollow sound — makes you sound like you're in a bathroom
Busy or chaotic backgroundDistracts from your message, raises questions about your environment
Reading from a script off-screenEyes dart away constantly — you look unconfident and unreliable

The Pre-Send Check

Before sending any Loom: watch it back with the sound off, then with your eyes closed. Sound off: Does it look professional and credible? Eyes closed: Does your voice sound confident and clear? If both pass, send it.

A Loom that makes you cringe is not a Loom you should send. Record it again. You get better quickly. The reps who send imperfect Looms because "it's good enough" are leaving response rate on the table.
