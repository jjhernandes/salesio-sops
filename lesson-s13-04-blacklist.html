The Blacklist

Some companies have already been flagged by reps in this community. Before you apply anywhere, check the list.

How the Blacklist Works

Community-Reported, Internally Verified

The blacklist is maintained by Sales.io based on reports submitted by reps inside the community. When a rep has a documented negative experience with a company, they submit it for review. We verify the claim independently before the company gets added.

This is not a place for grudges or subjective complaints. It is a documentation of patterns that make a company unsuitable for our reps to work with. One bad month from a rep who was not a fit does not land a company on the list. A pattern of behavior against reps does.

The blacklist exists because the community's collective experience is more valuable than any single rep's research. By the time a company gets flagged here, multiple reps have already had the experience you are about to avoid.

Common Blacklist Reasons

01
Payment
Doesn't Pay Commission on Time
The most common blacklist reason. Reps close the deals. Money comes in. Commission does not. The company delays, disputes, or finds reasons to withhold earnings. This is not a misunderstanding. It is a pattern. Companies on the list for this reason have had it verified by multiple reps.
02
Misrepresentation
Lies About Close Rate or Pipeline Quality
The company advertises a 20% close rate. Reps start and find out the real close rate is 6% because leads are old, cold, or not qualified. The misrepresentation may be intentional or the result of leadership not tracking their own data accurately. Either way, reps were misled.
03
Chargebacks
Unusually High Chargeback Rate
When customers refund or dispute at a disproportionate rate, it signals the product is not delivering on its promise. Some companies pass chargeback costs to reps in ways that eliminate earnings. High chargeback rate is also a sign of a product problem, which affects every rep on that offer.
04
Culture
Hostile or Disrespectful to Reps
Leadership that treats reps as interchangeable, blames reps for systemic problems, creates a culture of fear, or publicly humiliates reps in team settings. A rep's performance is partly a function of the environment they work in. Hostile environments produce inconsistent results even from strong closers.
05
Terms
Changes Commission Structure After Reps Start
The original agreement was 10%. After a rep's first good month, the structure quietly changes to 8% with a "new performance threshold" added. These unilateral changes to commission terms after reps are already working are a serious violation of trust and business ethics.

How to Check the Blacklist Before Applying

Step 1 — Check the Pinned List

The blacklist is maintained in the Sales.io community (WhatsApp and Skool). It is pinned in the resources section and updated whenever a new company is added after verification. Check it before you apply to any company, whether it appeared on our board or not.

Step 2 — Search the Community Channel

Before applying to a specific company, run a quick search of the community channel for that company's name. Other reps may have mentioned it in passing even if it has not been formally verified for the blacklist yet. Community intelligence is real-time.

Step 3 — Ask Directly

If you are considering a role and are not sure, post in the community channel and ask. Someone in the 100-200 rep community may have worked there, know someone who did, or have relevant information. You are not in this alone. Use the network.

How to Submit a Company for Review

If you have a documented negative experience with a company, your submission protects the next rep. Submit only what you can verify with evidence. Screenshots of payment disputes, written commission terms that were changed, documented communication patterns. Opinion alone is not enough.

  • ✓
    Document the situation first. Screenshots, written records, dates. The more documented your experience, the faster the verification process moves.
  • ✓
    DM a Sales.io coach or admin. Submit your documentation directly to a coach or program admin in the community. Do not post unverified claims publicly — that protects you and the process.
  • ✓
    We cross-reference with other reps. Before anything goes on the list, we independently verify the pattern with at least one other rep or source.
  • ✕
    Do not post company names publicly before they have been verified. False or premature accusations harm your credibility and expose you to legal risk. Run the process.

Submitting a company does not mean they are blacklisted. It initiates a review. Some submissions reveal misunderstandings that get resolved. That outcome is also valuable. The goal is accurate information, not punishment.
