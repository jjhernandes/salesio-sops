Call Reference

Hannah

Use this before and during every call.

Before Every Call

  • Look up their website — know what they sell before you get on
  • Search their main keywords in ChatGPT and Google — have the tabs open and ready
  • Know 1-2 of their competitors by name
  • Have the case studies pulled up (Campaign Monitor, etc.)

Call Flow

Goal of call 1: Qualify them, create curiosity, book call 2. Pricing never comes up on this call.

  1. 1
    Open + elevator pitch
    Set the agenda and give them a quick 2-line overview of what SWAM does. Do this right away so they have context for every question you ask. See scripts below.
  2. 2
    Discovery
    What are you doing now for digital visibility — SEO, paid ads, digital PR? What's working, what isn't? Where does your traffic come from?
  3. 3
    Show them the gap
    Share your screen. Search their keywords in ChatGPT and Google. Show them where their competitors show up and where they don't. Let the visual do the work. Ask: are these your main competitors, or is there anyone else I should be looking at?
  4. 4
    Book call 2
    Tell them what they'll get on the next call — the full analysis, publication list, and a free first placement if they move forward. Lock in a date before you hang up.

What to Say

Elevator Pitch — Simple

"We help brands get featured in publications like Forbes and USA Today — the ones that show up when your customers are searching on Google or asking ChatGPT. We work with companies like Intuit QuickBooks and Visa. This call is just discovery — I want to understand what you're doing now and see if there's a fit."

Elevator Pitch — More Detail

"SWAM lives at the intersection of digital PR, SEO, and GEO. We get clients placed on the biggest publications — Forbes, USA Today — that show up in search results and AI results for the keywords your customers are actually using. This call is just discovery."

When They Ask About Pricing

"Totally fair question. What I'd rather do is use this call to understand your situation, and then on the next call I'll put together an actual roadmap with a recommendation that makes sense for your business. Pricing depends on what's right for you — we'll cover all of that then."

When They Ask If Placements Are Guaranteed

"Yes — we guarantee the total number of placements in your campaign. We have a 95% success rate getting you placed on the publications you pre-approve. If something like Forbes isn't ready that month, we move it to the next and publish you on another pre-approved site instead."

When You Don't Know the Answer

"Good question — that's more on the technical side. Let me take that back to the team and I'll get you a clear answer on the next call." (Or just pull Matt in if he's on the call.)

Bridging to Call 2

"What I'll do from here is put together a full analysis — where you're showing up, where your competitors are, and a sample publication list. We also include a free first placement if you move forward. Does [day] or [day] work better for you?"

A Few Things to Keep in Mind

Own the first 90 seconds. If you set the frame at the start, the rest of the call follows your lead. If you let them set it, you're playing catch-up the whole time.

Questions should build on each other — current state, then goals, then the gap, then book the next call. If a question doesn't move the conversation toward that, skip it.

It's okay not to know something. Saying "I'll get that answered for you" is professional. Guessing or hedging is what loses trust.
