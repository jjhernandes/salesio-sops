Key Coaching from This Call

Core Issue

Mirroring alone doesn't move the needle

David's mirroring technique gets people talking and opening up — but he's collecting information, not creating emotional movement. Repeating back what they said gets acknowledgment. It doesn't get them to feel the weight of their problem. That feeling is what drives decisions.

"Any type of stalling in a sale is rooted in confusion. Any type of movement forward is rooted in clarity."

The fix: mirror, then probe. Repeat it back to confirm you heard it, then immediately ask a question that gets them to feel it — "why is that a problem for you?" or "what does that actually mean for your business?"

Technique

If you can't answer it in your mind — ask

The prospect said "I'm in a bind" and "I'm having difficulty." David moved past both without probing. JJ's rule: if you hear a charged word and you can't clearly articulate what it means for them, they can't feel it either. Vague pain stays vague. Specific pain moves people.

When you hear a word like "bind" or "difficulty" — pause the mic, ask yourself: do I know exactly what that means to him? If not, ask. "When you say bind, what does that actually look like for you right now?"

Technique

Elicit emotion, not just information

When Lou said he wanted to move away from contracting, David confirmed it and moved on. JJ flagged it — that was a moment to go deeper. "Why do you want to move away from it?" forces him to articulate his own reasons, which makes him feel them. When people say their own reasons out loud, they sell themselves.

"You want them to think about what they're saying so they can feel some emotion — that's what elicits movement onto the next stage."

Pattern

First 6 minutes sets the tone for the whole call

JJ watched 6 minutes and could already see where the rest of the call would go. The behaviors you allow early — letting them be vague, not probing on key words, skipping emotional depth — compound. By the end you're 30 minutes in with a lot of surface-level data and no real emotional connection built.

Identity Connection

How you operate off calls shows up on calls

David acknowledged coming back home triggered old habits — worse time management, less structure, less awareness. JJ connected this directly to his call behavior: same pattern, different context. The scattered structure off calls showed up as scattered structure on the call. More structure in life, more structure on calls.

"It's almost like how you're operating is showing up in your calls. I hope you see that."

Key Principles

Mirror + probe. Mirroring gets them talking. Probing gets them feeling. You need both. One without the other leaves you with long calls and shallow outcomes.

If you can't define it, they can't feel it. Vague words like "bind," "difficulty," and "struggle" mean nothing until you make them define it. Ask what they mean. That's where the real pain lives.

You're in less control when you only mirror. Predictable outcomes require a predictable process. Letting the prospect lead through mirroring alone means your results vary. Probe questions give you the wheel.

Clarity sells, confusion kills. Everything that slows a sale is rooted in confusion. Everything that speeds it up is rooted in clarity — in them, and in you.
