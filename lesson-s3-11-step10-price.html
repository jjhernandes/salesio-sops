Step 10 — Price Reveal

Say it once. Hold the silence. Everything you do after stating the price — before the prospect speaks — makes it worse.

The Rule

State the price once. Clearly. Then stop talking.

Not "the investment is $7,500 — which includes everything I mentioned, the coaching, the job board, the community, and the guarantee — so, yeah, it's $7,500." Just: "The investment to join the mastermind is $7,500." Then silence.

Why Once

Every word after the price is a signal to the prospect that you're uncomfortable with it. When you immediately justify, explain, or add context after stating the number, you are telling them: "I'm not sure you're going to be okay with this." That signals to them that maybe they shouldn't be okay with it.

What Happens When You Over-Explain Before They React

Rep says the price and immediately continues: "...which I know sounds like a lot, but when you break it down it's really just a small monthly payment if you do the three-way split, and we also have Klarna which can get it down to around $300 a month, plus the guarantee means if you don't make it back we refund you so really the risk is low..."

What the prospect hears: this is expensive, the rep knows it, and they're bracing for my reaction. The rep's anxiety has now been transferred to the prospect. What might have been a manageable reaction becomes a bigger deal because the rep treated it like one.

The Silence Does the Work

After you state the price and stop, the prospect is thinking. They're processing. They're deciding what they think and feel about that number. Let them do that without interference. The silence isn't awkward — it's the moment the decision is being made. Your job is to hold it, not fill it.

Holding the Silence — The Hardest Thing Most Reps Can't Do

Why It's Hard

After you say a high price and then stop talking, every instinct tells you to fill the silence. Your body registers the quiet as danger. You feel the urge to rescue the conversation from potential rejection by adding context, softening the number, or offering something.

Resist all of it. The silence is not rejection. It's thinking. And the rep who holds it communicates certainty — which is exactly what the prospect needs to see.

The silence after price is one of the most concrete expressions of non-neediness in the entire call. When you hold it comfortably, you are demonstrating that you believe the price is fair, the solution is real, and you don't need the prospect to react in any particular way.

That certainty transfers. Prospects are more likely to say yes to a rep who holds the price without flinching.

How long to hold it: until they speak. There is no time limit. Five seconds. Ten seconds. Twenty. Let them break the silence. The first person to speak after the price loses the frame.

How to Respond to the First Reaction

First ReactionComposed Response
"Wow, that's a lot." [2-second pause] "Yeah it's a real investment. What part of it feels like the stretch for you right now?"
Silence for 8+ seconds Continue to hold. Do not speak first. The prospect is thinking — that's what you want.
"That's more than I expected." [pause] "What number were you expecting?" — then listen. Don't justify the price yet.
"Okay. How does payment work?" They're in. Walk them through payment options. Don't add more selling.

Call Exchange — Flinching vs. Holding

VERSION 1 — Rep Who Flinches on Price
Rep
"So the investment is $7,500 — which, I know that's a big number, but we do have financing options through Klarna where you can get it down to like $300 a month, and the three-way split gets you to $3,333 a month over three months, and there's also the guarantee so really your risk is..."
Prospect said nothing. Rep pre-empted a reaction that hadn't happened. The justification signals insecurity about the price. Prospect now thinks: maybe I should push back on this.
VERSION 2 — Rep Who Holds the Price
Rep
"To join the mastermind — the investment is $7,500."
Stated once. Clearly. Then nothing. Silence. Rep holds it.
Prospect
"Hmm. That's... yeah, okay. How does that work?"
Rep
"You can do it in full, or we have a two-pay at $3,750 each, or a three-way split at $3,333 a month. What works best for you?"
The silence worked. The prospect processed on their own and moved to logistics. Rep responded with options, not justifications. The close is in motion.
