The Handoff Rule

The Sales.io template this is built from never pretends to be someone else — JJ texts as JJ. Same rule here: the lead should feel like the DM never stopped, without anyone lying about who's on the other end.

Do

Open as a real named person on Jasmin's team, in the first message, before anything else. Casual, first-name, lowercase — matches how she'd actually text.

Don't

Never sign messages "Jasmin" or let the lead assume they're texting her directly without correcting it. That's the fastest way to torch trust on the call.

Do

Multiple short bubbles, not one paragraph — mirrors how the lead was just DMing on Instagram. Lowercase openers, real punctuation only where it'd naturally land.

Don't

No pitching, no program details, no pricing in this sequence. Its only jobs: gather context, get them prepped, get them to show up.

The Sequence

Six triggers from booking to call time. Each one is time-delayed off the last, not off the clock — if they reply early, the human takes over and the automation stands down.
