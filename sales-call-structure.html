Call 1 — The Free Placement Anchor Call

Goal: Understand their situation, anchor the free placement, get them to pick their publication, confirm they have the money, and set Call 2 with momentum already built. This call should end with them having already made a decision — they just need to see it on paper.

01
Open & Discovery
0 – 8 min

They replied to a cold email about PR and media placements. They know what Swayyem does in general. Your job is to find out why it matters to them specifically — before you say anything about the offer.

1
Ask what's going on with their PR and media presence right now. Let them talk. Don't interrupt.
2
Ask: "What publication would actually move the needle for your business?" — Listen for the name. This is the free placement you'll anchor with.
3
Ask: "What would getting on Forbes actually do for you — is it credibility, traffic, clients, or something else?" — This tells you their pain, which you'll reference when you close.
4
Keep this short. 5–8 minutes max. You need enough to know which free placement to anchor with. Then move.
02
Drop the Free Placement
8 – 12 min
⚡
Most important moment on the call. Say the line below, then stop talking. Let them respond. Do not jump to the bundle logic until they react to the free placement first.
Say This — Word for Word
"So here's how we work — we always include one free publication placement with every campaign. Based on what you just told me, I think [Forbes / Wired] makes the most sense for you. Do you want to be on Forbes, or does Wired feel like a better fit?"

They pick one. That's the commitment. The moment they say the name — they're in. Now move to the bundle.

Immediately After They Pick
"Perfect. So [Forbes] is on the house — that's yours. Here's the thing: one placement alone is never going to move the needle. If you only run one, you won't see changes in traffic, authority, or anything else worth measuring. We always do a minimum of three total placements so the campaign actually gets traction.

The free one is locked in. We just need to figure out which two make the most sense alongside it. Are you thinking [TechCrunch] and [USA Today], or is there a different combination that fits your audience better?"
They engage — start picking
They're thinking about which publications to pair. They're building the package with you. This is exactly where you want to be. Stay curious — ask questions, don't talk over them.
"Okay so Forbes is the free one, and you're leaning toward TechCrunch and USA Today — that's actually a really strong combination for your space. Let me tell you what that scope typically looks like..."
They say "I only want the free one"
Fast disqualifier. Don't fight it. Confirm the rule calmly and let them decide.
"Totally get it — and we can't do just the one. Minimum three, otherwise there's not enough volume to actually produce results for you. If the timing isn't right, no worries at all — when it is, come back to us."
03
Lock in the Two Paid Placements
12 – 18 min

By now they've picked their free publication. They're invested. Now you're helping them choose the two paid placements — not selling them. Guide them toward a combination based on what they told you in discovery.

Guide the Decision
"Based on what you said about [what they're trying to achieve], I'd probably recommend [TechCrunch] for the tech credibility angle and [USA Today] for the broader reach. That combination with Forbes gives you coverage across authority, industry, and mass awareness. Does that feel right, or do you want to swap one out?"
⚠
If they say "I need to check with my boss" — get the boss on Call 2. Do not move forward without the decision-maker. "Can we get them on the next call? I'll have the drafts ready to show you both — it'll be 20 minutes."
04
Qualify Budget
18 – 22 min

They've already picked their publications — they're emotionally invested. Budget questions land softer after commitment than before it. Ask directly. Don't dance around it.

Ask This Directly
"Just so I can make sure we build this out the right way — what does the investment side look like for you right now? I want to put together something that actually makes sense for your situation, not just the biggest package I can sell."
Budget confirmed
Move straight to setting Call 2. Don't linger here. The deal isn't closed yet.
Vague or hesitant on budget
Get specific. Don't accept "I'm not sure right now" as a full answer.
"That's fair — is it more of a timing thing or is the investment itself the concern? I just want to make sure we're solving the right problem."
05
Set Call 2 — Give Them Something to Show Up For
22 – 25 min
Close Call 1 With This
"Here's what I'm going to do before we get back on. I'm going to have our team start putting together what your [Forbes] piece could actually look like — the angle, the headline, the type of story that performs best in your space. I noted that you mentioned [specific thing from discovery], so I want to show you how we'd position that.

Let's get on the calendar for tomorrow — I just need 20–30 minutes to walk you through what this looks like. Sound good?"

Before you hang up, confirm three things: who's making the decision, whether anyone else needs to be on Call 2, and what day/time works. Do not end the call without a booked follow-up.

Call 2 — Design Review & Close

Goal: Show them what their free placement looks like, confirm the package, and close. This call should feel like a delivery — not a pitch. They already made the decision on Call 1. You're just showing them the product of that decision.

01
Anchor Back to What They Decided
0 – 3 min
Open With This
"Good to have you back. So last time you landed on [Forbes] for the free placement, and you were thinking [TechCrunch] and [USA Today] as the two. I had our team pull together what that Forbes piece would actually look like based on what you shared with me. Let me show you what we've got."

Recap their decisions in their own words. They chose this. You're just confirming it and delivering on what you promised.

02
Walk Through the Draft Concepts
3 – 15 min
1
Show the Forbes piece concept — the angle, headline direction, positioning. Make it specific to what they told you. If they mentioned a specific goal or achievement, it should be in there.
2
Walk through brief concepts for TechCrunch and USA Today. Show how the three placements work together — authority, industry credibility, broad reach.
3
Ask: "Does this feel like the right direction for how you want to be positioned?" — Not "do you like it?" You want them reacting to fit, not aesthetics.
03
Close
15 – 25 min
The Close — Say This
"So here's what we've got. [Forbes] is included — that's yours on the house. The two placements we talked about, [TechCrunch] and [USA Today], come out to [price]. Three placements total, the minimum we need to produce results you'll actually feel.

Based on everything you've told me, I think this is the right move. Do you want to lock this in, or is there something we need to work through?" STOP TALKING
⚡
After the closing question — do not fill the silence. The next person who speaks loses the frame. Whatever they say tells you exactly where to go next.
They say yes
Collect payment or send the contract before the call ends. Don't hang up without next steps locked.
"Perfect. I'm going to send the contract over right now — as soon as that's signed we kick off. We'll schedule the briefing call to get your full story and the angles locked in."
They have an objection
Understand what it actually is before responding. Don't assume it's price.
"I want to make sure this is right for you. What's giving you pause — is it the investment, the timing, or something about the approach we put together?"
Objection Handles
"I only want the free placement."
Say This
"Completely get it — and the free placement is real, it's yours. The reason we can't do just the one is that a single placement doesn't produce enough distribution to move anything you'd actually measure. Traffic, authority, backlinks — they all need volume. If we ran one placement for you, we'd be setting you up to feel like it didn't work, and that's not something we're willing to do.

If the timing isn't right for the full package right now, that's totally fine. When it is — we're here."

Then let it go. If they still won't move, they're not a buyer. Don't chase. Move to the next lead.

"I need to think about it." / "I need to talk to my boss."
Say This
"That makes sense. What specifically do you need to think through? I want to make sure you have everything you need to make this easy."

If it's a boss: get them on Call 2. A proposal sent to a gatekeeper without the decision-maker on the call is a dead deal. "Can we get them on the next call? I just need 20 minutes — I'll have the concepts ready to show you both."

"The price is too high."
Say This
"Help me understand — is it that the investment doesn't feel worth it for what we're delivering, or is it more of a cash flow or timing issue right now?"

These are two different problems. Worth-it is a value conversation. Cash flow is a structuring conversation — payment terms, phased start, smaller initial package. Don't treat them the same.

"Can we just start with one and see how it goes?"
Say This
"If one placement would work for you, we'd offer it. The reason we don't is that one placement doesn't give us enough distribution to produce results worth measuring. You'd spend the money and feel like nothing happened — and that's on us. Three is the minimum because it's the truth, not because we want to sell you more."
Disqualify Fast

Enzo said it clearly: figure out if they have the money and disqualify fast on the first call. Every minute you spend on a non-buyer is a minute off a real one. These are the exits.

What They Say / DoWhat It MeansYour Exit
"I only want the free one." Not a buyer. Wants something for nothing. "Totally understand — minimum three for it to work. If the timing changes, we're here."
"We don't have budget right now." Not ready. Could come back in 60–90 days. "Makes sense. When does that change? I'll follow up then."
Can't name a publication they care about. Doesn't understand the value of PR. Hard to close. Educate briefly. If they still can't engage — close the call politely.
Decision-maker won't come to Call 2. You're talking to a gatekeeper. Deal will stall indefinitely. "Can we get them on together? Otherwise we keep going back and forth and I don't want to waste your time."
"We're just exploring options." Low urgency. Not a near-term buyer. "What would need to change for this to become a real priority?" If no clear answer — exit.
Still won't pick a publication after two attempts. Not engaged. Wasting your time. "I want to make sure this is actually relevant for you right now — is it?" Then listen.
Quick Reference
Everything on One Card
The offer
1 free placement (Forbes or Wired) + minimum 2 paid placements (TechCrunch, USA Today, etc.) = minimum 3 total. One placement alone doesn't move metrics.
Call 1 goal
Get them to pick their free publication. Lock in the two paid ones. Qualify budget. Set Call 2 with draft concepts ready.
Call 2 goal
Show the Forbes draft. Confirm the package. Close. Collect payment or send contract before the call ends.
The anchor line
"We always include one free placement. Do you want Forbes or Wired?"
The bundle line
"Minimum three total — one free, two paid. One placement alone won't produce results you can measure."
Fast disqualifier
"I only want the free one" = not a buyer. Exit in under 10 minutes. Move on.
After you close
Stop talking. Silence wins. The next person who speaks loses the frame.
The math
60 meetings/month × 15% close = 10–12 deals. That's a massive win. Volume isn't the problem — this framework is the fix.
