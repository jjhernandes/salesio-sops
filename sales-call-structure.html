Swayyem · Sales Call Framework

Own the Search.
Win the AI.
Two-Call Close.

This isn't PR. It's market presence infrastructure. Every call starts with showing prospects the gap between where they are and where their competitors already are — in search results, AI answers, and the publications that build authority. The free placement is the anchor. The roadmap is what closes.

What They Think They're Buying
Press coverage. A few placements in known publications. Good PR.
What They're Actually Buying
Owned presence in the search results and AI answers their prospects use to evaluate who to buy from.
Your Job on Every Call
Show them the gap live. Make the invisible visible. Then close it with a roadmap.
Call 1 — Market Presence Audit

Goal: Uncover their PR/search/AI gaps through discovery, then make those gaps visible on a live screenshare. Anchor Call 2 as a strategy session where you'll bring back filtered site inventory, a deeper search analysis, and a roadmap built around what you learned today. The free placement gets anchored here as part of what's coming on Call 2.

01
Discovery — PR, Search & AI Audit
0 – 12 min

You're building a picture of where they stand — in PR, in search, and in AI results. These three are connected. Everything you learn feeds directly into the live gap analysis you're about to run. Don't rush this. The better the discovery, the more powerful the screenshare.

Layer 1 — Current Situation
Open With This
"Before I get into anything on our end — walk me through what you're currently doing for PR, search visibility, or building your online presence. What does that look like right now?"
Then Ask
"When a potential client is evaluating you against a competitor — where are they looking? What do they find?"
Listen For
  • No real strategy — they're doing social media, maybe some SEO, but nothing structured for authority or search presence
  • Past PR attempts that felt hollow — one placement, no results, didn't know how to use it
  • Awareness of the problem — they know competitors are showing up somewhere they're not
  • A specific trigger — lost a deal, a competitor got press, a funding round is coming
Layer 2 — Search & AI Visibility

Most prospects have never thought about where they show up in AI answers or what search results look like when their buyers are evaluating them. These questions make that visible — before you even pull up a screen.

Ask This
"If one of your ideal clients went to Google right now and searched for something like '[your category] + [buying intent]' — do you know what comes up? Is your name in there?"
Then Ask
"What about AI — if someone asked ChatGPT or Perplexity who the best [category] is in your space, what do you think comes up? Have you ever checked?"
Dig Into Competitors
"Who are the two or three competitors you run into most often — the ones you're compared against when you're in a deal?"
⚡
Write down every competitor they name and every search phrase they use. You'll pull these up live in Phase 2. This is your screenshare material.
Listen For
  • Named competitors — you'll search these live and show exactly where they're winning
  • Search phrases or questions — these are the queries you'll run during the screenshare
  • Uncertainty or "I've never checked" — this is perfect. The screenshare will be a revelation
  • Already knows they're behind — they've seen it and know they're losing ground
Layer 3 — Implications
Ask This
"If a competitor is showing up in those results and you're not — what does that actually cost you when a buyer is making a decision?"
Then Ask
"What made you pick up the phone today — was there a specific moment or has this been building?"
Listen For
  • Lost deals or opportunities — credibility was the gap
  • A named competitor gaining traction — urgency is real
  • An upcoming event or milestone — launch, conference, fundraise, rebrand
  • Vague urgency — "just feels like the right time" — probe for something real before moving on
Before the Screenshare — Confirm You Have These
2–3 competitors they named. These are what you'll search live.
3–5 search queries or questions their buyers use. The exact phrases matter.
Their current PR/search/AI strategy — or confirmation that there isn't one.
What a missed deal or lost visibility has cost them. They said it — not you.
Their ICP — who's actually buying from them, and where those buyers go to research decisions.
02
Live Gap Analysis — Screenshare
12 – 22 min

This is the most powerful moment on the call. You're making the invisible visible — showing them exactly where they're missing and where competitors are already winning. Pull up your screen. Don't narrate what you're doing. Let the results do the work.

⚡
Ask permission first: "Do you mind if I pull up your screen and show you something live?" Then share your screen. Run everything in real time — don't use screenshots or prepared slides. Real results land harder.
Search — Run These Live
Start with Google. Run the queries they gave you. Then run the same queries with their competitor names.
[Their category] + [buying intent phrase they used]
best [their service/product] for [their ICP]
[Competitor name] reviews
[Competitor name] vs [their company]
What to Point Out
Show where competitors appear — in editorial coverage, mentions, Google's featured results. Then show where the prospect is not. Don't editorialize. Just say: "See this? Your competitor is here. Here's where you show up." Let the silence land.
AI Answers — Run These Live
Open ChatGPT and/or Perplexity. Ask it what their buyer would ask. Show them what the AI recommends — and whether they're in it.
Who are the best [their category] companies for [their ICP]?
What should I look for when choosing a [their service]?
Compare [competitor A] and [competitor B]
What to Point Out
AI answers are built from published content. If a company has editorial coverage in authoritative publications, the AI references it. If they don't, they don't exist in the answer. Show this contrast live. Then explain: "The way you get into these answers is by building the published presence that AI trains on."
After the Screenshare — Say This
"So that's the gap. Your competitors are showing up in the places your buyers go to make decisions — search, AI recommendations, editorial coverage. You're not. That's what we fix. And the way we fix it isn't just by getting you one press hit — it's by building the presence that makes you show up consistently across all of those touchpoints."
03
Frame the Solution & Anchor Call 2
22 – 27 min

Don't pitch the full package yet. Frame what's coming on Call 2 — a tailored strategy built from what you learned today. The free placement gets introduced here as a concrete starting point, not the whole story.

Say This
"Here's what I want to do before we get back on. Based on what you told me today — your competitors, the queries, your ICP — I'm going to have our team filter our inventory for the sites and publications that actually reach your buyers and carry the authority to move search and AI results. I'm also going to pull a deeper analysis of your specific competitors against those exact queries.

When we get back on, I'll show you a roadmap — which placements, in what sequence, to start closing that gap. And here's the thing: we always include one free placement with every campaign. Based on what you just showed me matters most to you, I want to come back with a recommendation on where that free placement should go and why.

This isn't about getting one press hit. It's about building the presence that puts you in the room when your buyers are making decisions."
⚠
Don't name the free publication yet. Let them ask about it or bring it up on Call 2 — it lands harder when it's revealed as part of a tailored strategy, not offered generically on the first call.
04
Qualify Budget
27 – 31 min

They've just seen the gap live. They understand the problem. Budget questions land better now than at any other point on the call. Ask directly.

Ask This Directly
"Do you have a budget set aside for PR or search/AI visibility work?"
Budget confirmed
Move straight to setting Call 2. Don't linger.
Vague or hesitant
Get specific. Don't accept "I'm not sure right now."
"That's fair — is it more of a timing thing or is the investment itself the concern? I want to make sure the roadmap I put together is actually realistic for where you're at."
05
Set Call 2 — Give Them Something Real to Show Up For
31 – 35 min
Close Call 1 With This
"Here's exactly what I'm bringing to our next call: a filtered list of sites and publications that fit your ICP and carry the right authority for search and AI visibility, a deeper look at your competitors against the specific queries you gave me today, and a campaign roadmap tied to your actual goals — not a generic package.

It'll be 25–30 minutes. I want to show you what this actually looks like before you make any decision. Does tomorrow work?"

Before you hang up: confirm who's making the decision, whether anyone else needs to be on Call 2, and lock the time. Do not end the call without a booked follow-up.

Before Call 2 — Build These
Filtered site/publication inventory matched to their ICP and the authority types that influence their search/AI results.
Deeper search/AI analysis using their exact competitors and the specific queries they gave you on Call 1.
Campaign roadmap — which placements, in what sequence, and why. Tied to the gaps you showed them live.
Free placement recommendation — one specific publication, with a clear reason why it's the right anchor for their goals and their ICP.
Pricing — total investment for the recommended package, ready to present when you close.
Call 2 — Strategy Presentation & Close

Goal: Deliver on exactly what you promised — filtered inventory, a deeper search/AI analysis, and a roadmap. Present the free placement as the anchor for the campaign. Close. This call should feel like a delivery meeting, not a pitch. They know the problem. You have the plan.

01
Open — Anchor Back to What They Saw
0 – 3 min
Open With This
"Good to have you back. So last time we pulled up your search results live and looked at where [competitor A] and [competitor B] are showing up — and where you're not. I went and built what I said I would. Let me show you what we put together."

Reference specifically what they saw on the screenshare. The gap they felt on Call 1 is what makes them ready to buy on Call 2.

02
Present the Research
3 – 15 min
1
Show the filtered site inventory — the specific publications and sites selected for their ICP. Explain why each one matters for their search/AI presence and their buyer's decision journey. This isn't a menu. It's a curated recommendation.
2
Run the deeper search/AI analysis — use the exact competitor names and search queries from Call 1. Show updated results, highlight the gaps, and connect each gap to a specific placement in the inventory. Make the problem and the solution visible at the same time.
3
Introduce the free placement — "Part of every campaign is a free placement we include as the anchor. Based on everything you told me and what I've now seen about your space, I'd recommend [specific publication] — here's why it's the right fit for your goals and your buyer." One recommendation. A clear reason. Then ask: "Does that feel right?"
⚠
Don't overwhelm them with every site in the inventory. Present the 3–5 that matter most for their specific situation. Quality of reasoning beats quantity of options.
03
Walk the Roadmap
15 – 22 min

Show them the sequence — not just what placements, but in what order and why. Connect each step back to the gaps they saw on Call 1 and the outcomes they said mattered most in discovery.

1
Free placement first — sets the authority anchor. Explain how it feeds into search and AI results.
2
Paid placements in sequence — each one adds a layer of coverage: industry credibility, broad reach, AI training signal.
3
Expected outcome — "By the time this campaign runs, when someone searches for [their category] or asks an AI who the best [their space] is, you start showing up in those answers."
Check the Fit
"Does this roadmap match what you're trying to accomplish, or is there something we should adjust?"
04
Close
22 – 30 min
The Close — Say This
"Here's what we've got. [Free placement] is included — that's yours on the house. The campaign we've put together — [placements] — comes out to [price]. This is the minimum package that actually starts moving your search and AI presence in a measurable way. Anything less and you won't see results worth measuring.

Based on everything you've told me and what I've seen in your space — this is the right move. Do you want to lock this in, or is there something we need to work through?" STOP TALKING
⚡
After the closing question — do not fill the silence. The next person who speaks loses the frame. Whatever they say tells you exactly where to go next.
They say yes
Collect payment or send the contract before the call ends. Don't hang up without next steps locked.
"Perfect. I'm sending the contract right now — once that's signed we kick off. We'll schedule a briefing call to lock in your angles and get the content started."
They have an objection
Understand what it actually is before responding. Don't assume it's price.
"I want to make sure this is right for you. What's giving you pause — is it the investment, the timing, or something about the approach we built?"
Objection Handles
"I only want the free placement."
Say This
"Completely get it — and the free placement is real, it's yours. But one placement alone doesn't build the presence we're talking about. What you saw in those search and AI results — that's built over multiple touchpoints, not a single hit. If we ran one placement for you, you'd spend the money and feel like nothing changed. That's not something we're willing to do.

If the timing isn't right for the full campaign right now, that's fine. When it is — we're here."

Then let it go. If they still won't move, they're not a buyer today. Don't chase.

"How long until we see results?"
Say This
"Typically 60–90 days before you start seeing the presence build in search and AI results. The placements go live, they get indexed, and they start influencing what shows up when your buyers search. The roadmap we put together is sequenced to build that momentum — it's not a one-shot thing."

If they push for guarantees — be honest. Results depend on the space, the competition, and the campaign quality. You can't promise ranking positions but you can show them the logic.

"I need to think about it." / "I need to talk to my boss."
Say This
"That makes sense. What specifically do you need to think through? I want to make sure you have everything you need to make this easy."

If it's a boss: get them on Call 2. A proposal sent to a gatekeeper without the decision-maker on the call is a dead deal. "Can we get them on the next call? I just need 25 minutes — I'll have the full analysis and roadmap ready for you both."

"The price is too high."
Say This
"Help me understand — is it that the investment doesn't feel worth it for what we're building, or is it more of a cash flow or timing issue right now?"

These are two different problems. Worth-it is a value conversation — go back to what they saw in the screenshare and what they said it costs them to stay invisible. Cash flow is a structuring conversation — payment terms, phased start, adjusted package scope.

"We're already doing SEO / we have a PR agency."
Say This
"That's good context — what I showed you today isn't SEO and it's different from traditional PR. What we're building is the editorial presence that search engines and AI models actually cite. SEO optimizes your owned pages. What we do is build the third-party coverage that determines whether you show up in results and AI answers that you don't control. They work together — they're not the same thing."
Disqualify Fast

Qualify budget and decision authority early. Every minute you spend on a non-buyer is a minute off a real one. These are the exits.

"We don't have budget for this." Not ready. Could come back in 60–90 days. "Makes sense. When does that change? I'll follow up then — and what you saw today isn't going to get better while you wait."
Can't name a competitor or a search query. Doesn't have enough market awareness to engage. The screenshare won't land. Educate briefly on why this matters. If they still can't engage — close the call politely and follow up when they're more informed.
Decision-maker won't come to Call 2. You're talking to a gatekeeper. Deal will stall indefinitely. "Can we get them on together? I'm going to show a full analysis of your search and AI presence — they'll want to see this."
"We're just exploring options." Low urgency. Not a near-term buyer. "What would need to change for this to become a real priority?" If no clear answer — exit and set a follow-up date.
Disengaged during the screenshare. They don't feel the pain yet. Hard to close without urgency. "I want to make sure this is actually relevant for where you're trying to go — is search and AI presence something that's on your radar for this year?" Then listen.
