The Free Placement
Anchor Framework

A two-call structure that uses the free publication placement as the anchor to compress the sales cycle, qualify budget faster, and disqualify time-wasters in under 10 minutes.

Why This Works
22–24%
Reply rate when free placement was in the offer
14–15%
Reply rate after free placement was removed
2 calls
Target cycle (down from 3–5)

The data is clear. The free placement is the conversion mechanism — it gets replies in outreach and it should anchor the sales conversation. Right now it disappears the moment someone picks up the phone. That's the gap this framework closes.

Reciprocity
Giving something real first creates obligation. Letting them choose their free Forbes placement builds ownership before the paid conversation starts.
Commitment & Consistency
Once they say "I want Forbes" — they've made a micro-commitment. Call 2 is not a pitch. It's a delivery on a decision they already made.
Fast Disqualification
"I only want the free one" = not a buyer. You have your answer in under 10 minutes. That's saved three wasted calls.
The Bundle Logic
Minimum 3 placements for traction is true. One placement doesn't move metrics. This gives you a principled reason they can't just take the free one and leave.
Call 1 — The Anchor Call

Goal: Anchor the free placement, get them to pick their publication, qualify budget, and set Call 2 with momentum already built.

01
Open & Discovery
0 – 8 min
1
Build quick rapport. Ask what brought them to the call. Let them talk. You want to understand what they're trying to achieve, not what they know about Swayyem.
2
Key questions:

"What does your current PR or media presence look like?"
"What publications would actually move the needle for your audience?"
"What would getting on Forbes or Wired do for your business specifically?"
3
Listen for which publications matter to them. This tells you exactly which free placement to anchor with. File it away for Phase 2.
02
Drop the Free Placement Anchor
8 – 14 min
⚡
Most important moment in the call. Drop it, then pause. Let them respond before you say anything else. Do not rush to explain the bundle.
Say This
"So here's how we work — we always include one free publication placement with every campaign. Based on what you've shared, [Forbes / Wired / TechCrunch] would probably be the strongest fit for your audience. Does that feel right, or is there another publication that would make more sense?"

Let them pick. The moment they say the name — that's the commitment. Now move to the bundle.

Then Say This
"Perfect. So [Forbes] is yours on the house. Here's what we've learned — one placement alone doesn't move the metrics. Traffic, authority, backlinks — they all need enough volume to register. We always run a minimum of three total placements for a campaign to actually work for you.

The free one is yours. We just need to figure out which two make the most sense alongside it. Based on your audience, are you thinking [TechCrunch] and [USA Today], or is there a different combination that fits better?"
They engage
They start thinking about which publications. They're building the package with you — not being sold to. This is where you want to be.
"Okay so [Forbes] is the free one, and you're thinking [TechCrunch] and [USA Today]. That's a strong combination for your space. Let me tell you what that scope typically looks like..."
They hesitate on price
Don't panic. This is a budget signal. Acknowledge it and ask directly.
"That's totally fair. What does the investment side look like for you right now? I want to make sure we're building something that works within your reality."
03
Qualify Budget & Decision Process
14 – 20 min
1
Ask about budget directly. By this point they've chosen their free placement — they're invested. Budget questions land softer after commitment than before it.
2
Ask about decision-making. "Is this something you can move on, or do you need to loop someone else in before we build this out?"
3
If they need approval: get that person on Call 2. "Can we get [name] on the next call so we're not going back and forth? I'll have the draft concepts ready to show you both."
Budget Question
"Just so I can build this out correctly — what's the range you're working with? I want to make sure we put together something that actually makes sense for you, not just the biggest package we can sell."
04
Set Call 2 with Momentum
20 – 25 min
Close Call 1 With This
"Here's what I'm going to do before we get back together. I'm going to have our team pull together what your [Forbes] piece could actually look like — the angle, the hook, the type of article that performs best in your space. You mentioned [specific thing from discovery]. I want to show you how we'd position that.

Let's get on the calendar for [day/time] — 30 minutes is all we need. Sound good?"
⚠
Before hanging up: confirm who's making the decision, who else needs to be on Call 2, and their timeline. Do not leave this vague.
Call 2 — The Review & Close

Goal: Show them what their free placement looks like, reconfirm the package they built on Call 1, and close. This should feel like a handoff, not a pitch.

01
Anchor Back to Their Decision
0 – 3 min
Open With This
"Good to have you back. So last time you landed on [Forbes] for the free placement, and you were thinking [TechCrunch] and [USA Today] for the two. I had our team pull together what the Forbes piece could look like based on what you shared. Let me show you what we've got."

Recap their decision in their own words. They made this choice — you're confirming it. This is not a pitch. This is delivery on a promise.

02
Present the Draft Concepts
3 – 15 min
1
Show what the Forbes article could look like — the angle, headline direction, positioning. Make it specific to them. Generic doesn't close.
2
Brief concepts for the two paid placements. Show how all three work together to build traction.
3
Ask: "Does this feel like the right direction for where you want to be positioned?" — not "do you like it?" You want them responding to fit, not aesthetics.
03
Present the Package & Close
15 – 25 min
The Close
"So here's what we're looking at. You've got [Forbes] included. The two placements — [TechCrunch] and [USA Today] — come out to [price]. That gives you the minimum three we need to actually drive traction for you.

Based on everything you've shared, I think this is the right move. Do you want to lock this in, or is there something we need to work through?"
⚡
After the closing question — stop talking. The next person who speaks loses the frame. Let them respond. Whatever they say tells you exactly where to go.
They say yes
Collect payment or send contract before the call ends. Do not end the call without next steps locked.
"Perfect. I'll send over the contract now and we kick off as soon as that's signed. We'll schedule the briefing call to get your story and angles locked in."
They have an objection
Understand what it actually is before responding. Don't assume it's price.
"I want to make sure this is right for you. What's giving you pause — is it the investment, the timing, or something about the approach?"
Objection Handles
"I only want the free placement."
"Completely understand — the free placement is real, it's yours. The reason we can't do just one is that a single placement doesn't move the needle. Traffic, authority, backlinks — they all need enough volume to register. If we did just one, we'd be setting you up to feel like it didn't work, and that's not fair to you.

If the timing isn't right for the full campaign, no problem. When it is — we're here."

Then let it go. If they won't move, they're not a buyer right now. Don't chase.

"I need to think about it." / "I need to talk to my boss."
"That makes sense. What specifically do you need to think through? I want to make sure you have everything you need to make this easy to decide."

If it's a boss: get the boss on the next call. Do not send a proposal and wait. A proposal without a follow-up call is a dead deal.

"The price is too high."
"Help me understand — is it that the investment doesn't feel worth it for what we're delivering, or is it more of a cash flow / timing thing?"

Worth-it is a value conversation. Cash flow is a structuring conversation. They're completely different. Don't collapse them.

"Can we start with just one and see how it goes?"
"I wish we could — and if one placement would actually work for you, we'd offer it. The reason we don't is that one placement doesn't give us enough distribution to move your metrics in a way you'd feel. It ends up being a cost with no clear outcome. Three is the minimum for a reason — we're not trying to sell you more, we're trying to make sure it actually works."
Disqualifiers

If you hear any of these on Call 1, exit gracefully and move on. Do not push for Call 2.

SignalWhat It MeansExit Line
"I only want the free one." Not a buyer. Wants something for nothing. "Totally understood — if the timing ever changes, we're here."
"We have no budget right now." Not ready. Come back in 60–90 days. "Makes sense. Let's reconnect in [timeframe] — I'll reach out then."
Can't name a publication they care about. Not serious about PR. Doesn't understand the value. Educate briefly. If they still can't engage, close the call.
Decision-maker won't join Call 2. You're talking to a gatekeeper. Deal will stall. "Can we get [name] on together? Otherwise we're just going back and forth."
"We're just exploring options." Low urgency. Not a near-term buyer. "What would need to be true for this to become a real priority for you?"
