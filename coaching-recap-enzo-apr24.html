Daniel's Onboarding Plan

Run this in sequence. Don't skip steps or rush through them — the goal is that Daniel feels ready before he's ever live.

1
Run the scorecard interview. He walks in knowing exactly what he's stepping into — the expectations, the standards, the process. No surprises.
2
Week 1: Handbook review. He reads through the full handbook. Not skimming — reading. KPIs, pipeline stages, the framework, reporting standards. Know the system before operating in it.
3
Shadow 3 live calls with Enzo. Camera off, mic muted. He's observing. After each one, quick debrief — what did you notice, what questions do you have?
4
Review 3 recorded calls from Enzo independently. He takes notes on what he observes — what's working, what he'd do differently. He's not copying the style, he's building pattern recognition.
5
Roleplay before going live. Non-negotiable. He runs through the framework with Enzo before he's ever on a real call. Catches the obvious gaps before they happen in front of a prospect.
6
First live calls: Enzo in the room, camera off. He runs the call. Enzo listens. After each call — debrief. Not a performance review. A conversation.
7
Weekly KPI check-ins. Show rate, close rate, AOV. One to two adjustments per review — no more. Adjust, test, repeat. Don't overload him with feedback all at once.
How to Debrief After Calls
Not: "What the hell was that?" Not: nothing. The framework:

"What do you feel like worked?"
"What do you think didn't?"
"What's one thing you'd adjust next time?"

Let him diagnose first. Then add what you saw. If he's defensive or collapses — that tells you something. If he's curious and self-correcting — you've got a keeper. The debrief is a test as much as a coaching moment.
How to Have the Presentation Conversation with Daniel

You noticed the beard. You're right that it matters — especially in B2B at this price point. The people you're selling $20K+ to are judging everything they see in the first 30 seconds. That's not shallow, it's just true.

Frame it as a standard, not a personal critique. Come from care, not judgment. He'll respond well — Daniel's not soft.

How to Say It
"Before we get started, there's something I want to share with you — not as criticism, but because I genuinely want this to work for you. I've been in this space a long time, and one of the things I know for certain is that the first 30 seconds on a call sets the tone for everything that follows. The standard we have here is: look good, feel good, to be good.

That means business casual — clean collared shirt, no wrinkles. And a groomed presentation. Your background is great. I just want to make sure everything else matches it. The clients we're selling to are running serious businesses. When they're deciding whether to trust someone with their money, they're watching all of it. I'm not saying change who you are — I'm saying let's make sure how you show up earns you credibility before you even speak."
⚡
This conversation builds respect, not resentment — if you deliver it right. Closers stay on teams where the owner invests in them. This is an investment. Frame it that way.
