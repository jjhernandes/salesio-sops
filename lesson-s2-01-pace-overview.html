PASE Personality System

Scripts fail because they assume every prospect is the same person. They're not. PASE gives you a framework for reading who's in front of you and adapting in real time.

What PASE Is

PASE is a four-type personality framework built specifically for sales conversations. The four types are Practical, Action, Social, and Emotional.

Every prospect leans toward one primary type. Your job isn't to put them in a box — it's to understand what they respond to, what they fear, and how they make decisions so you can meet them there instead of making them meet you.

Why This Framework Exists

Most sales training teaches you what to say. The same pitch, the same objection handlers, the same close. That approach assumes everyone responds to the same triggers — they don't. A Practical type shuts down when you get emotional. An Emotional type disconnects when you lead with data. Same pitch, opposite reactions.

PASE teaches you to read the person first, then decide how to communicate. The pitch comes last, not first.

The Four Types

P
Practical
Data-driven. Skeptical. ROI-focused.
P types want proof. They ask hard questions and expect clear answers. They're not cold — they're cautious. They need to understand the logic before they feel. Over-emotionalizing the pitch or skipping the evidence kills the call with a P type.
Signals: lots of clarifying questions, references to past experiences that didn't work out, requests for specifics ("what does that actually mean?"), methodical pace.
A
Action
Competitive. Fast. Results-obsessed.
A types want to win and they want to win now. They're impatient with long explanations and energized by outcomes. They're often the most decisive buyers on a call — but if you move too slowly or get too nuanced, you lose them. Speed and directness close A types.
Signals: interrupting, asking "how fast can I see results?", dropping names of competitors or high performers, fast conversational pace, minimal small talk.
S
Social
Community-first. Risk-averse. Relationship-led.
S types make decisions based on belonging and safety. They want to know who else has done this, what the community is like, and whether this decision will be supported by people they trust. Isolating the decision or putting on pressure shuts them down immediately.
Signals: asking about the community, mentioning family, referencing what others have done, slower pace, lots of "we" language, wanting to feel understood before moving forward.
E
Emotional
Feeling-led. Story-driven. Transformation-focused.
E types buy based on how they imagine their future will feel. Logic is irrelevant until the emotional picture is clear. They connect through story, through shared experience, through vision. A clinical, data-heavy pitch makes them disengage instantly — not because they don't care, but because it doesn't speak to what's real for them.
Signals: sharing personal stories unprompted, emotional vocabulary ("I just feel like...", "I'm so tired of..."), asking "what will this actually be like?", wanting to be seen before being sold.

Why Personality Beats Scripts

The Script Problem

A script is a one-size answer to a question that isn't the same for everyone. "Here's why this program works" pitched the same way to a P type and an E type will land completely differently — one feels manipulated, one feels bored. Neither buys.

Personality-based selling doesn't give you different scripts. It gives you a different starting point: understand the person first, then decide how to speak. That flexibility is what elite closers have that average reps don't.

Wrong Approach, Wrong Type — What It Costs You

TypeWrong ApproachWhat Actually Lands
Practical (P) Leading with transformation stories, getting emotional, skipping the proof Specific results, clear ROI, logical sequence, no fluff
Action (A) Long rapport-building, over-explaining nuance, slow close Fast, direct, outcome-heavy, competitive framing, get to the point
Social (S) Pressure tactics, urgency, isolating the decision from family/peers Community language, safety, who else has done this, protecting what matters
Emotional (E) Data-heavy pitch, clinical delivery, skipping the human connection Story, vision, emotional permission, what the future actually feels like

The most common mistake: most reps pitch to their own PASE type. They lead the way they respond, not the way the prospect does. A high-A rep loses every S type they talk to. A high-E rep loses every P type. Know your type so you know your blind spots.

Call Exchange — Same Prospect, Two Approaches

VERSION 1 — Rep ignores PASE, defaults to emotional pitch with a Practical prospect
Prospect
"I want to understand exactly what I'm getting before I commit to anything. What's the structure?"
Rep
"Honestly it's transformational — people come in feeling stuck and they leave like completely different versions of themselves. The community is incredible, the energy is just different, you know?"
P type asked for structure. Rep gave feelings. P type is now skeptical of the entire conversation.
Prospect
"Right... but what does that actually mean in practice? Like what are the specific components?"
VERSION 2 — Rep reads the P type and adapts immediately
Prospect
"I want to understand exactly what I'm getting before I commit to anything. What's the structure?"
Rep
"Good — let me walk you through it clearly. Weekly 1-on-1 coaching, seven group sessions per week covering specific skill areas, a proprietary job board with 60-plus vetted companies, and a money-back guarantee if you don't hit your numbers. I'll give you specifics on each. What do you want to go through first?"
Matches their energy — direct, structured, no fluff. P type relaxes because the rep is operating at their frequency.
Prospect
"Let's start with the 1-on-1 coaching — what does that actually look like?"
