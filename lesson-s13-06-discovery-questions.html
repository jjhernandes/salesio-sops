Questions to Ask on the Offer Discovery Call

You are not there to impress them. You are there to evaluate a potential business partnership. That frame changes everything about how this call goes.

The Frame — You're Interviewing Them

Most reps go into an offer discovery call trying to convince the operator to hire them. That is the wrong orientation. You are evaluating whether this company is a good fit for you just as much as they are evaluating whether you are a good fit for them.

When you hold this frame, your questions become confident instead of tentative. You ask the hard ones. You do not flinch when they cannot answer. You treat vague answers as data. This is not arrogance — it is the behavior of someone who respects their own time and career enough to be selective.

Why This Frame Matters Practically

Operators can feel desperation. A rep who needs any role available asks soft questions or skips the hard ones entirely to avoid seeming demanding. A rep who is evaluating a business partnership asks the real questions and trusts that a good operator will respect the approach. The ones who do not are usually the ones with something to hide.

The 10 Questions Every Rep Must Ask

Question 01
"What is your current close rate, and who is generating it?"
Close rate is meaningless without context. 15% on warm inbound is different from 15% on cold outbound. Find out what the number is and what conditions are creating it.
Question 02
"What is the average deal size and commission structure?"
Get the specific number. Commission percentage. Whether it is flat fee or tiered. What the top-earning rep makes per month. You need the math to know if this is worth your time.
Question 03
"Where do leads come from and how many calls per week can I expect?"
This tells you your actual earning potential. 10% commission on 2 calls a week is poverty. 10% on 15 qualified inbound calls a week is a strong income. Volume and source matter as much as the rate.
Question 04
"What is your chargeback or refund rate, and how is it handled with reps?"
A high refund rate signals a product problem. And if the company deducts chargebacks from future commissions, you could be earning far less than the structure appears to offer. Know this upfront.
Question 05
"How long have your current reps been on the team?"
Rep tenure is one of the most honest data points available. Long tenure means reps are earning and being treated fairly. Short tenure or frequent turnover requires a real explanation.
Question 06
"What training or onboarding do you provide for new closers?"
Good operators have a ramp process. They want you to succeed. A company with zero onboarding structure puts all risk on the rep. Find out what support you get in the first 30 days.
Question 07
"What do your top reps make per month, and what does their pipeline look like?"
Ask for a real number, not a ceiling. Not "they can make up to $30K" but "our top rep made $18,400 last month. Here is what their call volume looked like." Real numbers or no deal.
Question 08
"What specific result does your product deliver for customers, and how do you track it?"
You need to believe in what you are closing. If the operator cannot articulate what customers get and how they measure it, you will have a hard time closing it with conviction. Conviction closes. Scripts don't.
Question 09
"Why did your last closer leave?"
This is the most revealing question on the list. Listen to how they answer it, not just what they say. Defensiveness, blame, or vagueness are all meaningful responses. A good operator answers this honestly and specifically.
Question 10
"What does a rep need to do to be successful here in the first 90 days?"
This surfaces expectations, structure, and whether the operator has thought carefully about rep performance. It also gives you a benchmark to hold yourself and them accountable to after you start.

What It Signals When They Can't Answer

Inability to answer the basic questions is itself an answer. An operator who has been running a successful sales operation for any meaningful period of time can cite their close rate, average deal size, and what their top rep earns. If they cannot answer three or more of these questions with specifics, the offer is either very new, poorly run, or actively misrepresented.

The Question That Reveals Everything
Rep
"Can you tell me what your current close rate is and what your top rep made last month?"
Operator (Red Flag Response)
"Well, it really depends on the rep. Our top reps are making really good money. We've had people do really well here. It's basically unlimited if you're good."
No close rate. No actual number. Deflects to rep quality. This is not an answer — it is avoidance. The rep should probe twice more. If still no specifics, walk away.
Operator (Green Flag Response)
"Sure. Current close rate is 14% on warm inbound. Our top closer made $17,200 last month. She ran about 18 calls. Average deal is $5,500 with a 10% commission structure."
Specific. Traceable. This operator knows their business. You can now evaluate whether the math works for you.
