Following Up After the Loom

Timing, tone, and how many times. The follow-up is where most reps either quit too early or go so hard they burn the relationship. Here's the protocol.

Why Most People Don't Reply Immediately

People who are interested but didn't reply usually have one of three things happening: they got busy and it slipped, they watched your Loom but wanted to "get back to it later" and forgot, or they need a reason to feel like it's worth their time to engage.

A follow-up gives them permission to re-engage without the awkwardness of "sorry I missed this." It also signals persistence — which, in the sales world, is a quality they're specifically looking for.

The tone of your follow-up IS your sample of how you'd handle prospect objections on their calls. If you follow up desperately, you're demonstrating exactly what you'll do when a prospect ghosts after a call. They see this. Follow up like a confident closer, not a needy applicant.

The Follow-Up Sequence — 3 Touches Maximum

Touch 1 — Loom reminder
2-3 days after Loom
"Just wanted to make sure this didn't get buried — [Loom link]. Around 60 seconds. No pressure on timing."
Short, light, non-needy. Just making it easy for them to engage if they want to.
Touch 2 — Add value or update
5-7 days after Touch 1
"I know you're busy — just wanted to follow up one more time. I've been looking at what [company] is doing with [specific angle] and think there's a real opportunity to accelerate [outcome they're going after]. If there's a window for a quick call, I'd love to hear what you're building."
This follow-up adds something — a specific observation about their business. It signals you're still paying attention and that you've done your homework.
Touch 3 — The graceful exit
5-7 days after Touch 2
"I'll stop reaching out after this — I don't want to crowd your inbox. If the timing ever works, I'd love to connect. I think we'd work well together."
The graceful exit often generates a reply. It's confident, it's final, and it respects their time. If they don't reply to this, let it go and move on.

What Kills the Follow-Up

Needy follow-up (kills it)Confident follow-up (works)
"Hey, just wanted to check in for the third time — did you get a chance to watch my video?""Just wanted to make sure this didn't get buried."
Apologizing for following upNo apology — it's a normal and professional thing to do
Sending the 5th or 6th follow-up with increasing desperation3 touches max, then graceful release
Making them feel guilty for not respondingGiving them an easy on-ramp back into the conversation
Following up on multiple channels simultaneouslyStaying in the channel where you connected initially

When to Move On

After 3 touches with no reply: move on. Not because they're a bad person — because they're not the right fit right now. There are dozens of offers on the board. The time you spend chasing one non-responder is time away from finding an offer that's actually ready to move.

Keep the door open. Leave them on a positive note. Markets are small. The hiring manager who doesn't respond today might be a decision maker on a future offer you want to join. How you exit matters as much as how you entered.
