The Call Review System — What You're Building

You showed your review of the James call. You're already doing this right. The color-coded transcript is one of the most high-level self-coaching tools that exists — it forces you to see your own patterns in writing, not just feel them on the call.

Your Color System (Refined)
You already had a solid instinct here. The one thing to add: put the legend at the top of every document so it's always clear what each color means. Your current system:
Green
Good. Kept this. Strong moment, right framing, solid language.
Blue
Uncertain. Not sure if this was right — flag it, review it, ask about it.
Red
Bad. This hurt the call. Would not repeat.
ROE
Rewrite. What I would have said instead. This is the most valuable column — this is the rep you're becoming.
The Habit to Fix First
You flagged it yourself — ums, arms, over-explaining. This is the most common bad habit for setters transitioning to higher-level work. Cut the fluff. Everything that isn't moving the call forward is fluff.

Instead of: "Oh, that's so great because of this, this, this, and this, and I can't believe blah blah blah…"
Try: "Okay. What's your goal?"

Shorter responses. More space. Let them fill it.
How to Review Properly
Listen to the recording while reading the transcript simultaneously — don't try to do the review from memory. The transcript alone misses tone. The recording alone misses the words. Together, you catch everything.

JJ will send you a Loom review of the Jay Brown call. Watch it with your own review open side by side — compare what you flagged versus what he flags. The gap between those two reviews is exactly what you need to work on.
