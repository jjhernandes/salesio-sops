The Role

"The setter is the first impression of Sales.io. You are not booking appointments. You are qualifying human beings and deciding who gets access to JJ's time."

— JJ Hernandez
1
Your job is qualification, not volume
Booking unqualified leads wastes the closer's time and kills the team's energy. A bad book is worse than no book. Every appointment you set should be someone who genuinely has a shot at becoming a student.
2
You are on commission only
This is a performance role. Your income is directly tied to results. There is no base. The upside is significant for people who execute. The floor is zero for people who don't.
3
You own the pipeline
Every lead you touch must be logged in Close CRM correctly and immediately. Stages must be accurate. Notes must be clean. If it isn't in Close, it didn't happen.

2-way split ($7,500 total) — commission paid on full deal value after first payment clears

Non-Negotiables

These are not suggestions. Breaking these is grounds for losing the role.

1
Every lead goes into Close CRM immediately
Name, number, email, source, and a note on the conversation. Not at the end of the day. Right when the conversation happens.
2
Never promise a price or outcome you can't guarantee
You can say "our program is $7,500 all-in." You cannot say "you'll definitely get placed in 30 days." Let the closer handle the full pitch. Your job is to get them on the call curious and qualified.
3
No booking unless they pass the baseline qualification
They need to have clear intent to work in sales, be coachable, and have access to $7,500 or be open to financing. If none of that is true — do not book them. Disqualify gracefully and move on.
4
Respond to inbound leads within 5 minutes during working hours
Speed to lead is everything. The longer you wait, the colder they get. If you can't respond within 5 minutes, flag it immediately to the team.
5
Do not pitch. Qualify.
Your job is to ask questions, build a small amount of curiosity, and confirm fit. The moment you start explaining the program in detail, you're doing the closer's job badly. Get them to the call. That's it.
6
Communicate bad news fast
If a lead cancels, no-shows, or goes cold — update Close CRM and Slack the team immediately. Don't sit on it. Everyone adjusts their day based on the pipeline being accurate.
What Good Looks Like
Not This
"Hey! I just wanted to reach out and see if you had any questions about Sales.io? We have a 12-week program and it includes 1-on-1 coaching and a job board and..."
This
"Hey [name] — quick question. Are you currently in sales or looking to break in? I want to make sure this conversation is actually worth your time before we go any further."
Not This
Booking someone because they seemed "interested." They said they were curious but couldn't answer what offer they're on, what they're currently making, or whether they're serious.
This
Disqualifying someone politely because they're not ready — and logging the note so the team can follow up in 60 days when their situation changes.
Not This
Close CRM note: "Spoke with John. He seems interested. Will follow up."
This
Close CRM note: "John — currently on $15K/mo in B2B SaaS. Wants to hit $30K. Has tried NEPQ, says it didn't stick. Budget confirmed — looking at PIF or 2-way split. Booked Thursday 3pm EST."

Disqualifiers

If any of these apply, do not book. Log the conversation and move on.

Signal What It Means What to Do
"I just want to learn more first" Not ready. Window shopping. Send to Skool community ($39/mo). Log and tag for 60-day follow-up.
Can't answer what offer they want to work No direction. Not serious yet. Ask clarifying questions. If still unclear, disqualify.
Refuses to discuss money / "price is a problem" Budget isn't there or not committed. Ask if they're open to financing. If hard no — not yet.
Already enrolled in a competing program Unlikely to buy. Not your target. Wish them well. Log the note. Check back in 3 months.
"I need to think about it" before the call Not bought in. Will likely no-show. Don't book. Follow up in a week. Re-qualify before rebooking.
Hostile or disrespectful in DMs Not a culture fit for Sales.io. Do not book. Flag to JJ if severe.
The Standard
Only book who you'd bet $375 on.
If you wouldn't be confident putting money on them closing — don't put them on the calendar. Every appointment you book is a bet you're making with your time and the team's energy.
