The Consciousness Levels

Where you're operating from determines what's possible on the call. This is the Map of Consciousness applied directly to sales performance. Know your level. Know how to move it.

Why This Applies to Call Performance

The Concept (From S1 Lesson 15)

In Section 1 you studied the Map of Consciousness — a hierarchy of emotional operating states from shame and guilt at the bottom through courage and neutrality in the middle to love and enlightenment at the top. That was the theory. This lesson is the practical application to your sales calls specifically.

Where you are on that map when you dial determines everything — your tone, your patience, your curiosity, your ability to hold silence, your reaction to objections, your close rate.

The practical range for sales performance is narrow: Desire (125) through Neutrality (250). Below desire is apathy and fear. Above neutrality starts to become abstract for daily performance. Your job is to identify which level you're operating from and, when needed, move up one level before the call starts.

The Core Insight

You cannot operate above your current state. A rep running from Fear cannot choose to access Courage on a call just because they want to. The state is operating the rep — the rep is not operating the state. This is why catching and moving your level before the call matters more than any technique you'll ever learn.

The Four Levels You'll Operate From

These are the states most reps cycle through on a daily basis. Learn to identify which one is running you right now.

Desire · 125
Wanting the Close
This is the most common low state in sales. It feels like motivation — "I want to close this deal." But Desire is energetically needy. It has an agenda. The prospect feels that agenda and it makes them guard their decision. Reps running from Desire fill silences, rush pitches, over-explain, and drop prices without being asked. The tell: you feel subtly desperate even when you're performing confidence.
Pride · 175
Needing to Be Right
Pride is better than Desire but still below the floor. Reps in Pride argue instead of listen, get defensive when challenged, and can't let objections land without countering immediately. The tell: you feel a flash of irritation when a prospect questions your credibility or your program. That irritation is Pride. It closes the conversational loop instead of opening it.
Courage · 200
The Floor for High Performance
Courage is the minimum viable state for a sales call. From Courage, you can ask the uncomfortable question, hold the silence, push back respectfully when needed, and not collapse when the prospect pushes back on you. You're not playing it safe. You're not holding back the real question. You say what needs to be said because you're committed to finding the truth of their situation, not just what's comfortable.
Neutrality · 250
The Optimal Operating State
Neutrality is not indifference. It's the state where outcomes don't run you. You care about the person — genuinely — but you're not attached to what they decide. From Neutrality, objections are information rather than threats. Silence is comfortable rather than terrifying. "No" is data rather than rejection. This is where the best closers live. The prospect feels it as unshakeable calm combined with genuine care. It's the most magnetic state you can bring to a call.

How to Move Up a Level in Real Time

Not meditation. Not journaling. Specific actions that shift your operating level before or during a call.

D→C
Desire to Courage
Stop needing and start deciding
When you're in Desire, your focus is on the outcome. Shift it deliberately to the process. Say this out loud: "I am going to ask the questions that need to be asked and let this person tell me the truth." That's a Courage move. You're committing to the right action regardless of the outcome.
Physical anchor: Stand up straight. Take one slow breath. The posture of someone in Courage is different from the posture of someone in Desire. Change the body first.
P→C
Pride to Courage
Get curious about what you're resisting
Pride contracts. Courage opens. When you feel defensiveness rising — toward a prospect who challenges you, toward a question that feels like an attack — the move is to get curious about the defensiveness itself. "Why am I feeling the need to defend this?" Usually it's because you're not confident in your own conviction. Curiosity dissolves Pride.
Ask this: "What would I say if I had nothing to prove?" Then say that.
C→N
Courage to Neutrality
Release the outcome completely
Courage still has a stake in the outcome — it just doesn't hide from it. Neutrality releases the stake. The move: remind yourself that your job is to tell the truth about what this person needs and let them decide. Whether they buy or don't is their decision. Your job is just to make sure they have everything they need to make it well. That release is Neutrality.
Ground phrase: "I am here to serve the truth of this conversation, not to manage the outcome." Say it before you dial. Mean it.

The Reset Moves — Catching Yourself Mid-Call

When You Notice You've Dropped Levels on a Call

You won't always enter a call at Neutrality or Courage. And even when you do, the call can pull you down — a difficult objection, a dismissive tone, a direct challenge. The skill is not starting at Neutrality. It's catching when you've dropped and moving back up while the call is happening.

  • !
    You catch yourself in Desire mid-call. The tell: you're talking too fast, adding unprompted justifications, or internally calculating how close you are to the close. Reset: pause. Ask an open question. The act of asking and genuinely waiting for the answer breaks the Desire pattern and pulls you back into presence.
  • !
    You catch yourself in Pride mid-call. The tell: you're subtly debating, you just countered an objection before you let it fully land, or you feel a need to prove something. Reset: say "tell me more about that" and mean it. Invite them deeper into their objection. The act of genuine invitation dissolves Pride.
  • ✓
    The universal mid-call reset. Slow down your speech by about 20%. Take a slightly longer pause before your next response. Ask one genuinely curious question. These three micro-moves will move you up at least one level from wherever you are. They work because they shift you out of reactivity and into intentionality.

"The rep who knows where they are is already halfway to where they need to be."

— JJ Hernandez

The biggest mistake: Thinking you're at Courage or Neutrality when you're actually at Desire. Desire can wear the costume of confidence. You feel certain, you feel ready — but you need the outcome. Run the pre-call check and answer Question Five honestly. That's how you know.
