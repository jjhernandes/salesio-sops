What He Got Right

The framework Willem wrote is accurate. Identity dictates action, action shapes reality, cognitive dissonance creates anxiety, limiting beliefs are the walls of the box you operate from. All of it is correct. That's not the issue.

Correct
Identity comes before achievement

You can't create a future reality while being the exact person responsible for your current one. That's not motivation talk — it's mechanics. The version of you that earns $25K a month makes different daily decisions than the version of you making $0. Willem understands this conceptually. That's a real foundation.

Correct
The three sources of anxiety are right

Nervous system dysregulation, lack of perceived control, and identity-action misalignment. He mapped these accurately. The third one is the most important for someone at Willem's stage — and he's already watching himself experience it in real time.

Correct
Limiting beliefs are the walls, identity is the area inside

Clean metaphor. Expanding what you believe is possible for yourself expands the territory you can operate in. This is accurate and worth holding onto.

What's Missing

Gap 01
Where do limiting beliefs actually come from?

Willem describes limiting beliefs as the walls. He doesn't describe the builders. Limiting beliefs aren't random — they come from specific experiences, most of them early: rejection, failure that wasn't processed correctly, moments where you decided something about yourself. We call these the Four Wounds.

You can't fully destroy a wall if you don't know why it was built. The work isn't just "push through the anxiety." The work is tracing back to where that belief was formed, understanding it wasn't a fact — it was a decision — and consciously replacing it. That's the level underneath what Willem wrote.

Gap 02
The sales connection is completely absent

Everything Willem wrote is true. None of it was connected to what happens on a call.

Who you are off your calls is who you are on your calls. The anxiety he experiences in life — the analysis paralysis, the identity-action gap — shows up on the call in a specific way: over-thinking objections, hesitating before asking for money, intellectualizing instead of being present. The identity work isn't separate from the sales work. It IS the sales work.

The next step is mapping each personal pattern directly to a call behavior. What does "analysis paralysis Willem" look like at minute 40 of a sales call? What does it cost him?

Gap 03
He's describing the process but hasn't named his specific walls

He wrote about limiting beliefs in the abstract. He hasn't written: "My limiting belief is _____. It was formed when _____. It currently shows up in my life as _____. And it shows up on my calls as _____."

The framework is useless unless it becomes personal. A map without a location marker doesn't tell you where to go.

The Real Trap

He caught himself. The last message he sent — "now I'm realizing I'm really a maniac trying to rationalize everything" — is the most important thing he said. That's not self-deprecation. That's accurate self-diagnosis. And it's the exact pattern to watch.

The trap

Willem is extremely good at building frameworks. He reads a concept, processes it deeply, structures it cleanly, and produces something that looks like mastery. But the framework IS the procrastination. Every hour spent building a better map is an hour not spent walking the terrain.

He's been in Sales.io for less than two weeks and has already written a sophisticated breakdown of identity psychology. That's impressive. It's also a delay tactic — even if it doesn't feel like one. The "manic rationalization" he's laughing about is the same thing that will keep him at $0 for another 6 months if it's not addressed directly.

Understanding is not becoming. You can read every book ever written about swimming and still drown.

What this looks like in practice

He understands cognitive dissonance better than most people coaching in this space. He still hasn't made his first sale. That gap is the whole problem. His intelligence creates the illusion of progress without the movement that produces results. This is the thing to confront — directly, without sugarcoating it.

Direction for June

Focus 01
Stop building the map. Walk.

One rule for June: if you haven't done 10 mock calls on a concept, you don't get to analyze it further. Understanding has to be earned through reps, not reading. Every time Willem feels the urge to write a framework or research a concept, the check is: how many times have I actually done this?

Focus 02
Name your walls specifically

The homework is this: write out your three biggest limiting beliefs — not philosophically, personally. Then trace each one back. When did you first decide that was true about you? What happened? How old were you? The more specific and uncomfortable it is to write, the more important it is.

Then map each belief to a behavior on the call. What does that belief make you do or not do when you're 20 minutes into a call with a hot prospect?

Focus 03
Connect identity to sales — every day

After every mock call and every live call, one question: which version of me was on that call? Was that the $25K Willem or the Willem who's still figuring it out? If it was the second one, what specifically showed up — hesitation on price, losing frame under pressure, over-explaining, backing off when they pushed back?

This is how the identity work becomes practical instead of philosophical.

Focus 04
Volume beats understanding right now

Willem's instinct is to understand before he acts. June needs to flip that. Act until you understand. 20 mock calls in June minimum. Not because volume is the only thing — but because right now, Willem's problem isn't a knowledge gap, it's a reps gap. He's been building the engine for weeks. It's time to drive.

Action Items — This Week

  • Write out your three specific limiting beliefs — personal, not abstract. Send them to JJ before the next call.
  • For each limiting belief: trace it back to the moment it formed. Where were you? How old? What happened?
  • Map each belief to a sales call behavior — what does it make you do or avoid on calls?
  • Book and complete 5 mock calls this week. No framework review until all 5 are done.
  • After each mock call, write one sentence: "The version of me on that call was _____ because _____."
  • Read back what you wrote about identity and anxiety — but this time, fill in every blank with your name and your specific situation. Make it personal, not general.

Watch for this pattern: Willem will feel productive because he's building frameworks and reflecting deeply. That feeling of productivity is not the same as the discomfort of doing the actual reps. If a week passes and he's written three more frameworks but done zero mock calls, the pattern won. Catch it early.
