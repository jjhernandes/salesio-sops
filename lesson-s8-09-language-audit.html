The Language Audit

The words you choose shape the world the prospect experiences on the call. This worksheet runs your current language through the full S8 framework. Pull your actual script and start here.

How to Use This Worksheet

The Process

Pull a recent call transcript, your current script, or your written-out discovery and close flow. Work through each section below. Write your current language in the "Current" fields. Then rewrite it using S8 principles in the "Rewrite" fields.

Do not rush this. The audit is only useful if the current language is your actual language — not what you think you say, but what you actually say on calls.

The goal is not a perfect script. Scripts are for robots. The goal is language that is so internalized that it flows naturally, carries the right framing, and doesn't signal effort. You get there through deliberate rewriting, then deliberate drilling.

After the Audit

What to Do With the Rewrites

The rewritten language is your new starting framework. Don't memorize it word-for-word — internalize the framing. Run it in mock calls with a partner this week. The goal is for the rewritten patterns to feel natural, not recited, within 5-7 practice reps per section.

After your next 3 real calls, come back to this worksheet. Check: are you actually using the rewritten language? Where did the old patterns creep back in? Those are your drill priorities for the following week.

The biggest mistake after an audit: thinking the insight is the work. The insight is 10% of the work. The other 90% is drilling the rewrites until they're automatic. Awareness without repetition doesn't change a call.
