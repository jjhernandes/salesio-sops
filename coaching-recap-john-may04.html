Fernando's Data — What the Numbers Show

JJ walked through Fernando's setter data from the session right before this one. It's a perfect example of how the Anti-Fragility framework exposes bottlenecks. The offer: Alex Eubanks' program at $6,200/year, targeting 18-19 year olds on Instagram.

Setting skill is clearly there — qualify rate from connects is solid. The bottleneck was downstream: the closes weren't happening. Why? 18-year-olds were hopping on closing calls with their parents. The kid was already sold. The parent wasn't. And the closer was running the call for the kid.

The fix is simple: ask the parents "what questions do you have?" Make them certain. Make them feel confident you're legit. The kid is already bought in — close the parent.

Fernando started mid-month and still closed 2 grand with old leads. Had he started from day one with warm leads and fixed the parents-on-call issue, he would have hit $8K. That's the iteration. Fix one bottleneck, and the math completely changes.

The Mistake
  • Running the call for the kid
  • Treating parent as friction, not the decision maker
  • Not adjusting close strategy to the room
The Fix
  • Ask parents: "What questions do you have?"
  • Establish credibility and competence to the parent
  • The kid is sold — close the adult in the room

The lesson for John: data tells you exactly where you're broken. That's why tracking matters. You can't fix what you can't see.
