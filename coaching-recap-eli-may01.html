The Identity Gap

John's presentation at the Mastermind hit on something Eli had already been experiencing without the language for it:

"Closers will hit 20K a month, but their identity doesn't align — they don't believe they deserve that income. And it slips."

John, Mastermind presentation

Eli's honest response: "I didn't feel like I was a 20K a month closer." That's the gap. The income was there, but the belief wasn't wired in. So it slipped.

He's been working on this independently. That's the right move. The identity work is ongoing — it doesn't resolve in one session. What matters is that he can now name what was happening, which means he can actually address it.

The income is a lagging indicator of who you believe yourself to be. Fix the belief first, the income follows and stays.

The Metrics Trap

Eli flagged something important: after the company cuts, he started monitoring his numbers obsessively. Cash collected per book call. Show rate. Close rate. And when he loses a sale, his first thought isn't "I missed helping them" or "I left money on the table" — it's "what does this do to my close rate?"

Wrong Frame

Tracking metrics as a scoreboard to survive by. Every miss creates anxiety about the numbers, not the person or the performance.

Right Frame

Tracking metrics as a diagnostic tool. Numbers tell you where the lever is broken — they don't define your identity or your security.

The cuts created scarcity energy. Eli noticed it, doesn't respond well to it, and is aware enough to call it out. That self-awareness is the first step. The work now is not getting pulled into the team's anxiety — staying in your own lane, focused on skill and conversion, not survival.

Eight people got cut. Twelve closers remain. New closers are fired up. Some veterans are spooked. This is actually an opportunity — if Eli chooses to see it that way.

You, Sabian, and Sarah are the LeBrons on this team. New closers need someone to look up to. That can be you — if you walk with that identity.

The Critical Lever — Lead Routing

Everything else is secondary to this. The company cut 8 people, but if leads aren't routed to the right closers, it doesn't matter if the team is 4 or 12. The volume gets diluted and the best closers still underperform.

Priority Action

Eli is talking to Ravina today about how leads are being routed post-cuts. Get clear answers: How are leads distributed? Has the system changed? Are the best closers getting first access to the best leads?

JJ's framing: if Fatima was the one carrying $200K last month, she's proof that the offer can produce. The question is whether the routing lets Eli and the other LeBrons access the same quality of opportunities. That answer changes everything about what May looks like.
