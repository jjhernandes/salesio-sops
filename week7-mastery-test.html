Cognitive Dissonance & Persuasion

The Hallway · OS of the Mind · Life Script vs Ancestral Script · PCP Model · Five Frames

Question 16 of 30
In the "Persuasion is a Hallway" metaphor, what is on the LEFT wall?
A
Cognitive dissonance — tension between identity and reality
B
Anxiety — the fear of what happens if they don't change, or if they do
C
Agreements and rewards — small yeses that resolve tension
D
Logic — the prospect's rational defense against the decision
Correct. The left wall is Anxiety. The right wall is Cognitive Dissonance. The street (the only path forward) is Agreements and Rewards. Your job is to build the hallway narrow enough that the only comfortable path is the one you've designed.
Not quite. Anxiety is on the left wall. Cognitive Dissonance is on the right wall. The street between them — the only way through — is Agreements and Rewards. You build the hallway; the prospect walks it.

Not quite. PCP stands for Perception, Context, Permission. Set how they see you. Set the frame of the conversation. Give them permission to be honest. Then — and only then — run the dissonance.

Lever Framework & Closer Levers

7-Stage Cascade · Constraint Theory · Closer Funnel · 4 Levers · Your One Thing

Question 21 of 30
The Lever Framework has a 7-stage cascade. One broken stage causes problems in every stage after it. Which stage comes immediately AFTER "Appointment"?
A
Close
B
Offer
C
Revenue
D
Show
Correct. The cascade: Activity → Connection → Appointment → Show → Offer → Close → Revenue. If Show is broken, everything after it suffers — even if Activity, Connection, and Appointment are all healthy.
Not quite. The order is: Activity → Connection → Appointment → Show → Offer → Close → Revenue. A broken Show Rate isn't a closing problem — it's a confirmation and commitment problem. Fixing your close won't change anything there.
Question 22 of 30
The Closer Funnel ends: Scheduled → Show → Offer → Close → Revenue × ___. What fills the blank?
A
Close Rate
B
Offer Rate
C
AOV — Average Order Value
D
Show Rate
Correct. Revenue × AOV (Average Order Value). Same 10 closes at $3,500 = $35K. Same 10 closes at $5,000 = $50K. Same calls. Same close rate. Just a different number.
Not quite. Revenue is multiplied by AOV (Average Order Value). It's the fourth lever and one of the most overlooked. You don't need more calls to increase revenue — sometimes you just need a higher number on the same calls.

Scriptless Selling & On-Call Framework

Subjective Language · Three Probing Moves · Depth Meter (L1–L4) · 3 Rules · 6 Stages

Question 26 of 30
Scriptless Selling teaches that every probe you'll ever need fits into one of three moves. What are the Three Probing Moves?
A
Ask, Listen, Follow Up
B
Qualify, Clarify, Confirm
C
Define It, Quantify It, Extend It
D
Open, Probe, Close
Correct. Move 01 — Define It (what does this word mean to them?), Move 02 — Quantify It (give me a number, timeframe, or frequency), Move 03 — Extend It (what does this mean for you? what happens because of it?). Learn these three and you never need a script again.
Not quite. The Three Probing Moves are: Define It → Quantify It → Extend It. Most reps stop at Define. The real depth — and the real emotional pull — is in Extend.
