Standard Operating Procedure

Follow-Up Email Sequence

5-touch automated follow-up system for Sales.io prospects in Close CRM. Set it up once and never manually chase a lead again.

Owner JJ Hernandez
Used by JJ + Faris
Offer Sales.io Mastermind — $7,500
Sequence 5 emails / 21 days
1

Overview & Rules

⚡
The goal is not to close on email. The goal is to get them back on a call. Every email either gives value, creates urgency, or removes a reason not to reply.
Who this sequence is for
Any prospect who had a call with JJ or the team and did NOT close on the call. Once a call ends without a close, the lead gets enrolled in this sequence same day.
Who it's NOT for
Closed students. Leads who said "not interested" or "remove me." Leads who are still in active conversation — don't automate over a live thread.
Core rules
1. Always personalize the [FIRST NAME] and [SPECIFIC DETAIL] tokens before sending.

2. Stop the sequence the moment a lead replies. Resume human conversation, don't keep sending automated emails.

3. If a lead books a second call, pause the sequence. You can resume if they no-show again.

4. The break-up email (Day 21) is the hardest closer. Do not skip it.
2

Close CRM Setup

ℹ️
Requirement: Close Professional plan or higher. Sequences are not available on Starter.
1
Open Close CRM and navigate to Sequences
In the left sidebar, click Sequences. If you don't see it, you may need to enable it in Settings.
2
Click "Create Sequence"
Name it: Sales.io — Post-Call Follow-Up
3
Add 5 email steps
One step per email below. Set each to the correct delay (Day 0, 2, 5, 10, 21). Use the templates from this SOP as your email body. Toggle "Stop on reply" ON.
4
Set sender to JJ's email address
Emails should come from JJ directly, not a generic team inbox. This builds personal connection. Connect via Settings > Email.
5
Enroll leads after every call that doesn't close
After ending a call, go to the lead's profile in Close, click Add to Sequence, and select Sales.io — Post-Call Follow-Up. Do this same day, every time.

D0

Email 1 — Same-Day Recap

💡
Strategy: Send within 2 hours of the call ending. This is not a pitch. It's a reflection. You're showing them you were actually paying attention — which builds trust and sets you apart from every other program they're looking at.
Day 0 — Same Day Recap + anchor
Subject: Quick recap for you
From: JJ Hernandez <jj@psycho-sales.com> 
Hey [FIRST NAME],

Appreciate you taking the time today.

Here's what I took away from our conversation:

- You're currently at [CURRENT SITUATION — e.g., $8K/month, struggling to break $15K]
- The main thing holding you back is [SPECIFIC BLOCKER — e.g., inconsistent tonality, losing frame late in calls]
- Where you want to be: [THEIR GOAL — e.g., $25K months within 90 days]

That gap is closeable. I've seen it done dozens of times with people at exactly where you're at.

The only question is whether Sales.io is the right vehicle for you to close it.

If you want to keep thinking it through, take your time. I'm not going anywhere.

If you want to move forward, reply and I'll send over the next steps.

— JJ
D2

Email 2 — Day 2 Follow-Up

💡
Strategy: Keep it short. Most follow-ups are too long — they restate everything and beg. This one is calm, confident, and gives them a simple yes/no. The person who needs the sale always loses. Don't be that person.
Day 2 — Follow-Up Confident check-in
Subject: Re: our call
From: JJ Hernandez <jj@psycho-sales.com> 
Hey [FIRST NAME],

Just circling back.

You mentioned [SPECIFIC THING THEY SAID — e.g., "I need to think about the investment"]. That's fair.

What I'll say is this: the reps who get the most out of Sales.io aren't the ones who had no hesitation. They're the ones who made a decision instead of sitting on one.

If there's a specific concern you're working through, just reply and tell me. I'm direct and I'll give you a real answer.

If it's a no, that's fine too — just let me know so I can stop taking up space in your inbox.

— JJ
D5

Email 3 — Day 5 Value Add

💡
Strategy: Give before you ask. This email shares a specific framework or insight tied to what they told you on the call. It shows competence without pitching. It earns the right to follow up twice more.
Day 5 — Value Give value, earn trust
Subject: Something I thought you'd find useful
From: JJ Hernandez <jj@psycho-sales.com> 
Hey [FIRST NAME],

Not following up to push — just something I thought you'd actually want to hear.

You mentioned [THEIR BLOCKER] on our call. Here's the honest truth about why that's happening:

[INSERT SHORT INSIGHT — 3-5 sentences. Example: "Most reps lose frame in the back half of a call because they start optimizing for the close instead of the conversation. The second you start thinking 'how do I get them to say yes,' you've already lost. The reps making $30K+ months aren't more persuasive — they're more present. They're not performing, they're just talking to people."]

That's what we fix in Sales.io. Not by giving you new lines. By changing how you think on the call.

That's it. No ask.

— JJ
D10

Email 4 — Day 10 Bump

💡
Strategy: This is the urgency email. Not fake urgency — real urgency rooted in opportunity cost. Every week they wait is a week of bad calls they can't get back. Keep it tight and direct.
Day 10 — Urgency Real cost of waiting
Subject: Still thinking about this?
From: JJ Hernandez <jj@psycho-sales.com> 
Hey [FIRST NAME],

Quick check-in.

It's been 10 days since we talked. In that time, you've probably been on another 10-15 calls. How are those going?

If the same things are breaking down, that's not bad luck. That's a pattern. And patterns don't fix themselves.

I'm not saying Sales.io is the only answer. But I'll be honest — waiting for the "right time" is how a lot of reps end their year in the same place they started it.

If something specific is holding you back from saying yes, just tell me. I'll give you a straight answer.

If you're just not interested, that's fine too. Just reply and let me know.

— JJ
D21

Email 5 — Day 21 Break-Up

🔥
Strategy: This is the highest-performing email in any sequence. You're removing the pressure by walking away. Most people who haven't responded will respond to this one. Send it, and mean it. If they don't reply, the lead goes to a long-term nurture list — not deleted.
Day 21 — Break-Up Highest reply rate — don't skip this
Subject: Closing the loop
From: JJ Hernandez <jj@psycho-sales.com> 
Hey [FIRST NAME],

I'm going to close the loop on my end.

I've reached out a few times since our call and haven't heard back. That tells me the timing either isn't right or Sales.io isn't the right fit for where you're at — and both of those are completely valid.

I won't keep following up after this.

If something changes down the road — you're ready to invest in your skills, or you just want a second opinion on where you're at — my door is open. You know where to find me.

Either way, I genuinely hope the calls start going the way you want them to.

Good luck.

— JJ
