Top Line

Matt is a closer who can hold a room, do research before calls, and book next steps consistently. That's real. But across all 3 calls, he runs nearly the same pitch regardless of who he's talking to, does almost no real discovery, and redirects objections instead of resolving them. The prospects are doing his job for him — Elliott volunteered 3 minutes of pain before Matt asked a single question. That's not a replicable sales process. That's luck.

Call 1 of 3

Ali Baird — Confido

15-Minute Intro Call
June 25, 2026 · 9 minutes · Outbound cold email → booked intro
6.5

Context

Ali is relatively new to Confido, they've started focusing on marketing only in the last 2 months, and they're at the very beginning of their AEO strategy. A new marketing hire is joining next week who will own this area. Ali isn't the decision maker yet.

What Matt Did Well

  • Good opener: Easy small talk, got location, warm fast. No awkwardness getting into the call.
  • Research done: He'd looked at Confido's site and had a clear understanding of what they do before the call.
  • Booked the next step: Got July 15th at 3pm before the call ended. That's the job.
  • Adjusted to a gatekeeper: Once he understood Ali isn't the buyer, he reasonably pivoted to "let's get the right people on the next call."

What Needs Work

  • Pitched into a blank room. Ali told him they're just starting their AEO journey, barely two months into marketing. That's not a qualified buyer yet. Matt should have asked more questions to understand where they actually are before running the full pitch.
  • Didn't acknowledge the stage they're at. Ali said "we're kind of at the beginning of our AEO journey." Matt's response was to explain why their current in-house approach won't work on LLMs. That's correct information, but it felt like he was talking at her, not with her.
  • The affiliate marketing objection was mishandled. Ali said "So it's like affiliate marketing." Matt said "Not really" and moved on. He should have asked "What makes you see it that way?" — that question tells you exactly what the confusion is and lets you clear it up for real, not just dismiss it.
"So I want to understand what you're doing currently digitally and just some of your game plans and revenue and that kind of stuff, like how are you guys building out revenue, and then see if this would be a fit."
Matt — immediately followed by launching into the pitch before Ali fully answered
The Core Issue

Matt asked one discovery question and then started pitching before Ali was done answering. She gave him a clear opening — "we're kind of just starting" — and he didn't use it. He could have spent 3 minutes understanding her situation and come out with a far more specific next step. Instead he ran the standard deck.

What to Fix

The Three Things That Will Move His Close Rate

1 — Run Real Discovery Before the Pitch

Right now Matt asks one surface question and then presents. The rule: spend at least 5-7 minutes in discovery before touching the pitch. Ask about their current situation, what they've tried, why it didn't work, what they want instead, and who needs to be part of the decision. The pitch should feel like a natural conclusion to what you've already learned — not a separate event.

Before next call: write out 5 discovery questions specific to AEO prospects. Practice running them in sequence until they feel natural, not interrogative.

2 — Adapt the Pitch to What You Found in Discovery

The current pitch is identical on every call. That tells the prospect they're being pitched, not helped. After discovery, the pitch should reference their specific words, their specific pain, and their specific goal. Elliott said he cares about 5 specific publications. The pitch to Elliott should have included a direct answer to that. Enrico is fundraising. The pitch to Enrico should have connected visibility to fundraise credibility specifically.

The stats and guarantees can stay. But every pitch should open with: "Based on what you told me, here's exactly where we focus..."

3 — Resolve Objections, Don't Redirect

When a prospect pushes back, Matt says "yeah, but..." and returns to his pitch. The objection stays alive. The pattern to replace it with: ask what's behind the objection, then address that, then check if it's resolved.

Elliott's "5 publications" concern: "Which 5 are they? [pause] Okay — tell me what happened when you tried to get placed in those before. [pause] Got it. Here's exactly how our approach is different for those specific outlets — and here's what we can guarantee."

Ali's "affiliate marketing" comment: "What makes you see it that way? [pause]" — then address the actual confusion, not the surface statement.

Secondary — Ask About Decision Makers in Minute 3

Standard question, early. "Before we get into the details — who else typically gets involved when you're evaluating something like this?" This changes the entire call if the answer reveals a gatekeeper. It should never be discovered by accident at minute 12.

Secondary — Future Pace the Next Step

When booking the follow-up, don't just say "I'll send the deck." Say: "When we get together on the 6th, I'm going to show your team exactly where Turtl's content gaps are specifically, and then we'll put together a publication strategy built around your 5 target outlets — so they can see exactly what the plan looks like before making any decision." Now they're excited to show up. And they know what they're walking into.

Practice

3 Drills for This Week

Drill 1 — The 5-Question Discovery Run
Write out 5 discovery questions for an AEO/PR prospect. Have a partner play the prospect and answer them realistically. Matt's job: don't pitch. Don't go to the deck. Just ask, listen, and dig deeper on whatever they say. Run for 7 minutes. Debrief: what did you learn that you wouldn't have gotten from your normal pitch call?
Drill 2 — The Objection Resolve (not redirect)
Partner gives Matt an objection: "I'm not sure we'll get into the specific publications our buyers read." Matt cannot say "but" or pivot back to the pitch. He must ask a question that goes deeper into the objection, acknowledge what he hears, and then give a direct answer. Run each objection 3 times until the response is natural.
Drill 3 — The Tailored Pitch
Partner gives Matt a prospect profile: company stage, specific goal, budget signal, prior pain. Matt has to run a pitch that leads with "based on what you told me..." and connects every claim to something specific about that profile. No generic stats as the opener. Run on 3 different prospect types (early-stage, enterprise, post-merger).
