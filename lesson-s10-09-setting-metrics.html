Setting Metrics

Connect rate. Booking rate. Show rate. These three numbers tell you everything about where your setting system is broken — and which lever to pull first.

The 3 Core Setter Metrics

Every setter performance problem traces back to one of three metrics. Connect rate tells you if you're reaching people. Booking rate tells you what happens when you do. Show rate tells you what happens between booking and call.

Fixing the wrong metric is expensive. A setter with a great booking rate but a broken show rate doesn't need better opening lines — they need a better confirmation sequence. Understanding which number is broken tells you exactly what to fix.

Connect Rate
Target: 15-25% of outreach attempts
The percentage of outreach attempts (DMs sent, calls dialed) that result in an actual back-and-forth conversation — not just a reply or a pickup, but genuine engagement. A "yes" to a calendar link from a cold DM doesn't count as a connect if there was no real conversation first.
Booking Rate
Target: 30-50% of real conversations
Of the people you actually have a conversation with, what percentage book a call? This measures the quality of your setting conversation itself — your hook, your qualification, your bridge, your ask. A low booking rate means the conversation isn't working. The opener might be fine but the bridge to a call is breaking down.
Show Rate
Target: 70-85% of booked calls
Of the calls that get booked, what percentage actually show up? This is almost entirely a function of your confirmation system and lead quality. A high booking rate with a low show rate usually means either your confirmation sequence is weak or you're booking people who weren't actually ready for a call.

Connect Rate — What Good Looks Like

What It Measures

Connect rate tells you whether your outreach is reaching the right people through the right channels. If your connect rate is below 10%, the problem isn't your opener — it's who you're reaching out to or how you're finding them.

Connect rate can also be depressed by the wrong timing (cold calling outside business hours), the wrong platform (LinkedIn vs. cold call vs. Instagram), or targeting prospects who are too senior and screen all unknown contacts.

Connect Rate Diagnosis Fix
Below 5% Wrong audience, wrong channel, or wrong time Audit your targeting. Are you reaching people who match the ICP? Is the channel right for this prospect type?
5-15% Reach is okay but opener may be triggering sales guard Review first message on LinkedIn or first 10 seconds on cold call. Is it triggering the "sales call" pattern?
15-25% Healthy connect rate Focus on converting connects to bookings (booking rate).
Above 25% Strong connect rate — possible over-indexing on warm list Check if you're only reaching out to easy contacts. Great connect rate + low booking rate = not qualifying well.

Booking Rate and Show Rate — Diagnosing the Break

Low Booking Rate — What's Breaking

If you're connecting with people but not converting to calls, the problem is inside the conversation itself. Either you're pitching too fast (triggering rejection), not qualifying enough (running out of things to say), not bridging naturally to the call (asking for the calendar too abruptly), or not creating enough genuine interest before the ask.

Fix: Record your conversations. Listen for where the energy shifts. The moment they start pulling back is the moment your conversation broke. Work backwards from there.

Low Show Rate — What's Breaking

If you're booking calls but they're not showing up, the problem is almost never lead quality alone — it's the post-booking system. You booked someone who was excited in the moment but not invested enough to hold the commitment 48 hours later. The confirmation sequence (Lesson 7) is the primary fix for a broken show rate.

Secondary issue: you may be booking people who didn't fully qualify. Someone who books out of politeness, not genuine interest, has a very high no-show rate.

Don't fix the wrong metric. A setter with 20% connect rate, 40% booking rate, and 55% show rate has a show rate problem — not a connect or booking problem. Spending time on opener refinement won't fix a confirmation system that isn't working.

Exchange — Metrics Review Coaching Session

COACHING CALL — Jalen Diagnosing a Setter's Numbers
Coach (Jalen)
"Walk me through your last two weeks. How many outreach attempts, how many real conversations, how many books, how many shows?"
Setter
"I sent about 120 DMs on LinkedIn, got maybe 18 replies that actually went somewhere, booked 9 calls, and 4 showed up."
120 outreach. 18 connects (15%). 9 books (50% of connects). 4 shows (44% of books). Connect and booking rates are solid. Show rate is broken.
Coach (Jalen)
"Your connect rate and booking rate are actually solid. 15% connect, 50% booking — you're setting the call well. The issue is entirely post-booking. What does your confirmation sequence look like right now?"
Setter
"I send them the calendar link and then I remind them the day before."
Coach (Jalen)
"That's the problem. You have two touchpoints — you need three, and none of them should be generic. The immediate message after booking needs to reference what they told you. The 24-hour message needs to anchor the cost of not showing up. The day-of message needs to give them a reschedule path. You're not losing these people because the set was bad. You're losing them because you went cold after the book."
The metrics diagnosed the problem precisely. No guessing. The fix is specific: improve the confirmation sequence, not the opener or the hook.
