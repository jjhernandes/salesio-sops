Your North Star Doc — What JJ Saw

You built the Google Sheet from the Week 1 modules and shared it live. JJ's reaction: "This is fucking great. This is actually really solid." You understood the intent. One thing needed sharpening.

What you had right
North Star: "Be a top closer on their offer."
Goal: Consistent closing at 40%+ close rate.
This is exactly the right format — specific, measurable, tied to a role.
What JJ fixed — how to use bottlenecks correctly
Each milestone has its own 3 bottlenecks — not a general list for everything. Bottlenecks should be specific enough that when you solve one, you cross it off and move to the next. They shouldn't be vague. They should be actionable and completable.

Example: Not "need more training" — but "discuss with JJ what metrics and levers to pull to hit 40% close rate." That's one conversation. When it's done, it's done. Cross it off.

The goal is 5 clear steps. Nothing complicated. Nothing uncertain. If you can't see the next step clearly, that's the bottleneck — not the thing you thought was the problem.
"I get you so clear that there's no way you fail. The reason people fail is they're not sure where to start. They're just confused. I want you to have 5 steps — that's it."
