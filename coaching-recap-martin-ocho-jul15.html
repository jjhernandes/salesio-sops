Main Coaching Moment

The Map of Consciousness, Applied to Sales

Concept
Levels of consciousness sales reps actually operate from
Fear
Frozen state — "I'm so afraid I can't move." Anxious, avoidant, doesn't act throughout the day.
Desire
Comparison-driven — needing what someone else has ("I need to beat James, I need what he has").
Anger
JJ's take: not as bad as it sounds — anger creates movement, unlike desire, which fixates on taking what someone else has instead of finding your own.
Pride
The one JJ said he actively avoids being around — "it's my way," "I'm right, you're wrong." Seen it turn students who hit $20–30K/month arrogant.
Courage
Where real steps get taken — JJ's read on where Martin actually is: you wouldn't have made the leap into this if you were still in fear or pride.
Reason
Most people are naturally capable of operating here — what blocks it is unprocessed emotion getting in the way, not lack of logic.
Love
JJ's own operating level, by his account — no competition, no need to "beat" anyone, including other coaches who frame him as competition.

Second Coaching Moment

Finding the Root — the "Why" Practice

Concept
Every reaction is rooted in something specific — find it instead of sitting in it
Rule
Resistance = pain. Anger = feeling let down. Confusion = missing information. Reactivity (outside of positive moments) never shows up without pain underneath it.
Practice
The moment something feels off, ask "why" immediately rather than letting it linger unexamined. Example JJ gave from his own life: catching jealousy and admitting directly what it's actually about (someone having something you want) instead of letting it sit as vague discomfort.
Two outcomes
Either it stings because it's true and you get emotional finding it, or it's simple and obvious once named. No in-between — the discomfort of asking is the same either way.
Shortcut
"If you were giving advice to yourself, or someone else in the exact same scenario, what would you say?" JJ used this on someone else the day before the call — it cut straight past the hesitation to the answer she already knew.
