The key point on "failure envisioned"

Most people hear "failure envisioned" and think: make $0, get nothing done. That's wrong. If you move with initiation, use your resources, and hit a breaking point — you will know exactly where it broke. You can't get nowhere if you actually go. Post one YouTube video: no data, no feedback. Post 100: you know where the gap is. Same applies here. Failure should still be forward motion.

Why a brand new offer is risky for you right now

If someone is doing $0 and you're their setter, your earning opportunity is basically zero. A role attached to an established offer — one doing $200K-$300K/month — gives you a real vehicle to earn while you learn. That's not the only consideration, but it's a big one. Don't trade a live runway for a blank slate just because the offer sounds exciting.

Setter vs. Closer — Getting Clear

You want to be a closer. Starting as a setter is an accelerant, not a detour.

Most people underestimate how correlated the two roles are. The way Sales.io teaches setting is not "check the boxes and book the call." It's opening their mind to the concepts, understanding what the prospect values, building genuine curiosity. That is the exact same skill you need closing. If you start setting and do it the right way, you will be a materially better closer for having done it. And your training continues either way — you don't set aside the closer track to take a setting role.
