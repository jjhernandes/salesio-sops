Call SOP — Arun Singh

The Process

ATX Global  ·  Government-Funded Investments  ·  Use on every call.

The One Rule

Target: 30-35 minutes max. Your buyers are busy professionals. They came inbound — they're already interested. Your job is to qualify, build trust, show the deal, and book the next step. Not to educate them for an hour. When they say yes, stop selling.

Phase 1 — Open 2 min

Set the frame

"Good to meet you. Quick intro — I'm Arun, senior investment consultant at ATX Global. We specialise in government-funded property investments — supported living, social housing, elderly care. I've invested my own capital in this space, so I know it well. This call is just discovery — I want to understand your situation, and if what we have makes sense for you, I'll show you. If not, no problem. Sound good?"

Then stop talking. Let them go first. Do not launch into the company pitch or the £174M stat yet. Save those for when they carry weight.

Phase 2 — Discovery 10-15 min

Ask in this order. Each question should flow from the last. Don't skip ahead to budget before you understand their pain — the numbers land harder when the pain is established first.

  1. 1
    Current situation
    What are you invested in right now? Buy-to-lets, commercial, something else?
  2. 2
    What's not working
    What made you look at alternatives? What's the frustration with what you have now?
  3. 3
    Press on the pain
    How long has that been going on? What's it actually costing you — in money, in time, in stress?

    This is where most calls go shallow. Stay here longer than feels comfortable. The pain drives the decision.
  4. 4
    What the ideal looks like
    If this investment did exactly what you needed it to do, what would that look like? Cash flow, hands-off, security — what's the priority?
  5. 5
    Budget and structure
    In terms of the capital you're looking to allocate — what are we working with? And are you thinking cash, or is mortgage/refinance part of the plan?
  6. 6
    Timeline
    Is this something you're ready to move on in the next few weeks, or are you still in research mode?
  7. 7
    Long-term vision
    What's the end goal here — retirement income, something to pass on to children, just replacing what the buy-to-lets aren't giving you?
When they go on tangents

Let them talk for 60-90 seconds. Then pull it back: "That's exactly the pattern I see — let me show you why this solves that." Don't let them run the discovery phase.

Phase 3 — Transition to the Deal 1 min

The bridge

"Based on what you've told me, I'm confident we can help. Let me show you something specific that I think fits what you're looking for."

Do not go through the full deck. Skip the company overview slides. They came inbound — they know who you are. Go straight to the deal. The numbers will do more in 5 minutes than the presentation does in 20.

Phase 4 — Present the Deal 8-10 min

  1. 1
    The asset
    What it is, where it is, who developed it. Keep it to 3 sentences.
  2. 2
    The yield
    What they get from day one. Show the monthly and annual number. Then show year 5 with the CPI uplift.
  3. 3
    Address the price gap upfront — don't wait for them to raise it
    "One thing I want to be upfront about: these sell at investment value, not vacant market value. There's a gap of roughly £X. Here's why that's not actually the issue — [yield recovery timeline, stamp duty contribution, 30% buyback option]."

    Say it first. Own it. Move on.
  4. 4
    The refinance scenario (if relevant)
    Only show this if they've indicated they want to pull capital out later. Show the numbers — what they put in, what they pull out, what the net monthly is after the mortgage. Make it real.
  5. 5
    Completely hands-off
    One sentence: "No voids, no repairs, no agency fees, no calls. The HA covers everything including the boiler."

Phase 5 — Risk Handling Only if raised

Do not bring up the HA going bust unless they ask. You're volunteering a 5-minute objection before it exists. If they ask, handle it. If they don't, move on.

When they ask "what if the housing association goes bust?"

"Great question — and it's the biggest one people have. The short answer: legislation requires another organisation to step in. It's happened multiple times — 2007, 2012, 2024. No investor lost their cash flow in any of those cases. The government has a legal obligation to house these people, and the cost of not doing so is billions a year. That protection isn't going away."

When they ask about government policy risk / change of legislation

"That's the real risk — and I'll be honest about it. If a government removed the NHS tomorrow, this model would be affected. Is that likely? No. But it exists as a risk, and you should weigh it. What I'd ask is: compared to Section 21 being removed, Renters' Rights, 12-month evictions — which risk feels more real to you right now?"

Phase 6 — Close the Call 3 min

Watch for the buy signal. When you hear it — stop selling and book the next step.

Buy signals — stop and book when you hear these
  • "That sounds like what I'm looking for"
  • "Send me everything you have"
  • "How do I move forward?"
  • "That makes sense"
  • "I don't see any issues with this"
  • They start asking about timelines and next steps unprompted
Close — booking the next call

"Perfect. What I'll do is send you the full deal pack — financials, floorplans, HA accounts, and the investment brief. Have a proper read through it. Then let's get a second call in to go through any questions you have and look at the numbers in detail. Does [day] or [day] work for you?"

Always leave with

A booked call in the calendar and a WhatsApp sent before the day is out. If you didn't get a booked call, send the email and follow up with a WhatsApp the same day. Don't let it go cold overnight.

Hard Rules

  • Never run past 35 minutes unless they're actively keeping you there with specific questions
  • Never show the full deck — skip company overview, go straight to the deal
  • Never explain the HA going bust risk before they ask
  • Never let a prospect monologue for more than 90 seconds without pulling the frame back
  • Never move to budget before you've established what's wrong with their current situation
  • Never end a call without a booked next step or a clear agreed timeline for the email follow-up
