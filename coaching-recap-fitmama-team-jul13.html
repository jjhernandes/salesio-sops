Topics Covered

What was discussed

01
KPI framework — BAMs and Reaches explained
This is the first week running a real KPI structure for the FitMama team. JJ introduced the BAM/Reach model: BAM (bare-ass minimum) is the floor you commit to no matter what. Reach is what you're selfishly chasing.
Setters track sets (shows). Closers track cash collected — because closers control that lever directly. Eventually, the team can forecast cash collected from setter sets, but not yet.
The number you put in writing is the number you're accountable to. A team with no goals is a team with no results.
02
Setter attribution — what counts as a set
Micah and Stacy raised a legitimate question: with the new VSL funnel auto-booking qualified leads directly onto Jess's calendar, what counts as the setter's set?
JJ's rule: Any call where the setter had a touchpoint — a real conversation — is their set. If a lead comes through the VSL and auto-books with zero setter contact, that's a marketing set, not a setter set.
Khalil's framing: If VSL books 6 calls for the day, the question isn't "do we stop?" — it's "why can't we book 12?" VSL auto-books don't reduce setter targets. They're parallel tracks.
Future state: If the VSL performs as intended, Khalil will shift ad spend fully to it and redirect setter focus from DM outreach to calling missed VSL opportunities — people who started the application but didn't finish, or qualified leads who didn't book.
03
SendBlue — seamless handoff plan
Team sentiment: SendBlue is working. iMessage appearance helps response rates. The personal touch from the closer in the thread is effective. Automations were going "crazy" at one point but have since settled. Stacy uses it regularly; Micah finds the Jazz impersonation for non-applicants is driving fill rates. Jess got invited to SendBlue during the call — needs to log in and review.
JJ's plan — the seamless handoff: Build a prerecorded voice or video message from Khalil that bridges him to Jess before the call. The goal is to transfer Khalil's authority to Jess so prospects arrive primed — not surprised they're talking to someone else.
The model: Iman used this with JJ. He'd send a message introducing JJ as his right-hand, sometimes including JJ's Instagram. Prospects would check, see it was real, and show up with a different level of respect and readiness.
Expected impact: Show rate minimum 2x. Prospects arrive treating Jess as the authority, not as a gatekeeper. Reduces the need to "fight for respect" at the start of every call.
Watch for: Don't over-set the expectation that they're talking to Khalil directly. The bridge message should transfer authority, not create confusion about who's on the call.
JJ to send Khalil a few variations of handoff messages. Khalil plans to implement the same day.
04
Wednesday — Jalen demos the triaging script
Micah asked for the triaging/dialing script so setters can start calling missed VSL opportunities right away. JJ will send the script before Wednesday, but more importantly — Jalen is coming in on Wednesday to demo the process live.
Jalen has been JJ's setter since one of his earliest offers. He'll walk the team through what the triaging process looks like in practice — not just theory.
Wednesday is also the first check-in point on KPIs — if anyone's not on track, that's the moment to surface what they need.
