Your 4 Levers
01
Show Rate
Are your scheduled calls actually happening?
If Low
What to fix
Pre-call sequence — are you reaching out before the call? A message the day before and the morning of dramatically changes show rates. Make it personal, not automated.
Confirmation — did they actually confirm, or did you just assume? Get a real response, not just a calendar hold.
Relationship in the set — if you inherited the lead from a setter, how warm was the handoff? A cold handoff produces cold shows. The relationship has to carry over.
02
Offer Rate
Are you making an offer on every qualified call?
If Low
What to fix
Confidence — if you're hesitating before the offer, they feel it. The hesitation kills the frame before a word comes out. The offer has to come from a place of certainty.
Frame — are you positioned as the expert recommending a solution, or are you asking for permission? Frame determines everything. You don't ask if they want to hear an offer. You transition into it.
Over-qualifying — if you're self-selecting out before you make the offer, you're closing yourself out of deals. Make the offer. Let them tell you no. Don't make that decision for them.
03
Close Rate
Are your offers converting?
If Low
What to fix
Discovery depth — most closers think they have a closing problem. They don't. They have a discovery problem. If the prospect isn't bought in emotionally before the offer, the close was already lost.
Emotional urgency — did you build enough gap between where they are and where they want to be? If the pain isn't real enough, the investment won't feel necessary.
Objection handling — are you handling objections or caving to them? An objection is a question in disguise. Understand what they're actually asking before you respond.
04
AOV — Average Order Value
Are you leaving money on the table?
If Low
What to fix
Defaulting before they ask — if you're presenting the lower option first or assuming they can't afford the higher one, you're making the pricing decision for them. Present the full value, let them decide.
Framing the investment — how you present price determines how they receive it. Anchor to what it's worth, not what it costs.

The Most Important Thing in This Lesson
Most closers think they have a closing problem. Usually it's a discovery problem.
If you're hitting the offer and getting consistent nos — go back to your discovery. Ask yourself: did they feel the gap? Did they describe their pain in their own words? Was there a real emotional reason to change? If any of those answers are no, you lost the deal before you made the offer.
The Rule
Pull your numbers: show rate, offer rate, close rate, AOV. Find the one that's lowest relative to where it should be. That's your lever for Phase 2. Don't split your focus — go deep on the one thing that, if fixed, changes your monthly number the most.
Find Your Lever — 3 Steps
01
Pull your last 30 days: calls scheduled, calls shown, offers made, deals closed, total revenue. Calculate each conversion rate.
02
Find where the biggest drop is. That's the lever. If you're offering but not closing — it's discovery. If you're showing but not offering — it's frame and confidence. Follow the number.
03
Commit to it. Write down your one lever and your target number by end of Phase 2. Bring both to your next 1-on-1.
