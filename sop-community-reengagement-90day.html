Why Communities Go Quiet

A quiet community is not a people problem. It's a structure problem. People don't engage when there's no reason to show up, no one to show up for, and no cost to staying silent. Fix the structure and the people follow.

What kills it

Broadcast-only content. One person talking, everyone watching. No prompts, no accountability, no stakes. Members feel like subscribers, not participants.

What builds it

Rituals. Recurring moments people can anchor to. Daily questions, weekly wins, challenges with a finish line. Community is a habit, not an event.

The real issue

When JJ is quiet, the community is quiet. The founder sets the temperature. If you're not posting, not DMing, not showing up, neither are they.

The fix

Consistency over intensity. One intentional post per day beats a launch-day flood. Show up every day for 90 days — the community will mirror you.

2

Core Principles

Principle 01

Identity over information. Posts that make people reflect on who they are get more replies than posts that teach something. "What do you believe about yourself that's keeping you stuck?" will outperform a framework post every time.

Principle 02

Surface wins relentlessly. Nothing activates a quiet community faster than visible proof that other members are winning. One win post creates 10 "I want that" moments. Make wins public, specific, and frequent.

Principle 03

DMs are the real community. Every public post JJ makes should be backed by 5-10 personal DMs to specific members. Public content creates the signal. DMs create the relationship. The combination is unstoppable.

Principle 04

Give them a reason to come back tomorrow. Every post should end with a hook forward — a challenge, a question to answer, a result to report back on. "Tell me how this goes" creates return visits.

Principle 05

Peer-to-peer beats top-down. JJ can't personally engage with everyone. Structure the community so members engage with each other. Breakout rooms, accountability pairs, student spotlights — systems that create connection horizontally, not just vertically.

Month 1 — Foundation (June)

Theme

Reactivation. You're reigniting the fire, not starting a new one. The goal is to break the silence, establish daily presence, and remind every member why they paid to be here.

Week 1 — The Wake-UpBreak the silence
Mon
The Announcement
JJ posts across all platforms: "The next 90 days of this community are going to look different. I'm showing up every day. Here's what's coming." Make it specific — challenges, spotlights, live Q&As. This is a statement of intent, not a hype post.
Tue
The Honest Question
Post in Circle and WhatsApp: "Be honest — where are you actually at right now? Not where you want to be. Where you are." Give a 3-option reply format: on track / stuck / starting over. No judgment, just data. Watch who replies.
Wed
DM Sweep
Personally DM every member who hasn't posted in 14+ days. Use the "I noticed you" DM template. Don't broadcast — personalize. Reference something specific from their profile or a past conversation. This week the goal is 15 personal DMs minimum.
Thu
Voice Note Drop
Record a 60-second voice note in WhatsApp — not a text, a voice note. JJ's voice, raw and direct. "I've been thinking about where you all are at. Here's what I want you to do this week." This is the highest-converting thing you can do in WhatsApp. Nobody else does it.
Fri
First Win Spotlight
Reach out to 3 students who have had a recent win (pull from Close CRM or ask coaches). Post a proper win spotlight across Skool and Circle. Full story — where they started, what changed, what they did. Tag them. This starts the win culture.
Week 2 — The CommitmentGet people to declare publicly
Mon
Public Commitment Thread
Launch a thread in Skool and Circle: "Post your #1 commitment for June. One specific, measurable thing. I'll be checking in on this at the end of the month." Public commitments have 3x the follow-through rate of private ones. Make them say it out loud.
Tue
LinkedIn Social Proof Drop
Post on LinkedIn about the community: real results, real names (with permission), real numbers. Not a sales post. A proof post. "A student I've been coaching for 6 weeks just [specific result]." Drive curiosity about what Sales.io actually does.
Wed
YouTube Drop
Upload a coaching clip (from a group call or 1-on-1 with permission) and post in Skool with the prompt: "This is what we work on in Sales.io. Watch this and tell me: which part of your calls does this most apply to?"
Thu
Challenge Launch
Announce the first challenge: the 5-Day Mock Call Challenge. One mock call per day, posted in the designated channel, with one thing they're working on and one thing they improved. Starts Monday. Create urgency: "The reps who do this will have more reps in a week than most people get in a month."
Fri
Reply to Everything
Every post, every comment from the week — JJ replies personally. Just this week, go all-in on responsiveness. This signals to the community that when you post, JJ actually sees it. Changes behavior immediately.
Week 3 — The Challenge5-Day Mock Call Challenge
Mon–Fri
5-Day Mock Call Challenge is live
Daily accountability in WhatsApp and Circle. JJ checks in each morning: "Who's posting their call today?" and each evening: "What was your best moment from today's call?" DM anyone who goes silent mid-challenge. The ones who complete it get a personal shoutout from JJ on Friday.
Mid-week
Content from the Challenge
Film a short reaction to one of the mock call summaries (anonymized or with permission). Post to YouTube Shorts and Instagram Reels. This turns community activity into content and shows prospects what it's like inside Sales.io.
Fri
Challenge Recap
Post the results: who completed it, what they noticed, what changed. Make it feel like a milestone. Even if 3 people completed it, celebrate those 3 loudly. The next challenge will have more participants.
Week 4 — The Identity PostGo deeper — make it personal
Mon
JJ Vulnerability Post
JJ posts something real about his own journey — a specific moment he failed, doubted himself, or had to make a hard call. Not inspirational fluff. A real story with a real low point. This breaks the "guru on a pedestal" dynamic and invites the community to open up too.
Tue
Identity Question Thread
Post in Circle: "One question. Who are you being on your calls right now — and who do you need to be?" Let it breathe. Don't give the answer. The most interesting communities are the ones where members teach each other.
Thu
Month 1 Check-In
DM everyone who posted their commitment in Week 2. Check in personally: "You said [their specific commitment]. Where are you at?" Report back to the group publicly with how the month went overall. Close the loop — this is what builds trust.

Month 2 — Momentum (July)

Theme

Now that the community is active, deepen the structures. Introduce accountability pairs, peer coaching, and content from inside the community itself. The goal is to reduce dependence on JJ for every post and build horizontal connection between members.

Week 5–6 — Accountability PairsPeer-to-peer structure
Launch
Pair Every Member
Match members into accountability pairs — ideally similar level and goal. Pairs check in with each other twice a week (Wednesday and Sunday) in their own DM thread. Give them a template: "This week I committed to ___. I did / didn't do it because ___. This week I'm committing to ___." JJ checks in on pairs monthly, not weekly.
Ongoing
Hot Seat Thread
Weekly in Circle: one member volunteers to be in the "hot seat." They post their biggest current challenge in detail. The community coaches them for 48 hours. JJ adds his take at the end. This creates engagement, teaches everyone, and makes the volunteer feel genuinely supported.
Week 7–8 — The Student Spotlight SeriesSocial proof engine
Weekly
One Student, One Story
Every week, pick one student and go deep on their story. Not a testimonial — a transformation story. Where were they 30 days ago? What did they believe about themselves? What changed? What specific result happened? Publish on LinkedIn and Skool. Cross-post to YouTube Shorts. These become your best lead gen content.
LinkedIn
Tag and Amplify
When you post the spotlight on LinkedIn, tag the student. Ask them to share their own version of the story in their own words. This creates organic reach into their network, which is likely other sales reps who are exactly your ICP.
Week 9 — Live EventHigh-energy group moment
Event
Live Community Q&A / Game Tape Session
Schedule a live call specifically for the community — not a regular coaching call. "Bring your worst call from this month. We're going to pick it apart." Record it. Post the highlights to YouTube. This creates a moment the whole community rallies around and gives you 3-4 pieces of content from a single session.
DM
Pre-Event Hype
DM 20 members personally the week before: "I want you to bring something to this call. What's the one moment from a recent call you wish had gone differently?" This pre-sells attendance and gives you content before the event even happens.
Week 10 — The Challenge Round 2Bigger, better, more visible
Launch
The Objection Gauntlet
A 7-day challenge. Each day a different objection is posted in WhatsApp and Circle (timing, money, need to think, partner, already has a coach, etc.). Members post their handle of the objection as a voice note or short video. Top response each day gets a shoutout from JJ. This is competitive, specific, and directly builds the skill.

Month 3 — Identity (August)

Theme

The deepest work. By now the community has rhythm and trust. Month 3 is where you go into identity, belief systems, and what it actually means to be a Sales.io closer. This content converts prospects and retains students simultaneously.

Week 11–12 — The Four Wounds SeriesDeep identity content
Series
One Wound Per Week
Over 4 weeks, JJ breaks down each of the Four Wounds in a long-form post — what it is, how it shows up in sales specifically, and what breaking it looks like. Post the full version in Circle and Skool. Post a condensed version on LinkedIn. After each post, ask: "Which of these do you see in yourself?" The replies will be the most engaged posts in 90 days.
Week 13 — The 90-Day RecapClose the loop, set the next chapter
Recap
Results Post Across All Platforms
Document what the community did in 90 days: wins, challenges completed, mock calls logged, commitments kept. Specific numbers where possible. Post it everywhere. This is your proof that the community is alive and working — and it becomes your best lead gen asset for Q4.
DM
Personal Note to Every Active Member
Send every member who engaged meaningfully in 90 days a personal DM: what you noticed about their growth, one thing they should focus on next. This takes time. It's worth every minute. It's the thing they'll screenshot and remember for years.
Preview
Set Up Q4
Announce what's coming in Q4 — quarterly mastermind, new curriculum, new offers. Give the community something to stay for. The worst time to think about retention is when someone's already left.

Platform Playbooks

🎓
Skool
Home base — modules, recordings, community feed
  • Pin one post every Monday: "This week's focus is ___. Here's the one thing to apply by Friday." Keep it short, specific, actionable.
  • Win of the Week thread — pinned every Friday, replaced every week. Anyone can post a win, big or small. JJ reacts to every single one.
  • Monthly "Confessions" thread — anonymous option. "What's the one thing you're still struggling with that you haven't said out loud?" Creates safety and surfaces real coaching needs.
  • Attach a question prompt to every course module: "After watching this, what's one belief you need to change?" Forces engagement with the content itself.
  • Leaderboard for course completion — visible to all members. People are competitive. Make progress visible.
  • Replay of the Week post — tag a specific call recording with a coaching question attached. Don't just post the link. Give them something to look for.
💼
LinkedIn
Personal brand + social proof engine + inbound leads
  • 3-4 posts per week. Mix: identity takes (1), student stories (1), controversial sales opinion (1), personal story (1). Not selling on every post.
  • Weekly "Sales.io Student Spotlight" — short format. Name, where they started, one specific result. Always ends with: "This is what we do inside Sales.io."
  • Hot take posts that spark debate: "Scripts are why you're stuck." "NEPQ is dead." "The best closers don't try to close." These drive comments from people who disagree — which is reach.
  • Post a question to community members publicly and tag them: "I asked my community this question. Here are the answers." Shows the community is alive and thinking.
  • DM every person who comments something thoughtful on a post. Don't pitch. Just reply genuinely. Engagement creates connection which creates inbound.
  • Once a month: post behind-the-scenes of a coaching call (no personal info). "This is the exact conversation we have in Sales.io coaching."
▶️
YouTube
Primary inbound driver + evergreen community content
  • Source video topics directly from the community. Post in Circle weekly: "What do you want me to break down on YouTube this week?" Then make exactly that video and tag them when it drops.
  • "Community Questions Answered" monthly video — compile the best questions from Circle and Skool, answer them on camera. This makes community members feel seen and creates curiosity for prospects watching.
  • Student transformation videos — structured interviews: where were you, what changed, what specifically improved. 10-15 minutes. These become the best sales tools you have.
  • Game tape reaction series — react to anonymous call recordings submitted by members (with permission). Educational for the community, addictive content for the channel.
  • Always end community-sourced videos with: "This came from inside Sales.io. If you want in, the link is in the description." Soft CTA that converts.
  • YouTube Shorts from coaching moments — pull 60-second clips from group calls. Hook in the first 3 seconds: "This is the moment most sales reps lose the frame."
📘
Facebook
Retargeting warm leads + alumni + extended network
  • Cross-post LinkedIn content 24-48 hours later. Don't write separate content — repurpose. Facebook audience and LinkedIn audience rarely overlap.
  • Facebook Group (if exists) should have a weekly question post pinned every Monday. One specific question, simple to answer, low barrier to engage.
  • Facebook Live once a month — unscripted Q&A. Announce it 48 hours in advance. 20-30 minutes max. These build trust fast with people who haven't pulled the trigger yet.
  • Use Facebook to retarget people who interacted with past posts but never converted — run a simple retargeting ad to the YouTube channel or Skool community page during active enrollment periods.
  • Post student win graphics (simple, branded, name + result) — these get shared more than any other content type on Facebook.
💬
WhatsApp
Daily pulse — highest open rate, most personal
  • Voice notes over text, always. A 45-second voice note from JJ will get more replies than any text post. It's personal, it's human, nobody else is doing it. Make this a non-negotiable habit.
  • Monday voice note: "Here's what I want you focused on this week. One thing. Not five things. Tell me yours." Ask for a reply.
  • Wednesday mid-week check: "How's the week going? Real answer. DM me if you need me." Short. Not a lecture.
  • Thursday accountability push: "It's Thursday. If you've been avoiding something this week — you know what it is. Do it today." No fluff, no motivation speech. Just a direct push.
  • Sunday wind-down: "What was your biggest win this week? Drop it here." The replies become the Monday win shoutout, which creates a loop.
  • Use WhatsApp for rapid challenge check-ins during challenge weeks. Quick polls ("who posted their call today?") and instant accountability before it slips.
⭕
Circle
Deep engagement hub — JJ's fastest response channel
  • Daily Ask-JJ thread — one post per day where any member can ask JJ one question. JJ answers every one within 24 hours. This is the highest-value habit in the community. Members will check Circle just to see the daily answer.
  • Weekly "Framework of the Week" — JJ breaks down one Sales.io concept in detail, then asks: "How have you seen this show up in your calls this week?" The replies become coaching content.
  • Monthly "Identity Check-In" — post the same 3 questions every month: What belief about yourself has changed this month? What belief is still holding you back? What's one thing you did this month that the old version of you wouldn't have done? Track the evolution over 90 days.
  • Hot Seat thread every week (see Month 2). This is Circle's signature format — member-driven, community-coached, JJ closes it out.
  • "Commit and Report" channel — members post a commitment on Sunday evening, then report back Friday. Public accountability, low effort to set up, high impact.
  • JJ commits to responding to every post on the first day of each month — "Super Response Day." This creates a month-start ritual that pulls people back in.

Prompt Library

How to use

Pull from this bank when you need a quick post. Rotate through them across platforms. These are designed to trigger replies, not just views.

Identity Prompts

"Who are you being on your calls right now?"

"What's one thing you believe about yourself that is costing you sales?"

"Describe the version of you that closes $30K months. What does that person do differently on a Tuesday morning?"

"What's the one story you keep telling yourself that isn't true?"

Sales Psychology Prompts

"What's the last objection you heard that you didn't know how to handle — and what do you think was really going on?"

"When did you last lose frame on a call? What triggered it?"

"What does your prospect feel in the first 5 minutes of your call? How do you know?"

"What's the difference between a rep who closes 30% and one who closes 60%? One answer only."

Accountability Prompts

"What did you commit to this week that you haven't done yet? Why?"

"Rate your calls this week 1-10. What would make it a 10 next week?"

"What's the one thing you've been avoiding? Say it out loud."

"If your results this week were permanent — is that okay with you?"

Engagement Sparks

"Controversial take: [hot take]. Agree or disagree?"

"The best advice I got about sales that nobody talks about: _____. What's yours?"

"Post your #1 close from this month. Full story. I want to hear it."

"What's one thing you learned in Sales.io that changed how you think — not just on calls, but in life?"

One rule: Never end a post with a statement. Always end with a question or a directive. "Let me know in the comments" is weak. "Drop your answer below — I read every one" is better. "What's yours?" is best. Open loops pull people in. Closed statements push them away.
