Before the Call

Prep Checklist

⏱ 5–10 min before

Do this before every first call. Walking in prepared is what separates a coach from a professional.

Review their Client Intake form — know their goals, health history, and injuries before they say a word
Review their Movement Assessment — note any limitations or areas to address in Phase 1
Write down their top 2–3 goals from the intake so you can reference them by name on the call
Have the Client Hub link ready to share on screen
Have your Zoom link sent to them in advance (at least 1 hour before)
Be in a quiet, well-lit space — you are setting the tone for the entire program
Show up 2 minutes early — never make a new client wait
💡 If they haven't filled out the Intake or Movement Assessment yet, the first call should start with those. Don't proceed without them — those forms are the foundation of everything you'll build.
01
Minutes 0–5

Open Warm

⏱ 5 minutes

The first 5 minutes set the entire energy of the relationship. Be real, be warm, be present. This is not a corporate onboarding — this is a conversation between two people.

Greet them like you mean it
Use their name. Smile. Ask something real — not "how are you?" but something that shows you already know a little about them from their intake.
Break the ice with something personal
Reference something specific from their intake — their goal, their challenge, something they wrote that stood out. This immediately signals: "I read what you sent. I'm paying attention to you."
Example Opening
"Hey [Name], so good to finally talk to you. I went through everything you filled out and honestly I'm really excited for what we're going to do together. Before we dive in — how are you feeling about starting?"
Let them talk first — don't rush to your agenda
Give them space to share how they're feeling about starting the program. Most people have nerves. Let them express it. The most important thing in the first 5 minutes is that they feel heard.
Set the tone for the relationship
Tell them how you like to work — direct, honest, no hand-holding, but fully in their corner. Tell them you're going to push them AND support them. People need to know what they're getting into.
Setting the Tone
"I want you to know — I'm going to be honest with you, always. If something's not working, we change it. If you're not showing up, I'm going to tell you. And if you're crushing it, I'll be the first person celebrating you. That's how I work."
02
Minutes 5–15

Understand Their Real WHY

⏱ 10 minutes

The intake tells you what they want. This section uncovers why they actually want it. The "why" is what you'll use on the days they want to quit.

📌 The rule: Keep asking "why" until you get to something emotional. "I want to lose 20 pounds" is a goal. "I want to feel confident in photos of my kids" is a why. You need the why.
Ask about their primary goal — then dig one layer deeper
They'll give you the surface answer. Go past it. "And why does that matter to you?" — ask it twice if needed. The real answer is almost never the first one.
Walk me through what a typical day looks like for you right now — food, movement, energy.
What's the #1 thing that's gotten in the way of your goals before?
What have you tried that didn't work — and what do you think went wrong?
If 12 weeks from now everything goes perfectly — what does that look like? How do you feel?
Is there anyone in your life this would impact besides you?
Write down what they say — in their words
Take notes during this section. These exact phrases are what you'll come back to when they're struggling in Week 6 and want to give up.
Reflect it back to them before moving on
Before you go into the program overview, summarize what you just heard in their language. This is the moment they feel truly understood — and that's the foundation of trust.
Reflecting Back
"So what I'm hearing is — it's not just about the weight. It's about feeling like yourself again. Feeling proud when you look in the mirror. That's what we're actually working toward. And that's exactly what we're going to build."
03
Minutes 15–30

Walk Through the 12 Weeks

⏱ 15 minutes

Paint the full picture of what the next 3 months look like. Make it feel real and specific — not a generic overview, but a vision tailored to what they just told you they want.

Phase 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1–4)
This month is about building the foundation — understanding how their body moves, establishing habits, and getting the baseline right. Nothing fancy yet. Just building the system that everything else sits on top of.
How to Explain Phase 1
"The first 4 weeks are going to feel manageable — on purpose. We're not going to throw everything at you at once. We're building habits, figuring out how your body responds, and getting the foundation locked in. By Week 4, you should already feel a shift."
Phase 2 — Build (Weeks 5–8)
Month 2 is where the work intensifies. Training gets harder, nutrition gets more precise. This is also usually where people hit their first real wall — and where having someone in their corner becomes everything.
Phase 3 — Integration (Weeks 9–12)
Month 3 is about making everything stick. By now the habits are theirs. The goal is that by Week 12, they don't feel like they're "on a program" — they feel like this is just how they live.
Tie each phase back to their WHY
As you describe each phase, connect it to what they told you in Section 02. "Remember what you said about wanting to feel proud in photos? By Phase 3, that's what we're building toward." Make it personal.
💡 Don't get into the weeds of specific exercises or exact calorie counts right now. That comes in the program. This conversation is about the emotional arc — what they'll feel at each stage, not what they'll do.
04
Minutes 30–40

Set Clear Expectations

⏱ 10 minutes

This is where you establish the rules of the relationship. Be direct. Unclear expectations are the #1 reason coaching relationships fall apart.

Weekly Check-In — every Sunday, no exceptions
They need to submit their weekly check-in every Sunday. Tell them you will text them if they miss it. This is non-negotiable — it's the data you use to coach them effectively.
Habit Tracker — daily
The habit tracker is their daily accountability tool. It takes 2 minutes. Make it clear: missing a day is fine. Missing a week means the program isn't working the way it should.
Strategy Sessions — every 2 weeks, 45–60 minutes
You meet every two weeks on Zoom for a real conversation — not a check-in, but a full deep dive. Tell them to come prepared: what worked, what didn't, and one specific thing they want help with.
WhatsApp access — open, but respectful
They have direct access to you on WhatsApp for questions, wins, and off-plan moments. Set the expectation: you respond within 24 hours during the week. Emergencies get faster. Let them know this line is real — they should actually use it.
Honesty goes both ways
You're going to be honest with them. You need them to be honest with you. If they're struggling, you need to know. You can't coach what you can't see.
How to Say It
"The one thing that kills coaching programs is when communication breaks down. I'm going to be in your corner 100% — but I need you to show up too. Fill out your check-in. Reach out when something's off. The more you put in, the more I can give you."
⚠️ Watch for this: If a client responds to expectations with "sure, yeah, no problem" without engaging — they might be agreeing to avoid conflict. Ask them directly: "Is there anything in what I just said that feels unrealistic for your life right now?" Give them space to be honest early.
05
Minutes 40–47

Walk Through the Hub

⏱ 7 minutes

Share your screen and walk them through the Client Hub live. Don't just send the link — show them exactly where things are so they have zero confusion.

Share your screen and open the hub
Open anafit-hub.html on your screen. Walk them through the quick access section — show them each document and when they use it.
Point out the week-by-week roadmap
Scroll down to the 12-week section. Show them exactly where they are right now (Week 1) and what they'll be doing each week. Make it visual — the roadmap takes away the uncertainty.
Click through the Intake form live if they haven't done it
If they haven't filled it out — do it together on the call right now. It takes 10 minutes and you get everything you need to build their program immediately.
Send them the hub link in WhatsApp before the call ends
Don't wait. Send it while you're still on the call so they have it immediately. Tell them to save it to their phone's home screen.
Walking Through the Hub
"This is your home base for everything. Every form, every tracker, every piece of the program is right here. You don't need to remember anything — just come back to this link. I'm going to send it to you on WhatsApp right now."
06
Minutes 47–50

Close the Call Strong

⏱ 3–5 minutes

The last 3 minutes should leave them feeling clear, excited, and fully committed. Don't let the call die — close it intentionally.

Confirm their #1 next action
Make it crystal clear what they're doing today. "After this call, you're going to: open the hub, fill out the Movement Assessment if you haven't, and start the Habit Tracker today." Specific action, not vague direction.
Confirm the date of the next strategy session
Book the Week 2 strategy session before you hang up. Don't leave it as "we'll figure it out." Have the date locked before the call ends.
Send them off with genuine energy
End on a high note. Tell them you're excited. Tell them you believe in what's possible for them. Make the last thing they feel before hanging up be: "She is in my corner."
Closing the Call
"Okay — I'm genuinely excited for this. Everything you told me today, that's exactly what we're going to work with. Your job right now: open the hub, finish the Movement Assessment, and start your Habit Tracker today. I'll be checking in Sunday. And I'm serious — if anything comes up before then, message me. That's what I'm here for."
07
Within 24 Hours

After the Call

⏱ Same day

What you do in the 24 hours after the first call sets the standard for the entire relationship. Don't drop the ball here.

Send a WhatsApp follow-up within 2 hours — confirm you're excited and reference something specific they said on the call
Send the Client Hub link again in WhatsApp so it's easy to find
Review their completed Intake and Movement Assessment forms — read every word
Begin building their Phase 1 program (deliver it before or at the Week 2 session)
Add the Week 2 strategy session to your calendar and send a calendar invite or WhatsApp reminder
If they haven't started the Habit Tracker by Day 2 — send a gentle reminder
Follow-Up WhatsApp Message
"Hey [Name]! So great connecting with you today. I'm already thinking about your program and I'm excited about what we're going to build together. Remember — open up the hub and get that Movement Assessment done today if you haven't. And your Habit Tracker starts NOW 🌿 See you Sunday! — Ana"
✅ The standard: A client should feel more confident and more committed after the first call than before it. If they leave feeling confused, overwhelmed, or uncertain — revisit this SOP and figure out which section was missed.
