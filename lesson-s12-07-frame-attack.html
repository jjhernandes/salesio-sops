Frame Attack Drill

Stress-testing your frame under consecutive pressure. Not an argument drill. A state drill. You win when you stay grounded and curious — not when you "win" the exchange.

What the Frame Attack Drill Is

One partner fires consecutive challenges — objections, credibility attacks, pressure statements — one after another with no pause. The other partner holds state: grounded, curious, unhurried. No scripts. No canned rebuttals. Just state management under fire.

The goal is not to have the perfect answer. The goal is to not break. To stay in your body. To respond from curiosity instead of defense. To maintain the same tone at challenge 5 that you had at challenge 1.

Why This Drill Exists

Most reps can hold frame for one objection. Maybe two. By challenge three, they're rushing, defending, getting louder, or completely conceding. The Frame Attack Drill isolates state management from everything else on a call. No need to track where you are in the conversation, no prospect relationship to manage — just consecutive pressure and your response to it.

Five rounds a week builds a different rep. One who doesn't flinch at the fourth objection on a real call because they've trained at level 5 in the drill.

5 Levels of Escalating Difficulty

Level 1
Soft Resistance
Attacker uses gentle pushback. "I'm not sure this is for me." "I've been thinking about whether I'm ready." "The price seems high." Three challenges, low intensity, no personal edge. Goal: establish the baseline of staying grounded and curious on mild resistance. Most reps pass Level 1 immediately.
Level 2
Consecutive Objections
Five consecutive objections with no validation between them. Price, timing, skepticism about results, comparison to a competitor, another price. No breaks. Attacker doesn't pause or wait for a complete answer — moves to next challenge after 8-10 seconds. Tests whether the rep speeds up or stays measured across a sustained run of resistance.
Level 3
Credibility Attacks
"How long have you been doing this?" "Have you actually been a sales rep or just a coach?" "My last coach told me the exact same thing and it didn't work." "Why should I believe your results are real?" Personal credibility is attacked. The rep must respond without becoming defensive, without listing credentials, and without apologizing. Staying curious here is genuinely difficult.
Level 4
Personal and Emotional
Attacker uses language designed to trigger ego: "This feels like a high-pressure sales tactic." "You sound like every other coach I've talked to." "I don't think you actually understand my situation." "I don't think this is going to work for me." These hit differently than price objections. They're designed to make the rep feel unseen or disrespected. The trained response is the same: slow down, get curious, don't defend.
Level 5
Full Gauntlet
Eight consecutive challenges mixing all four types: soft resistance, consecutive objections, credibility attacks, and personal/emotional hits. No pattern. The rep cannot predict what comes next. Duration: 3-4 minutes of continuous pressure. If the rep can complete Level 5 with consistent tone, no defensive acceleration, and at least one genuine curiosity response per challenge — they're ready for anything a real call can produce.

What Success and Failure Look Like

Frame Breaking — What Failure Sounds Like
Attacker
"Your results sound like every other coaching program I've heard about. I've done three of these and gotten nothing."
Rep
"No no, I totally get that — but we're really different, we actually have results to back this up, like I can show you testimonials, our method is completely different from anything else out there, JJ has worked with multiple eight-figure companies and our students consistently get results, plus we have a guarantee so there's literally no risk..."
Pace accelerated. Volume increased. Defensive list of credentials. Introduced "guarantee" unprompted. Zero curiosity about what those other experiences were like. Frame fully broken.
Frame Held — What Success Sounds Like
Attacker
"Your results sound like every other coaching program I've heard about. I've done three of these and gotten nothing."
Rep
[2-second pause] "Three programs. That's a real pattern. Tell me what those were like — what did each one promise you'd get, and what actually happened?"
Same pace. Same tone. Didn't defend. Got curious. Now the attacker has to explain their actual experience — which surfaces the real objection. Frame intact.
Attacker
"They all taught the same scripts. Generic stuff. Nothing connected to how I actually talk."
Rep
"That's exactly what this isn't. And I hear that skepticism — three programs is enough to earn it. What would have to be true about this for it to feel different to you?"
Acknowledged the skepticism without validating the conclusion. Invited them to define what would work. Curiosity sustained through pressure. Level 3 drill passed.

Running the Drill and Reviewing It

Setup

Two reps. Record every session. The attacker fires from the level chosen — no easing in, no warmup. Start at the agreed level immediately. The defender does not know what's coming next. Duration: 3-5 minutes per round. Switch roles. Both people need both sides of this drill.

Reviewing the Drill on Tape

After the session, review the recording together. Listen for: pace changes, volume changes, defensive language, filler words that appeared under pressure, the moment curiosity gave way to arguing. The reviewer gives one observation per challenge — specific, timestamped, behavioral. The defender commits to one change before the next session.

  • ✓
    Success = staying grounded and curious, not winning the argument. If you "won" by out-arguing the attacker, you failed the drill. The metric is state, not outcome.
  • ✓
    Frequency: twice weekly for reps with a frame ceiling. Once you consistently pass Level 4 without breaking, move to weekly maintenance.
  • ✗
    Failure signals: defensive speed increase, volume change, conceding before being asked, introducing unprompted justifications. Any of these mean the frame broke. That's what to fix next session.
  • ✗
    Don't debate about the "right" answer mid-drill. There is no right answer in the Frame Attack Drill. There is only state. Stay in state. That's the only metric.
