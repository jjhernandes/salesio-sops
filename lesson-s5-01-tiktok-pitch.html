The TikTok Pitch

Only hit the 3-4 things that matter to them. Everything else is noise. The pitch should feel like a mirror, not a menu.

What the TikTok Pitch Is

The Definition

The TikTok Pitch is a 2-5 minute pitch that covers a maximum of 3-4 points — all of them pulled directly from what the prospect said in discovery. That's it. Nothing else goes in.

The name matters. TikTok trained a generation to tune out anything that doesn't grab them immediately and stay relevant. Your prospect's attention works the same way on a sales call. If you haven't hit something they care about in the first 30 seconds of pitching, they're already somewhere else in their head.

The TikTok Pitch is not a shorter version of your full pitch. It's a completely different approach to what a pitch is. A traditional pitch covers the offer. The TikTok Pitch covers the prospect. Same product — entirely different lens.

The Core Rule

If the prospect didn't mention it in discovery, it doesn't go in the pitch. Not because the feature isn't valuable — but because value is personal. What's compelling to one person is irrelevant noise to another. You don't pitch features. You pitch their situation back at them.

Why Shorter Pitches Close More

This is counterintuitive. Most reps believe covering more ground builds more confidence. The opposite is true.

01
Reason
Specificity Signals Understanding
When you hit exactly what they said, they feel heard. That feeling — "this person gets it" — is more persuasive than any feature list. A short, precise pitch proves you were paying attention. A long pitch proves you have a lot to say about your offer.
02
Reason
Attention Is Finite
A prospect can genuinely absorb 3-4 things in a 5-minute window. After that, cognitive load kicks in and nothing sticks. The more you add, the less any single thing lands. You're not creating impact by covering more — you're diluting the impact of everything.
03
Reason
Shorter Creates Curiosity
When you stop at the right moment, prospects ask questions. Questions are buying signals. A pitch that answers everything before they ask leaves nothing for them to lean into. You want them pulling information out of you, not absorbing a download.
04
The Risk
Longer Pitches Introduce New Objections
Every new feature you mention is an opportunity for the prospect to find something they don't need, don't want, or disagree with. A tight pitch eliminates this surface area. The more you say, the more there is to object to.
Rep pitches the job board, the community, the mock calls, the 1-on-1 coaching, the B2B track, the WhatsApp access, the quarterly event, and the guarantee. Prospect latches onto the quarterly event: "I travel a lot, I probably couldn't make those." Now you're defending a feature you didn't need to mention.

How to Select the 3-4 Things

The Selection Criteria

Go back through everything the prospect said in discovery. You're looking for: what they said they wanted most, what they said hurt the most, and what they said they had tried before that didn't work. Those three categories map directly to your pitch points.

The 3-4 things are not the 3-4 most impressive features of the program. They are the 3-4 things that directly address what this specific person said in the last 20-30 minutes.

Discovery Signal What Goes in the Pitch
"I've been doing this for 6 months and I feel like I plateau'd at $8k/month" 1-on-1 coaching that identifies exactly where the ceiling is and breaks through it
"I'm using a script and I hate it, I feel fake on calls" Scriptless selling framework — natural conversation, their own words
"I don't have a good offer to work on right now" The job board — 60-80+ vetted companies hiring weekly, guaranteed placement
"I feel like I lose momentum on long calls and they go nowhere" Call structure and psychology framework — how to guide a conversation without losing the thread

What Happens When You Pitch 8 Things

Nothing sticks. That's not a guess — it's how memory works. When you present 8 items of roughly equal emphasis, the brain treats them as a list. Lists are not memorable. And what isn't memorable cannot drive a decision.

The prospect leaves the call able to say "it seemed like a lot was included" but unable to articulate why any of it mattered to them personally. That's not a closed deal waiting to happen. That's a "let me think about it" that never converts.

The feature dump is a confidence problem in disguise. Reps who dump features are subconsciously hoping that something in the list will land. That's a scattershot approach. It telegraphs that you don't know what matters to this specific person — which means you weren't really listening during discovery. Prospects feel this. It breaks trust exactly when trust is most important.

"The pitch should feel like a mirror, not a menu."

— JJ Hernandez

Call Exchange — Bloated vs. Tight

VERSION 1 — Bloated Pitch (8 Things)
Rep
"Okay so let me walk you through what's included. You get weekly 1-on-1 coaching, the seven group calls every week — Mock Call Monday, Setting Mastery, Coffee Chat, Sales Psychology, B2B Strategy, Inner Circle, and Call Review — plus access to our WhatsApp community, our Skool platform with all the modules, our proprietary job board with 60 to 80 vetted companies hiring every week, quarterly in-person events in Orlando, 1-on-1 Slack access to coaches, and a full money-back ROI guarantee. So pretty comprehensive, right?"
Eight features listed in a single breath. No connection to anything the prospect said. They're now tracking a list, not feeling understood.
Prospect
"Yeah that sounds like a lot... what's the price?"
Classic deflection. "That sounds like a lot" is not excitement. They need processing time because none of it connected to their specific situation.
VERSION 2 — The TikTok Pitch (3 Things, All From Discovery)
Rep
"Based on what you told me, there are three things that are actually going to move the needle for you. First — the 1-on-1 coaching. You mentioned you've been at $8k for six months and you don't know why. That's exactly what the 1-on-1 is for. We find the ceiling and break it, specific to how you operate on calls."
One thing. Tied directly to "I plateau'd at $8k/month." They recognize their own words.
Rep
"Second — you said you hate using a script and you feel fake. We don't teach scripts. We teach you how to have a real conversation guided by genuine curiosity. No memorizing lines. Your calls start feeling like you instead of a robot."
Second thing. Tied to "I feel fake on calls." Again — their words back to them.
Rep
"And third — the job board. You said you're not on a strong offer right now. We have 60 to 80 vetted companies posting every week and we guarantee placement. That problem gets solved in your first 30 days."
Third thing. Tied to "I don't have a good offer." The pitch is done. Three points. Under 90 seconds. Everything connected.
Prospect
"Okay, yeah — that actually sounds exactly like what I need. What does enrollment look like?"
Buying signal. They're pulling information forward. The pitch worked because it felt personal — because it was.
