Pattern Interrupting Stalls Before They Happen

Most reps wait for stalls to arrive, then try to recover. Elite reps see them coming and change the direction before the stall has time to form.

The Premise

Stalls are predictable. "I need to think about it," "let me talk to my spouse," "can you send me something?" — every rep has heard these so many times they should be able to prevent them. But most reps don't. They wait for the stall, then scramble.

Pattern interrupting is the practice of redirecting the conversation before the stall takes shape. Not by rushing to close, but by addressing the underlying condition that was going to create the stall in the first place.

The stall is a symptom. The pattern interrupt treats the cause.

What Creates Stalls

Stalls come from three places:

1. Uncertainty — they're not sure the program works or works for them specifically. They need more certainty before they can decide.

2. Identity misalignment — some part of them doesn't see themselves as "someone who does this." The stall is them retreating to a familiar identity.

3. Permission — they feel like they need someone else's buy-in (spouse, partner, mentor) before they can feel okay making the decision.

If you address all three of these during discovery and pitch — before the close — the stall either doesn't appear or appears in a weakened form that's easy to handle.

Pattern Interrupt Seeding — Lines to Plant Early

Seed these during the call — in discovery, during pitch, or at transition. Not at the close. By the time you're closing, these should already be in place.

Seed 1 — Certainty interrupt (use mid-pitch)
"I want to be upfront — a lot of people come into this call wanting to make sure it works before they commit. I'd rather you not join if you're not certain. So as we go through this, tell me what you're not seeing yet."
Why it works: Names their uncertainty, gives them permission to voice it now instead of at the close, positions you as the opposite of pushy.
Seed 2 — Identity interrupt (use after discovery)
"Can I ask you something? You mentioned you're serious about this. When you imagine the version of you who's actually at $20k a month — is there any part of you that still isn't sure that person is you?"
Why it works: Surfaces the identity doubt early. They address it now instead of it festering into a "let me think about it."
Seed 3 — Spouse/partner interrupt (use in discovery if they mention a partner)
"Is this something your [spouse/partner] knows you're looking at? I ask because decisions like this are better made together, and I want to make sure whoever matters to you is at least in the loop."
Why it works: Surfaces the spouse objection early while you can address it, instead of it appearing as a stall at the end of the call.
Seed 4 — Decision pattern interrupt (use before price)
"Before I show you the investment — I want to ask: when you make decisions like this, do you generally know pretty quickly, or do you need time to process?"
Why it works: Gets them to name their decision pattern. If they say "I need time," you can explore what that means. If they say "I generally know quickly," you can hold them to that.

The Pattern Interrupt in Action

Rep who didn't seed — standard stall at close
RepSo the investment is $7,500. What do you think?
ProspectI love it. I just want to think about it and talk to my wife and maybe get back to you next week.
RepOf course, totally makes sense. I'll follow up Monday!
Three stalls hit at once. Rep never seeded. Call is essentially over.
Rep who seeded — stalls addressed before they formed
Rep[Early in call] Is your wife aware you're looking at this? I ask because these decisions are better with both people understanding what's happening.
ProspectYeah she knows I'm on a call about sales training. She's supportive but probably wants to know more.
RepMakes sense. If she had one concern, what do you think it would be?
ProspectProbably just if it's actually going to work.
RepThat's fair. Let's make sure we actually address that today so you're not carrying that uncertainty into a decision.
Spouse concern surfaced and addressed in discovery. Can't become a close-time stall now.

Timing Is Everything

Pattern interrupt seeds must go in early — not right before the close. If you try to pattern-interrupt at the close, it reads as manipulation. It needs to happen during discovery or pitch, when the context is exploratory and you're genuinely trying to help them make a clear decision.

The goal is never to prevent the prospect from thinking or involving their partner. The goal is to do that work now, together, on this call — so they're not doing it alone in an information vacuum after you hang up.
