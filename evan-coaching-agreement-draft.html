Before You Sign

What to Lock In Before This Goes Live

⚠
WhatsApp access needs defined hours. "1-on-1 access" with no boundary is a fast path to coach burnout and resentment. Lock in weekdays, 24-hour response — that's it. Ascended students who need faster responses get scheduled calls.
⚠
Module quantity is 6-8, not 10-15. Committing to 10-15 up front creates the incentive to build padded content just to hit a number. Build the core 6-8 right, then expand if students need more. You approve every module before it goes live.
⚠
Sales calls need a clear handoff from Lu. Define whose leads go to Evan vs. Lu before day one. Overlap creates conflict. Evan gets overflow inbounds or a specific lead source — not open access to the whole pipeline.

Terms

90-Day Review Clause

At 90 Days, JJ and Evan Assess Together

This is not a performance improvement conversation — it's a built-in checkpoint to make sure the structure is working for both sides. If deliverables are being hit and students are getting results, the relationship continues and expands. If something slipped, you fix it before it becomes a bigger issue.

□
Calls delivered consistently. Were 2 group calls and 2 call reviews happening every week? Any consistent gaps?
□
Course modules built. How many modules completed and approved? Are they actually being used by students?
□
Student outcomes. Are students in Evan's calls improving measurably — close rates, placements, job offers? Ask the students, not just Evan.
□
Ascension pipeline. Did any students ascend? If not — is that because the program is too new, or because students aren't seeing enough value to invest further?
□
Sales call performance (if applicable). What was his close rate? How did students respond to his calls vs. the existing team?

If all five are solid — expand the role. If two or more slipped — renegotiate scope before renewing. This protects JJ from a fixed cost that underdelivers and gives Evan a clear target to hit.
