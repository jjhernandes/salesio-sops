SwayyEm — Hannah's Playbook

Old Lead Re-Engagement Cadence

5-Email Sequence Over 18 Days  ·  Phone Layered by Lead Age  ·  Full Script Bank

What This Is

Matt is giving you ~44 HubSpot leads who had a prior conversation 30–120 days ago and didn't convert. These are warm-but-dormant — not cold. Your job is to re-engage them and get them back on a call. Not to pitch. Not to inform. Just to reopen the conversation.

The backbone is a 5-email sequence over 18 days. Phone is layered on top based on lead age. Stop the sequence the moment they reply — any reply. Every email has a distinct angle. None of them say "just checking in." None of them say "hope this finds you well." Every one of them gives the lead a reason to care right now.

Target Reply Rate
15–25%
vs. 5–10% on old "checking in" sequences
Best Performing Emails
Email 5, then 1
Permission close + personalized audit drive the most replies

Before you dial or send anything: Pull up their HubSpot record. Read Fathom notes from prior calls if they exist. Read any email thread. Know what they discussed with Matt, what their pain was, and why they went cold. Use the info — it's what makes Email 1 land.

The 5-Email Sequence — Full Copy

Under 150 words per email. One CTA per email. Each one is independent — if they respond to Email 3 without seeing 1 or 2, it still lands. Stop the moment they reply. Do not send the next touch to someone who already responded.

Subject:

  • [Competitor name] keeps showing up. You don't.
  • Where [Their company] ranks in AI today
Goal:Land with something personalized. Make them see their specific gap.

Goal: get them talking. Don't pitch on this call. Find out where they're at and get them to a call.

Live Answer Script

Voicemail — First Voicemail (Any Track, Day 0)
Under 20 seconds

"Hey [Name], this is Hannah from SwayyEm — I work with Matt. I was actually running your category in AI search this morning and found something specific to your company I wanted to flag. Give me a call back at [number] or I'll send it over in an email. Thanks."

Do NOT explain what you found. Curiosity gets callbacks. Information doesn't.

Voicemail — Second Voicemail (Track A only, Day 7)
Slightly more specific — reference the gap directly

"Hey [Name], Hannah from SwayyEm again. I've been sending over some info on the AI search gap for [Their company] — I think the Day 90 guarantee we just launched changes the math pretty significantly from when you last spoke with Matt. Two voicemail rules: Never leave two voicemails back-to-back on consecutive days. And never explain what you found in the voicemail — the point is to get them curious enough to call back or open the email, not to deliver the information right there.

The Citation Gap Report — When They Ask for It

If a lead replies to any email asking "can you send the report?" — this is a great sign. Send a one-pager, then follow up with a call ask. Do not use the report as a pitch. Use it as the Call 1 opener.

What Goes in the Report
  • The category queries you searched — 3 buyer-intent variants
  • Screenshots of each AI result (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI)
  • Competitor citation counts with publication names
  • Their company's citation count (usually 0)
  • The publications competitors are cited from (Forbes, USA Today, etc.)
  • One closing line: "This gap is what SwayyEm closes — guaranteed by day 90 or the campaign is on us."

Don't pitch the report. Send it as a deliverable. The Call 1 is the next step. After sending: "Wanted to make sure you had the full picture. Happy to walk through what closing this gap would actually look like — do you have 15 minutes this week?"

Rules + Logistics

  • Any reply stops the sequence immediately. Do not send the next scheduled touch to someone who already responded.
  • Phone always precedes email on days when both are scheduled. Call first, then send the email within the hour.
  • Never leave two voicemails back-to-back on consecutive days. One voicemail per stretch.
  • Never send more than one email per day. You're reaching out, not running a drip campaign.
  • Best send times: Tuesday/Wednesday/Thursday, 8–10 AM in their timezone. Avoid Friday afternoon, Monday morning, and holidays.
  • Stagger sends — don't batch all 44 leads on the same day at the same time. Spread them out to avoid spam pattern detection and to keep your own volume manageable.
  • Log every touch in HubSpot immediately after the call or send. Not at end of day.
  • Hard bounce → close the lead, note the dead email, done.
  • "Unsubscribe me" → confirm receipt politely, close the file, do not retry ever.
  • After Email 5: stop. No follow-up unless they reach out first. Chasing past this point damages the brand.
  • If you don't know something on a live call — don't guess. "Let me check with Matt and follow up." Then actually do it.
