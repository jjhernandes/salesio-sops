What This Is

Matt is giving you ~44 HubSpot leads that were qualified in the last 2–3 months but never moved forward. These are real people who had some contact with SwayyEm — they're not cold, they're just quiet. Your job is to re-engage them and get them back onto a call. Not to pitch. Just to get the conversation open again.

Channel priority: Phone first. Email same day if no answer. Repeat until the cadence ends or they respond. Do not text. Do not rely on LinkedIn for these leads — Matt is building that channel but it's not active enough yet for outbound at this volume.

Before you dial anything: Pull up their HubSpot record. Read any Fathom notes from prior calls. Read any email thread. Know what they discussed with Matt, what their pain was, and why they went cold. Calling blind when the context is sitting right there is a wasted call.

Step 1 — Segment Your Leads Into Three Tracks

Before you pick up the phone, sort every lead into one of these three tracks. The age of the lead determines the approach. Treating a 90-day lead like a 30-day lead makes you sound out of touch. Treating a 30-day lead too gently loses momentum. Match the energy to the timeline.

Track A
30 Days or Less
Still fresh. They probably remember Matt. Reference the last conversation directly. Be direct and warm.
5 touches over 8 days
Track B
31–60 Days
Cooler but not gone. Re-establish context before going anywhere. Don't assume they remember the details.
4 touches over 10 days
Track C
61–90+ Days
Almost cold. Lead with something new — a hook that's relevant to right now. Don't lean on the prior connection.
3 touches over 7 days

Voicemail — First Voicemail (Any Track)
Keep it under 20 seconds

"Hey [Name], this is Hannah from SwayyEm — I work with Matt. Had something I wanted to flag with you specifically. If you have a few minutes this week, give me a call back at [number] or I'll follow up over email. Thanks."

Do NOT summarize what SwayyEm does in the voicemail. Curiosity gets callbacks. Information doesn't.

Voicemail — Second Voicemail (Day 7 Track A only)
Slightly more specific — create curiosity around the gap

"Hey [Name], Hannah from SwayyEm again. I was actually looking at AI search results for [their keyword or industry] today and there's something specific to your situation I wanted to walk you through. Going to send you a quick note — but if you'd rather just hop on a call, you can reach me at [number]. Talk soon."

Two voicemail rules: Never leave two voicemails in a row on back-to-back days — it feels like pressure. And never explain what you want to tell them in the voicemail — the point of a voicemail is to get them curious enough to call back or open your email, not to deliver information.

HubSpot — Log Every Touch

Log every call and email in HubSpot immediately — not at end of day. Use these status labels so Matt can see where each lead is without having to ask you.

Status to Set
When to Use It
Contacted — In Cadence
First touch made. Cadence is running.
Responded — Nurturing
They replied but aren't ready to book yet. Following up on a specific date.
Call Booked
A call is on the calendar. Move to call prep.
Archived — No Response
Full cadence completed, no reply. Stop outreach. Leave the record clean.
Archived — Not Interested
They explicitly said no. Note the reason. Done.

Log the outcome, not just the activity. "Called — no answer" tells Matt nothing useful. "Called Day 1 — no answer, voicemail left, Email 1 sent. Day 3 call — no answer. Waiting for Day 5 email." — that tells him everything. Take 60 seconds to write it correctly.

Rules

  • Phone first, always. An email is easy to ignore. A missed call followed by a specific voicemail creates real curiosity.
  • Send the Day 1 email within 1 hour of the call attempt — not at end of day. Momentum matters.
  • Never leave two voicemails back-to-back on consecutive days. Space them out. One voicemail per stretch.
  • Never send more than one email a day. You're reaching out, not spamming.
  • Do not deviate from the cadence sequence. Skip a day, lose the rhythm — the lead goes colder with each delay.
  • The goal of every touch is to get them on a call, not to pitch over email or voicemail. One CTA. That's it.
  • If they respond at any point in the cadence — stop the cadence immediately and move to the response protocol.
  • After the break-up email: stop. No follow-up unless they reach out first. Chasing past this point damages the brand.
  • Log every touch in HubSpot immediately. Not at the end of the day. Right after you hang up or send.
  • If you don't know something on a live call — don't guess. Say "let me check with Matt and follow up." Then actually do it.
