Why Loom Works

A 60-second video does more than a three-paragraph email ever will. Not because it is more impressive — because it is a sales call. And sales is your job.

What Video Does That Text Can't

You are applying for a sales role. The operator's most important question is: can this person sell? A text application gives them credentials. A Loom gives them evidence.

Video communicates tone, confidence, presence, and energy simultaneously. None of those things exist in a resume or a LinkedIn message. When you record a Loom, you are not describing yourself as a sales professional. You are showing it, right now, in real time.

The Information Gap

A text application can say "I am a confident communicator with strong tonality." A Loom proves it or disproves it within the first five seconds. Operators who have hired before know this. They know a Loom is the closest thing to a discovery call before the discovery call. The ones who apply by text are putting themselves at a deliberate disadvantage.

What a Loom Demonstrates Before You've Said a Word

  • ✓
    Confidence. How you sit, how you open, whether you fidget or hold the frame. The first three seconds of a Loom communicate whether you are comfortable being seen. Closers need to be comfortable being seen.
  • ✓
    Preparation. A targeted Loom that references something specific about the company signals that you did research. That means you are someone who prepares before calls. Operators want reps who prepare.
  • ✓
    Communication clarity. Can you make a point quickly and without filler? The ability to be concise and specific in a Loom is the same ability required to run a clean sales call.
  • ✓
    Energy and tonality. The operator can hear whether you are alive and present or dull and scripted. Tonality is half the job. Your Loom is your audition for a role where tonality matters every single day.
  • ✓
    Initiative. You chose the harder format. That alone separates you from 80% of applicants before they hear a single word of your content.

The Conversion Differential

Response Rate Reality

Reps who apply with a targeted Loom get discovery call responses at dramatically higher rates than reps who apply with text messages or resume links. This is not a small margin. We are talking about the difference between 5-10% response rates on text applications and 40-60%+ response rates on strong Loom applications to quality offers.

The math is simple: if your goal is to land 1 discovery call from 10 applications, you can either send 10 text applications or 3-4 Loom applications with the same result. Loom saves time and gets better outcomes simultaneously.

The operators posting roles are often running high-ticket offers. They are used to seeing Zoom calls, video content, and video applications. A Loom is native to their world. A copy-paste text application is not. Match the medium to the audience.

Text Application Loom Application
Operator reads credentials and makes a guess about who you are Operator sees you in action — energy, tone, preparation, presence
Looks identical to every other text application in the inbox Stands out immediately as someone who does more than is asked
5-10% response rate in a competitive applicant pool 40-60%+ response rate when paired with research and specificity
Proves nothing about sales ability before the discovery call Is itself a mini sales call — demonstrates the core skill before a word is exchanged

Why Reps Avoid Loom (and What That Tells You)

The most common reason reps do not record a Loom: they do not trust themselves on camera. They are afraid of how they look. They do not know what to say. They feel awkward watching themselves. They think they will look bad compared to their imagination of what "good" looks like.

This is important to understand, because sales calls are on camera. Discovery calls are on camera. Closing calls are on camera. If a rep cannot bring themselves to record a 60-second video to apply for a role, that is the same gap that will show up on a call when a prospect pushes back or the rep feels exposed.

Avoiding Loom is not a logistics problem. It is a confidence signal. The rep who will not record one because they are afraid of how they look will also pull back on calls when they feel scrutinized. Close the gap here. The skills transfer directly.

"You're not applying for a job. You're evaluating a business partner. The rep who interviews the company gets the offer. The rep who begs for the offer doesn't."
