02 · What We Found
The Root of the Fear
School Trauma
Teachers told you for months that you'd never make it. That voice is still running in the background. It created an anxiety around "not being good enough" that follows you off the calls.
Cultural Displacement
You're not from the US. On team calls, you hold back — sitting back, watching, giving short answers. You don't fully trust that your perspective lands the same way.
High-Value Lead Anxiety
When someone has serious money or serious experience, you get in your head. The gap between where you are and where they are makes you anxious instead of curious.
Emotionally Driven
80% of the time, you move based on how you feel. If you're tired, you rest. If you're not feeling it, you shift the schedule. Your emotions are running your calendar more than your intentions are.
03 · What Moves You
What Lifts You Up vs. What Drops You
Lifts You
  • Supporting your family — time, money, showing up
  • Being able to give back — to family, friends, even strangers
  • Closing a good call — the energy of performing well
  • When you're locked in and feel like you're making moves
Drops You
  • Late-night YouTube spirals — seeing 16-year-olds making $500K/mo
  • Comparing where you are to what you see online
  • Moving your schedule when you don't feel good — letting emotions run the day
  • No clear structure or obligation pulling you forward

"The comparison between the reality of what you're doing, and what you're seeing — that gap is what brings you down. We need to mitigate that."

04 · The Focus
The #1 Thing to Work On
Primary Focus
Structure & Intentional Living
You've never had structure until JJ told you to build it. Your subconscious is smart — it won't do things it doesn't feel obligated to do. The goal is to build systems that create obligation, so you stop waiting to feel ready and start moving because the structure demands it.
Your Subconscious Is Smart
If you don't have a reason to wake up at 6am, you won't. Not because you're lazy — because your brain is efficient. Build the reason. A call at 6am will get you up every time.
More Intention = More Clarity
The more intentionally you live your days, the clearer the direction gets. You already have the calendar mapped. Now you have to hold yourself to it — even when you don't feel like it.
05 · Action Items
Before Next Session
1
Review this document and re-watch the assigned content
Revisit the structure and intentional living material JJ sent. Know what systems you need. Don't just read — write down what you're going to implement.
2
Clean up your environment
Remove sticky notes from your desk, reduce visual clutter, and create a workspace that signals to your brain: this is where work happens. Set a boundary with your friend about distraction-free hours.
3
Stop moving your calendar when you don't feel like it
The next time you feel like shifting a work block because you're tired, don't. Do it anyway. You can rest after — not instead. Your emotions are not the decision-maker here. The calendar is.
4
Cut the late-night YouTube spiral
No consuming content about other people's success after 9pm. The comparison kills your morning. What you put in your head at night is what you wake up with. Protect that.
5
Show up more vocally on team calls
You're the top setter for 8 months running. You have earned the right to speak. The next team call — contribute. Ask a question, share a win, say something. Your culture concern is in your head. The confidence is already there on your calls; bring it to the team.
