Building a Monthly Review Habit

Weekly reviews catch noise. Monthly reviews catch signal. The pattern that's been true for 4 consecutive weeks is the real thing you need to fix — and monthly review is the only way to see it.

Why Monthly Review Is Different

Weekly reviews are reactive — they tell you what happened this week. They're subject to variance: one unusually strong week can hide a problem, one bad week can cause panic when the trend is actually fine.

Monthly review is pattern-level. It averages out the variance and reveals what's consistently true about your calls. One bad close is a data point. Four consecutive bad closes on the same step is a pattern — and patterns are fixable in a way single events aren't.

The Monthly Review Protocol

  • Pull the data. Close rate, show rate, deal size, objection frequency — pull the last 4 weekly reviews and look at them together. What's the trend in close rate? Is it going up, down, or flat?
  • Find the consistent pattern. What objection came up every week? What step did you score lowest in every week? The things that show up repeatedly are your real constraints — not one-off events.
  • Name the one thing. Not three things. One. The one constraint that, if removed, would have the largest effect on your results. This becomes your focus for the next month.
  • Set the drill. What specific practice or habit will you run every day next month to address the one thing? Not "work on discovery" — "Ask one deeper question after every surface answer. Every call. Review whether I did it after each call."
  • Set a baseline number. What do you expect your close rate to be next month if the one thing improves? Write it down. This is your target — not a goal to hit, but a hypothesis to test.

What Monthly Review Has Revealed (Common Findings)

The most common monthly review finding: The close rate was fine for weeks 1-3 and then collapsed in week 4. Almost always, this is a state management issue — fatigue, overconfidence after a good streak, or life circumstances bleeding into the calls.

Second most common: The close rate is consistent, but average deal size has been dropping. This usually means the prospect quality is declining (setter issue), or the rep is defaulting to split pay without exploring why.

Third most common: The rep thinks they're losing deals to objections, but the data shows most deals go quiet after the pitch — before any objection is raised. This is almost always a discovery problem: the pitch didn't connect, which means discovery didn't surface the right pain.

Making It a Habit

Schedule it. Last Sunday of every month, 90 minutes. Same time, same format, every month. The reps who do this consistently are the ones whose close rate trends upward over a year. The ones who skip it plateau.

The goal of monthly review is not to feel bad about what went wrong. It's to find the one lever that's available to pull. One lever, applied consistently for 30 days, compounded over 12 months — that's the growth path. Most reps try to pull 5 levers at once and move none of them.
