On the Program — Why It's Built This Way
The Balance Between Conceptual and Tactical
You came in expecting it to be more tactical — frameworks, scripts, process. And it will get there. But there's a reason it starts here.

If we're too deep in the conceptual, you're floating on a mountain meditating and none of it connects to a sale. If we're too deep in the tactical, you start ignoring emotions and psychology — yours and theirs — and you become mechanical. Mechanical closers hit ceilings fast.

The blend is what makes it stick. There will be sessions where we go straight into call reviews and deep tactical work. There will be sessions where we need to understand what's happening with you internally, because the gap isn't knowledge — it's something else. You'll know the difference as we go.

Concept 1 — The Story You're Living Into
Narrative Archetypes
We think in stories. You are the main character of your life — that much is true. But the more important question is: what is the narrative you are living into?

Every person operates from an underlying story. It shapes what drives you, what bothers you, what you're chasing, and how you show up. Most people have never named it. When you can name it, you can work with it.
The Hero
Working on themselves to grow, build, create impact. Motivated by becoming something — for family, for legacy, for the people around them. About 70% of entrepreneurs. JJ's archetype.
The Rebel
Breaking the matrix, my way or the highway. Everybody else is asleep. Intense, prideful, anti-mainstream. Sales guys who hate "mindset talk" and just want to execute.
The Explorer
Rooted in novelty. Always somewhere new, always trying something different. Looking for the thing that finally feels like home — but the search never ends.
The Underdog / Sage
Either proving something to people who doubted them, or using past pain as a why — trying to make sure nobody else goes through what they did.
You don't have to have one for life. These shift. But you need to know what story is running right now — because if you don't, it runs you.
Watch for the Hero vs. Rebel split on your calls. Rebel prospects push back hard. Hero prospects are looking to become something. The same pitch hits them differently.

Concept 3 — How to Use Reason as Your Floor
There's Always a Reason
JJ said he never goes below Reason — not on calls, not in arguments, not in difficult moments. Here's how the shift actually happens in practice:

On a sales call: A prospect comes in hot — "what is this call even about?" Instead of reacting, the question is: what's the reason they're saying that? Bad past experience. Skepticism. Fear of being pitched again. Once you see the reason, the emotion stops being directed at you and starts being information you can work with.

On yourself: If something bothers you — a peer's success, a comment someone makes, a result you didn't expect — ask what's the reason. The answer usually tells you more about where you need to grow than the thing itself does.

You said you already do this sometimes, intuitively. It's just not your default first move yet. That's what we're building toward.
When you see people's pain first, their behavior makes sense. An aggressive question on a call isn't about you — it's about every bad experience they had before you. Lead with that lens.
