The Anxiety Conversation

Pressure creates attachment — attachment kills performance

You mentioned your brother funded the first payment and you want to show him progress. That's real and it matters. But here's what actually happens when you carry that pressure into interviews: you stop being yourself. You get attached to the outcome and the person interviewing you can feel it. The ones who perform best in interviews are the ones who genuinely don't need the role — they're just showing up and being authentic. The actions are the same. The energy is completely different. Do the right things with no attachment to when the result arrives.

What JJ does with his own anxiety

JJ said it directly: he's naturally an anxious person. Instead of sitting in the anxiety, he converts it into action — texting students, making calls, doing the work. The anxiety doesn't disappear. It just gets channeled into something useful. That's the model. When you feel the pressure, go apply to 5 more roles. Don't sit in it.
