Program Pillars

What we actually work on

🍚
Pillar 1
Metabolic Reset & Nutrition
What we build
  • Caloric targets based on her metabolism, not a generic calculator
  • Macro structure that includes rice, adobo, sinigang, and Filipino staples — no food is off the table
  • Anti-inflammatory eating protocol for prediabetes, fatty liver, and high cholesterol
  • Blood sugar management strategies that don't require eliminating carbs
  • Sustainable deficit — losing fat while still cooking for the family
What we eliminate
  • The guilt around "bad" foods — rice is not the enemy
  • All-or-nothing meal plans that fall apart at the first family dinner
  • Western diet frameworks that erase her culture
  • Tracking obsession — systems that work even without a food log
  • Supplement dependency — food first, always
"She's been told her whole life that Filipino food is why she can't lose weight. We prove that wrong. Rice stays."
🏋️
Pillar 2
Heavy Lifting & Movement
Training structure
  • Full movement assessment at the start — identify imbalances, limitations, and postpartum considerations
  • Progressive strength programming: squat, hinge, push, pull, carry
  • Form checks via video — every major lift reviewed before loading increases
  • Diastasis recti protocol — modified programming that heals, not harms
  • Adapted for high BP, fatty liver, and T2DM — intensity managed to medical standards
What changes
  • She stops thinking cardio is the answer
  • She lifts heavy and feels capable for the first time
  • Her body composition changes even when the scale doesn't
  • Training fits in 45-60 minutes, 3-4x per week — not 2 hours every day
  • She can train while traveling, during school breaks, through life
📅
Pillar 3
Life Systems & Time Management
Systems we build
  • Weekly calendar blocking around work shifts, school pickups, and husband's schedule
  • Meal prep that feeds the whole family — not separate "diet food"
  • Travel protocols: how to train and eat at airports, hotels, and family events in the Philippines
  • Holiday and fiesta playbook — Noche Buena, birthdays, reunions without derailing progress
  • Energy management: when to train, when to rest, how to recover without guilt
Built around her real life
  • Career — shift work, long hours, corporate travel
  • Kids — their schedules, their activities, their chaos
  • Husband — support system or not, we build around it
  • Extended family — obligations, expectations, pressure
  • Social calendar — parties, parties, and more parties
🧠
Pillar 4
Mindset & Identity Work
What we address
  • Self-sabotage — the unconscious behaviors that undo progress right when it's working
  • All-or-nothing — one missed workout becomes a missed month. We break this pattern for good
  • Perfectionism — the belief that if she can't do it perfectly, why do it at all
  • Imposter syndrome — the feeling that someone like her doesn't deserve to be healthy or take up space
  • People-pleasing — the cultural pressure to put everyone else first, always
What shifts
  • She stops identifying as "someone who always falls off"
  • Health becomes part of who she is, not a project she's doing
  • She stops asking for permission to take care of herself
  • Consistency replaces motivation as the driving force
  • She models for her kids what a healthy relationship with food and body looks like
"She doesn't need more information — she needs a new story about herself. That's where the real work happens."
🩺
Pillar 5
Medical & Health Condition Support
Conditions we work with
  • Prediabetes & Type 2 Diabetes — blood sugar, A1C, carb timing
  • Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease — nutrition and exercise protocols
  • High blood pressure — intensity management, sodium awareness, medication context
  • High cholesterol — dietary fat strategy, fiber, movement
  • Diastasis recti — core rehabilitation, programming modifications
  • Postpartum — hormones, prolapse risk, return-to-lifting timeline
How we approach it
  • We work alongside her doctor, not around them
  • Lifestyle changes that move the lab markers — A1C, liver enzymes, lipids
  • Training and nutrition adjusted for her specific medical picture — not a generic plan
  • Progress tracked alongside medical check-ins so her providers see the results
  • She stops feeling like her conditions make her "too complicated" to help
"Her doctor told her to exercise and eat better. We show her exactly what that means for her body, her labs, and her life."
