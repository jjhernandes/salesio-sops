Action (A) Type

Closing the competitive, impatient prospect. The A type doesn't want to be understood. They want to win. Get to the outcome fast or get out of the way.

What the A Type Cares About

The A type cares about results, speed, and status. They want to know what they'll achieve and how fast they'll achieve it. They're competitive by nature — they pay attention to what top performers are doing, and they want to be in that group.

They respond to directness. If you hedge, slow down, or over-explain, you signal that you're not operating at their level. A types buy from people who move with the same decisive energy they do.

What drives the A type beneath the surface

Underneath the competitive exterior is a deep drive to prove something — to themselves or to the people around them. They're often running from a version of themselves that didn't perform, or chasing a version that does. Framing your offer around that internal story (becoming the person who makes $30K months, being in rooms with elite closers) hits harder than features ever will.

How the A Type Makes Decisions

Fast and outcome-driven

A types make decisions quickly when the right framing is in place. They don't need to process for days — they need to see the win clearly. If you've shown them the outcome, the speed to get there, and that other high performers have done it, the decision is often made before you even close.

The biggest risk with an A type is losing momentum. Every unnecessary tangent, every long explanation, every moment of hedging is a small energy leak. Enough of them and the A type starts to wonder whether you actually know what you're doing.

What Kills the Call with an A Type

Moving too slow. A types make up their minds fast. If you're still in rapport-building mode when they've already decided to evaluate, you've lost the room. Match their pace or they'll tune out.

Too much nuance and context. A types don't want the background story on why the curriculum was built this way. They want to know what it produces. Context is a tax on their time.

No urgency or competitive framing. The A type moves when there's a reason to move now. If your pitch has no edge, no sense of what they're leaving on the table by waiting, they file it in "I'll think about it" — which for an A type usually means no.

Softness in the close. Hedging, apologizing for the price, offering payment plans before they've even reacted — all of this reads as weakness to an A type. They want to buy from strength. If you flinch at the price before they do, you've already lost.

What Closes an A Type

  • ✓
    Lead with outcomes, not process. "You'll close at a higher rate and hit $20-30K months" before "here's how the coaching is structured." A types filter everything through "what do I get?" Get that answered first.
  • ✓
    Competitive framing. "The reps who are making $40K a month right now — this is where they were 90 days ago." A types are wired to benchmark against top performers. Show them where elite looks like and let them self-select into wanting to be there.
  • ✓
    Create real urgency. Not fake scarcity. Real stakes: "Every month you wait at your current close rate is money you're not making." A types respond to the math of inaction, not manufactured deadlines.
  • ✓
    Match their energy. Don't slow down for an A type. Move with them. Be direct. If they interrupt, roll with it. The conversation should feel like two people who both know what they're doing — not a rep explaining something to a prospect.

Call Exchange — Right vs. Wrong with an A Type

VERSION 1 — Rep moves slow, builds too much context
Prospect
"Look I'm not trying to waste time. What's the fastest someone has gone from where I am to $30K months?"
Rep
"Great question — so the way the program is structured, we actually start by building a foundation, because we've found that reps who rush into tactics without the fundamentals end up plateauing. So the first few weeks are really about identity work and understanding yourself as a closer, and then from there we build the tactical layer..."
A type asked about speed of results. Rep launched into curriculum backstory. A type is already half-gone mentally.
Prospect
"Right. I appreciate that. I'll think about it."
VERSION 2 — Rep matches pace, leads with outcome
Prospect
"Look I'm not trying to waste time. What's the fastest someone has gone from where I am to $30K months?"
Rep
"Fastest I've seen — 6 weeks. Jalen came in at $9K/month, closed his first $30K month in week seven. What's your current number and what's your close rate?"
Direct. Specific. Turned it back into a diagnostic immediately. A type now feels like they're talking to someone at their level.
Prospect
"I'm at $12K, close rate's around 22% but I know I can be better. What did Jalen change?"
