Calendar Optimization

Aaron peaks: 10:30 AM to 3 PM. Morning energy, sharp and alert. After 3, it drains. That's where calls should live.

⚠

Weekends: Stop taking them. At Iman's, JJ stopped weekend calls entirely after seeing the math — 9 bookings, 2-3 show, and they were the lowest-quality leads. Zero ROI. Block Saturday availability now.

The test framework: Monday and Wednesday at 10:30 AM, Tuesday and Thursday slightly earlier, Friday something different. Run it for a week. You'll feel the difference between each window, even with a small sample size. Track which time blocks produce the best leads and highest quality prospects.

Block Off

Questions to Ask Every Offer

Average call volume, current team close rate, number of closers on the team, and revenue. Revenue is the one that verifies the math is real.

What OTE Actually Means

On-target earnings = what you make if you hit the KPIs the team requires. Nothing more, nothing less. Here's the math to run on every offer you evaluate:

Formula
Calls Taken × Close Rate = Closes/Month
Then
Closes × Commission/Deal = Income
Example at $20K OTE
$1K/close → need 20 closes/month
At 25% close rate
80 calls/month = ~20 calls/week
Red flag check
Does their stated revenue ÷ closers match their numbers? If not, something's off.

For your job applications: include all collected revenue from your career — not just high-ticket remote. One student came from car sales, listed $17M collected, and nobody blinked. Use your real number, don't leave it out.
