What Makes a Vetted Offer

Not everything on the board is perfect. But everything on the board has passed a baseline. Understand what that baseline is and how to read above it.

The Vetting Criteria

Before a company appears on the Sales.io job board, it goes through a checklist. These are the four non-negotiables.

  • ✓
    Proven revenue. The company has demonstrated consistent revenue at a meaningful level. We are not posting startup ideas or pre-revenue offers. Reps need a real business behind them to close for.
  • ✓
    Defined commission structure. Commission terms are documented before the posting goes live. No "we'll figure it out" arrangements. The rep knows what they earn before they take the first call.
  • ✓
    Active pipeline. There are real leads coming in right now. Not "we're building our funnel." Not "we'll ramp up in Q3." Active, current, qualified leads in the pipeline today.
  • ✓
    Responsive leadership. The operator or sales manager has demonstrated they communicate and that reps are treated as partners, not disposable labor. This is harder to measure but we check it.

What "Vetted" Means — and What It Doesn't

Vetted is a floor, not a guarantee. It means the basics are in place. It does not mean the offer is perfect for you, the close rate is outstanding, or the operator will never have a bad month.

Your job is to use the vetting as a starting point, then do your own evaluation on top of it. Lessons 3, 5, and 6 cover that in depth.

The Practical Translation

A vetted offer is one where a skilled rep has a real shot at earning strong income. It is not a promise that you will close at 30%. The offer quality creates the conditions. Your skills determine the outcome.

This is the combination that produces consistent income: a solid vetted offer plus a rep who executes at a high level. One without the other breaks down. A great rep on a garbage offer is still working against the current. An average rep on a great offer is leaving money on the table.

The Offer Types Posted

B2C
Business to Consumer
Coaching, courses, masterminds, and transformation programs sold direct to individuals. Typically emotional buying decisions, higher close rates on warm inbound, deal sizes from $2K to $25K+. Most common offer type on the board.
B2B
Business to Business
Selling services, software, or solutions to other businesses. Longer sales cycles, multiple decision-makers, logic-first buying patterns. Higher deal sizes, lower volume. Best for reps who have some experience and are comfortable with consultative selling.
Agency
Done-for-You Services
Marketing agencies, lead generation, social media management, SEO. Usually B2B adjacent. Requires ability to articulate ROI and handle price objections around measurable deliverables.
SaaS
Software as a Service
Subscription-based software products. Deal sizes vary widely. Commission may be based on annual contract value. Good entry point for reps who want to move into tech sales longer term.

Why Vetted Offers Matter for Your Numbers

The Math Is Simple

A rep on a garbage offer calling bad leads will have a 5-8% close rate and wonder what they are doing wrong. A rep with the same skill set on a solid vetted offer calling warm inbound leads will have a 15-25% close rate and wonder why they waited so long to switch.

Your close rate reflects two things: your skill and your offer quality. Most reps only look at one of them.

Do not confuse a low close rate with a skill problem until you have confirmed the offer quality is solid. We see this mistake constantly. Reps grind through training thinking they are the problem when the real issue is the pipeline they are calling or the offer they are closing for. Diagnose the system before you diagnose yourself.

Good offer plus good rep equals predictable income. That is the equation. The board gives you the offer side. Your work in this program gives you the rep side. When both are in place, income stops being a mystery.
