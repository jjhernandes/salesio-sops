Key Lessons from Today

The ICP is the most important lever — and it's not in your control right now

Your qualified rate is 12%. That means for every 100 people you connect with, 88 cannot afford it. This is not a setting problem. This is a lead source problem. The audience coming through Discord and YouTube skews young and international — people who are interested but not buyers. If Justin and Tawa can tighten the ICP so that your qualified rate goes from 12% to 20%, your sets almost double without you doing anything differently. That conversation needs to happen on their side. You can flag it, but you cannot fix it alone.

The funnel type determines how you sell — and right now there is no consistent funnel

One of the hardest parts of your situation is that the lead gen keeps changing: waitlist, then webinar, then upsell, then waitlist again. Every format creates different levels of buy-in when someone gets on a call. A webinar prospect is pre-sold. A waitlist applicant is interested but cold. If you treat them the same way you will de-sell one and under-sell the other. The comparison: Hunter's closer works 2 days a week and does $30K because the webinar is so clear that prospects arrive almost ready to close. Eli is on a million-dollar offer and works 6 days a week because he treats every call the same regardless of where the prospect came from. Know what funnel you are working off of and adjust accordingly.

Dial volume during launches needs to be maximum speed

During a launch, the window is short and intent is highest on day one. The goal should be 200+ dials per day, not 50. Your volume dropped from 54 to 52 to 36 across three days because you were spending too much time on calls. That is a good problem — it means people want to talk. But the answer is to get through the list faster early so you are doing follow-ups later, not burning your best days slowly. Next launch: front-load the dials.

Your setting skill is already there

100% qualified-to-set rate across 33 leads is not luck. Every person you qualify, you book. That is not something you need to fix. The connect rate is strong, your follow-up instinct is good (calling back off texts within 30 minutes), and you are already reviewing your own recordings and taking notes before sending to Jalen. That level of self-awareness is rare at this stage.

The Career Conversation

Two paths — be clear on which one you're on

Tawa and Justin see the natural next move as setter management — because neither of them has set before, and they need someone who understands it. That is a real role and a real opportunity. The bottleneck is volume. They need to grow to a point where they need to hire more setters before that position makes sense.

The other option is closing. The opportunity is right in front of you — Tawa's calendar fills up, the demand is there, and you already understand the leads better than anyone. You do not need to wait for permission. Ask the question: is there a world where I take some of these calls? Even a few closes would accelerate your learning faster than anything else. The goal for the next 6-7 weeks is to be transition-ready either way.
