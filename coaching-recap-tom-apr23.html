The Direction Problem

Part of what's making the belief gap harder is that your external direction is unclear too. You don't know when or how setting management becomes real. That uncertainty creates a mental opening — you start looking around at other options, entertaining other paths, and that scattered attention makes it harder to commit fully to where you are.

This isn't a criticism. It's how it works. When you don't know where you're going, you'll keep glancing at every sign.

Two conversations worth having with your team
1. What does setting management actually look like? Not "when" — that's too vague. Ask: what would need to be true for that to happen? What are the conditions? What's the team size at that point? Get something concrete.

2. Ask for a base. You've never had this conversation. While the offer is in its fluctuation phase and you're essentially working through slow periods without income, a base of £2,000–3,000/month is a fair ask. Start the conversation at £3,500. If they land at £2,000, that's fine — that's negotiation. But you haven't asked, so you definitely don't have it.

5
State management awareness. Start paying attention to what you do before calls — good ones and bad ones. You don't know the pattern yet. That's fine. Start noticing. What were you doing in the 30 minutes before your best calls? That's the baseline you want to replicate.

The Bottom Line

You're further along than you think. The skill is there. The awareness is developing. The slow month is mostly external — and there's a launch in two weeks that changes the numbers.

What Phase 2 is actually about is simple: close the gap between what you know and what you feel. That happens through the belief work, through data that shows you your own progress, and through conversations that give you external clarity on your direction.

Stop changing everything at once. Pick a lane. Track what happens. Send the recordings. The answers are already there — you just need the evidence in front of you to stop second-guessing them.
