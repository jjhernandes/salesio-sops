Volume vs. Quality

The KPIs that actually matter for setters. Volume without quality is noise. Quality without volume is unsustainable. Here's where the line is and how to stay on the right side of it.

The Volume Trap

The volume trap: A setter books 25 calls in a week. The closer takes 18 of them. 3 close. The team celebrates the setter's "high volume." But the closer burned out on 15 terrible calls and is now questioning their own close rate.

Volume metrics look good on paper and feel productive in the moment. They also mask a quality problem that quietly destroys closer performance and morale over time.

A bad lead isn't neutral. It costs the closer an hour, it burns state, and if it happens consistently, it starts warping the closer's belief in the program. The setter who books unqualified leads is not performing well — they're creating damage.

The KPIs That Actually Matter

Show Rate
Target: 80%+
If show rate drops below 70%, the leads being booked aren't serious enough. Booking confirmation is weak or qualification was skipped.
Closer Close Rate on Setter's Leads
Target: Matches overall close rate
If the closer's close rate drops on a specific setter's leads, the quality is the problem — not the closer.
Booking Rate from Conversations
Target: 30-50%
What % of genuine conversations turn into booked calls. Below 20% = weak qualification or positioning. Above 60% = possibly not qualifying hard enough.
Revenue Generated per Lead Booked
The real number
The setter's actual contribution metric. Not "calls booked" — revenue closed from booked calls. Volume means nothing without this.

The Quality Floor — What Must Be True Before a Call Books

Don't book ifDo book when
They can't articulate why they're interestedThey have a clear and specific reason for the call
No indication they can or would invest at the program levelBudget capacity is either confirmed or reasonably inferred
Third-party decision maker not identified ("my wife would have to agree" with no plan to include her)Decision maker is on the call or their role in the decision is understood
They're "just curious" with no urgencyThere's a clear timeline or pain driving the inquiry
They booked to get the setter off the phoneThey booked because they genuinely want the conversation

Sustainable Volume

The goal is not to book fewer calls. It's to book the right calls at a volume that's repeatable. A setter who books 8 genuinely qualified calls per week is more valuable than one who books 20 mixed-quality calls — every time.

Volume for its own sake is ego. It feels productive. It looks good in the daily standup. And it slowly erodes the team's results, morale, and belief in the product. Quality is a form of respect for the closer's time and energy.

The right target: hit minimum quality standards on every call, then push volume within those constraints. Don't loosen the quality floor to hit a volume target. Ever.
