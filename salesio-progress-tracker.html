Phase 1 — Foundation

Weeks 1-4 · Who you are before who you sound like on a call.

You start with identity, not scripts. Who you are off your sales calls is who you are on your sales calls — this week is about seeing your default patterns before you try to change them.

Learn to read a prospect's personality type in the first two minutes, and adjust how you communicate accordingly. This is the skill that makes everything after it land.

The full call structure, mapped step by step — intro through close. Not a script. A map you can navigate off of no matter what the prospect throws at you.

Real discovery isn't a question list — it's building the gap between where they are and where they want to be, so the offer becomes the obvious next step.

2

Phase 2 — Influence

Weeks 5-8 · The pitch, the psychology, and the objections that used to beat you.

A tight, 2-5 minute pitch built around the 3-4 things this specific prospect actually values. No feature dump. No script.

Every major stall and objection, broken down to what's actually being said underneath it — and how to respond without getting defensive.

The research behind why people actually buy. People buy based on emotions and compensate logically — this week you learn to work with that instead of against it.

Checkpoint: Week 7 Mastery Test.

How to architect the decision itself, not just push toward it. This is where close rate typically jumps the most.

3

Phase 3 — Mastery

Weeks 9-12 · Language, frame control, and landing the role.

I'd rather lose the sale than lose the frame. This week is about holding your frame under pressure — tonality, word choice, and not folding when a prospect pushes back.

Cold calling, DM setting, tonality on the setting side, and how a clean handoff to a closer actually works.

Checkpoint: renewal doc goes out this week if you haven't received it yet.

How to stay level when the numbers dip — because they will. This is what separates people who make it a career from people who quit after a bad month.

Final call review, final numbers logged against your guarantees, and active use of the job platform if you're not already placed. You walk out of Week 12 with a role, not just a certificate.
