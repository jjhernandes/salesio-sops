Before Every Call

Step 1

Check SmartLead for setter notes

Matt's setter team at C-17 uses SmartLead for cold email outreach. Log in, search the company name, and pull up any email threads. You may not always find something, but if there's context from the conversation that got this call booked, read it. It tells you how they were approached and what got them interested.

  • Log into SmartLead (login sent from Matt)
  • Search the company name
  • Read the email thread if one exists — note what was said and what got the reply
Step 2

Find their main keyword

Go to their website. Look at their homepage headline and the language they use to describe what they do. That is your keyword. You're looking for the search term their buyers would type to find them. If it's not obvious, you can ask Claude: "What is the main keyword this website would be targeting?" and paste their homepage text.

  • Go to their website
  • Identify their primary keyword from the homepage messaging
  • Write it down — you'll use it in the next two steps
Step 3

Open two browser tabs — this is what you show on the call

This is the most important prep step. Before every Call 1, open these two tabs and leave them ready to share on screen:

  • Tab 1: Google — search their keyword, look at the AI Overview at the top. Screenshot or just keep the tab live. Note which competitors are showing up.
  • Tab 2: ChatGPT — search the same keyword. Note which companies are mentioned. Note if they're there or not.
  • Write down 2-3 competitor names you see in both results — you'll reference these on the call.

Call 1 — Discovery and the Gap

Opening · First 5-7 minutes

Ask before you say anything about SwayyEm

Do not pitch first. Ask two or three questions to understand why they booked and what they're trying to solve. This makes everything you say after feel relevant to them specifically, not like a standard pitch.

Script

"Before I walk you through what we do, help me understand where you are right now. What are you currently doing to get visibility and stay in front of new buyers — and where is it falling short for you?"

Follow-up if needed

"Is there a specific launch or timeline that's making this more of a priority right now?"

The Pivot · After you've heard them

Pull up the search results live

This is the moment you've prepped for. Share your screen and open the Google AI tab you prepared. Do not explain it before you show it — let them see it first.

Script

"Before our call I pulled up what [their keyword] looks like in Google's AI search today. Can I show you something really quick?"

[Share screen. Pull up Google AI tab.]

"So here's Google AI overview for your keyword. You can see [Competitor A], [Competitor B], [Competitor C] are all showing up here. When someone searches for this and uses the AI result to make a decision, those are the companies they're being pointed to."

[Switch to ChatGPT tab.]

"Same search on ChatGPT. Same result. Your competitors are being recommended. You're not in these results."

"Is this what you're seeing on your end when you search your own keyword?"

The Why · After they confirm the gap

Explain why this is happening — use the two stats

Once they see the gap, they'll want to know why. This is where you deliver the core explanation. Keep it short.

Script

"The reason this is happening is pretty straightforward. Eighty-nine percent of all AI search results pull from tier one publications — Forbes, TechCrunch, Bloomberg, that kind of thing. Your competitors are getting mentioned in those publications. You're not yet. LLMs are training on that content, not on company blog posts or websites. So if you want to show up when your buyers use AI to research, the fastest path is getting placed in tier one publications. That's what we do."

The Next Step · Before you hang up

Offer the analysis and book Call 2 before hanging up

Do not end Call 1 without a committed date for Call 2. The analysis is what gives them a reason to come back — it's personalized to their company and their keywords. Make it feel valuable and specific.

Script

"What I'd like to do before we talk again is put together a full competitive gap analysis specific to your company — your keywords, your competitors, exactly where the gap is and what we'd do to close it. That's what we'll walk through on our next call. Does [specific day and time] work for you?"

  • Get a committed date and time for Call 2 before hanging up
  • Ask them to confirm their primary keywords so you can run the analysis
  • Note any specific competitors they mentioned during the call

Call 2 — The Analysis and the Close

Before Call 2

Build the analysis deck — 4 to 5 custom slides

Matt's team does this, but Matt has said it would actually be good for you to do it yourself — it forces you to understand the client before you get on the call. Here is what goes in the deck:

  • Slide 1 — Traffic decline: Pull the company's site in Ahrefs. Screenshot the traffic and keyword position graph. The drop since mid-last year is visible across almost every client.
  • Slide 2 — Google AI results: Screenshot the AI overview for their keyword. Competitors visible. They're not.
  • Slide 3 — ChatGPT results: Same keyword, same result. Screenshot.
  • Slide 4 — Publication types: Show the types of placements SwayyEm executes. Match the examples to their industry — B2B examples for B2B clients.
  • Slide 5 — Recommendations + Pricing: Specific publications recommended for them and the package that fits.

Note: Slides 4 and 5 are mostly the same across clients. You're customizing slides 1-3 each time. Matt will send you the base deck to work from.

Call 2 Opening

Recap before you present

Start every Call 2 by showing you listened. Reference something specific from Call 1 before pulling up the deck.

Script

"Before I walk you through the analysis, I want to make sure I'm framing this correctly. Last time you mentioned [X — their main priority or pain point]. Is that still the main thing, or has anything shifted since we spoke?"

The Presentation

Present the analysis as a business problem, not a report

Each slide is a business point, not a data dump. Narrate what it means for them, not what it shows technically.

Slide 1 — Traffic

"So here's your organic traffic over the last 18 months. You can see this drop starting around mid-last year. That's not a coincidence — that's when Google started shifting traffic to AI results instead of traditional links. This is happening across every client we work with."

Slides 2 and 3 — AI Results

"Same thing we looked at on our last call — here's what your keyword looks like in Google AI and ChatGPT right now. [Competitor A] and [Competitor B] are showing up consistently. You're not in either of these results. Every time one of your buyers searches this and uses the AI answer, they're being pointed to your competitors."

Slide 4 — Publications

"This is how we solve it. These are the types of tier one publications we place clients in — [examples relevant to their industry]. LLMs are actively training on content from these sources. Once you're showing up in them, you start showing up in AI results for your keyword."

Slide 5 — Recommendation

"Based on your keyword, your industry, and where your competitors are right now, here's specifically what we'd recommend for you and what that looks like from an investment standpoint."

The Close

Find the real objection and get a decision before you hang up

Every call should end with either a yes or a clear next step with a date. "I'll think about it" is not a next step. If they're not ready, find out what the one remaining obstacle is and work through it on the call.

If they're interested but not deciding

"What's the main thing standing between where we are and a decision? Is it budget, getting someone else comfortable, or something else? I want to make sure we're solving the right problem on this call."

To close the next step

"What is the next step on your end? What date can we put on the calendar so this doesn't lose momentum?"

  • Do not hang up without either a decision or a specific next step + date
  • If they mention someone else who needs to be involved, ask: "Can we get them on the next call?"
  • If it's a budget question, find out if it's timing or approval — those are different problems

Within 30 minutes

Log what happened

  • What was their main pain point or stated goal?
  • Who makes the decision — and is everyone who needs to say yes now identified?
  • What is the next step and what date did you commit to?
  • What went well? What felt off?

The Pattern That Makes This Work

Call 1: show them the gap. Book Call 2. Call 2: present the analysis, make the business case, get the decision. The deck is simple. The talk track is simple. What makes it land is that you're showing them their specific company, their specific keyword, their specific competitors — not a generic demo. That personalization is what closes deals.

Bottom Line

Hannah, the structure is clear. Prep the two browser tabs. Do discovery before you pitch. Show them the gap live on screen. Book the analysis call before you hang up. Come back with a deck built for them specifically. Close or get a committed next step. That is the whole process.
