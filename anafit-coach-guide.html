Non-Negotiables

Key rules to remember.

Everything you need to run this program consistently — not perfectly, but consistently.

📌 1-on-1 calls are every 2 weeks, not every week. You have 6 total over 12 weeks. Don't burn out offering weekly Zoom calls — that's what the weekly check-in is for.
📌 Day 1 means Day 1. The Client Intake and Movement Assessment get sent the moment they pay. Don't wait until the first call. Get that information immediately.
📌 Text them every Sunday. If a client hasn't filled out their weekly check-in, send a reminder. That's how you keep them accountable and keep them in the program.
💡 Show them their progress. Clients need to see they're improving — if they can't see it, they won't renew. Use the monthly progress tracker and phase reviews to make progress visible.
💰 Week 12 is a sales call, not a goodbye. The graduation is where you pitch the next round. Have a concrete next goal ready — "now let's get you here" — before you get on the call.
When They Pay

Day 1 — exactly what to do.

The moment a client signs up and pays, here's your exact sequence.

1
Send the Client Hub link
This is their home base for the entire program. Send it via WhatsApp or however you communicate. If they speak Spanish, send the Spanish Client Guide link too so they know how to navigate everything.
2
Tell them to complete the Client Intake Assessment today
Health history, goals, current habits, sleep, injuries. This is the foundation of everything. You need this before you can build their program. Don't start building until you have it.
3
Tell them to complete the Movement Assessment today
This shows you how their body moves, where the risks are, and what needs corrective work. Also Day 1. Both forms — same day.
4
Build their 12-week program based on what you receive
Once their forms come into your inbox, you have everything you need to build their Phase 1 program. Deliver it before or during the Week 2 strategy session.
5
Remind them: Habit Tracker starts immediately, Check-In starts Sunday
They should start the Habit Tracker the same day they sign up. The Weekly Check-In starts the first Sunday after they join. Set that expectation on Day 1.

Month 3 → Month 4+

The renewal conversation.

Week 12 is not the end — it's the close for the next round. Here's how to handle it.

🧠 The mindset: 3 months gets them into decent shape — but not their best shape. They need you to take them further. Your job at graduation is to make them see the next level clearly.
1
Review their Phase 3 results together on the call
Pull up their progress tracker. Show them the side-by-side from Month 1 to Month 3. Make the results real and visible — this is your social proof in the room.
2
Give them a concrete next vision
Don't just ask if they want to continue. Tell them where they can go. "You've lost weight — now let's get you to the point where you're seeing real muscle definition." Make the next goal feel reachable and exciting.
3
Make the offer
Another 12 weeks. $2,000 to $6,000 depending on what you're charging. They already trust you, they've already seen results, and they already know how the system works. The close should be simple.
4
If they say yes — Day 1 starts immediately
Don't let momentum drop. The same system restarts. You already know their body, their habits, and their patterns. Phase 4 should be your best work together yet.
💰 The math: A client who renews twice is worth $6,000–$18,000 total. The retention conversation is the highest-leverage thing you do. Treat it like a sales call — because it is one.
