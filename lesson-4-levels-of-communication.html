What you say is 7% of what lands. The other 93% is how you say it, the intention behind it, and how you actually feel about yourself. Most sales training lives in the 7%. Most closed deals happen in the other 93%.

This is why a rep can memorize every word of a perfect script and still lose the sale. And why another rep with no script, no framework, and half the product knowledge closes it. The prospect isn't evaluating words. They're reading everything else — and they're doing it before you've finished your first sentence.

There are four distinct levels at which you communicate on every call. They're not equal. They don't fire at the same time. And most people have no idea they're even operating on four channels simultaneously.

"You can have the perfect script and lose the sale. You can have no script and close it."

The Four Levels

These four levels are always active on every call. The prospect is receiving all four simultaneously — they just can't name what they're picking up on. The question is whether they're all working together or working against each other.

The levels are stacked by impact — not by the order they're delivered. Level 4 is the foundation everything else rests on. If it's unstable, nothing above it holds. The prospect feels this in the first 90 seconds without knowing what they're picking up on.

Level by Level

Level 4 — The Foundation
How You Feel About Yourself

This is the identity layer. Not what you think about yourself in the moment — what you know about yourself at your core. A rep who has genuinely helped hundreds of people carries something different into a call than someone who hasn't. You can feel it. The prospect feels it. It registers in under a second, before you've said a word.

Level 4 is the Impulse layer — the fastest, most primitive signal a human can transmit. The gut read. It happens before your agenda is set, before you've introduced yourself, before you've asked a single question. And once that impression lands, everything you say at Levels 1 through 3 is filtered through it.

This is what "who you are off your sales calls is who you are on your sales calls" actually means. The call doesn't create Level 4. It reveals it.

Level 4 is built between calls — not on them. Every result stacked. Every hard conversation not avoided. Every piece of identity work done. You cannot shortcut it and you cannot fake it for long.
Level 3
Your Intention

Why are you actually on this call? Not the story you tell yourself — the real answer. If the answer is "I need to close this, I'm behind, I need this commission" — they feel it. Not your words. The intention. It radiates through your tonality, shapes your question quality, determines your ability to sit in silence, and affects your willingness to tell someone they're not a fit.

Intention is the Chemical layer. The emotional state of the communicator is transmitted before it's translated into words. A seller operating from genuine service creates a different atmosphere on the call than one running on desperation — even when the words are identical.

The right intention: "I want to find out if I can genuinely help this person. If yes, I'll tell them. If no, I'll tell them that too." When this is real — not performed — it resets every level below it.

Write your intention before every call. Not the script. The reason you're showing up. Read it before you dial.
Level 2
How You Say It

Tonality. Pace. The deliberate use of silence. The emphasis you put on certain words. This is the Behavioral layer — it operates through pattern recognition. The prospect's nervous system tracks your rhythm and mirrors it without knowing. Speak fast and anxiously, they get anxious. Slow down and pause, they settle. You are setting the behavioral tempo of the call whether you intend to or not.

The most underused tool at Level 2 is silence. Most salespeople are terrified of it. The best ones use it as a precision instrument. Say something true. Stop. Let the dissonance sit. The next words out of the prospect's mouth are the most honest thing they'll say on the entire call.

Record your next three calls and listen back — not to what you said, but how you said it. Speed, pausing, and tone tell you more about your Level 3 and 4 state than any self-assessment will.
Level 1 — 7%
What You Say

The words. The script. The framework. The objection handling language. This is where 90% of sales training lives. And it's the last thing that matters.

Words are processed at the Electric layer — the slowest to fire and the most easily overridden. The prospect's brain has already made a gut decision about you before it processes a single thing you've said. Words confirm or contradict what was already decided at Levels 4, 3, and 2. They matter. They just matter last.

The words still need to be right. A precise question in the right moment can surface pain that nothing else could. But precise words delivered with wrong intention and shaky identity will lose to imperfect words delivered with full congruence — every time.

The four levels aren't arbitrary. Each one maps to a specific layer of the human nervous system — and those layers don't fire in the order most salespeople assume. Decisions happen at the deepest, fastest layer first. By the time rational processing engages, the outcome is already determined.

This mismatch is why reps with better scripts keep losing to reps with better presence.

Level 4 — The Deep Work

Level 4 deserves its own section because it's the most impactful and the most misunderstood. You can't train it in a script. You can't turn it on for a call. You build it — or you don't — in how you live, work, and grow between calls.

The Composure Pendulum — Level 4 in Action

On any given call, your Level 4 state falls somewhere on this pendulum. Most salespeople swing left or right depending on how the call is going. The best ones stay in the center — not because they don't feel pressure, but because their identity isn't riding on the outcome.

Left
Collapse
Neediness. Desperation. Over-explaining. Level 4 is corrupted — the identity is tied to closing this deal. The need radiates before a word is spoken.
Center
Composure
Genuine indifference to the outcome. Genuine care about the person. Level 4 is clean. This is the only state that closes deals without feeling like selling.
Right
Posturing
Performed confidence. Aggression. Level 4 is faked. Feels like authority from the inside — reads as insecurity from the outside.

The pendulum swings in response to pressure. The first hard objection. A long silence. A prospect who pushes back. Whatever your Level 4 actually is — it surfaces under pressure. There's no hiding it.

Authority Tripwires — How Level 4 Signals Itself

Level 4 doesn't stay hidden. It radiates through five signals the prospect scans unconsciously before the conversation has truly started. These are not things you perform — they are things you embody or you don't.

  • Movement Deliberate, unhurried movement. Fidgeting, rushing, leaning in too much — all register as low-status before you've said anything.
  • Appearance How you carry yourself. Not clothes — composure. The person who looks like they need nothing reads as someone worth following.
  • Confidence Certainty in voice, pace, and silence. Especially silence. The ability to pause without filling the space is one of the clearest Level 4 signals there is.
  • Connection Genuine interest, not performed warmth. They can feel the difference between someone who is actually curious about them and someone running a rapport script.
  • Internal Feeling This is Level 4 itself — radiating outward. How you feel about yourself is not contained inside you. It transmits. You cannot fake this for long under real pressure.

The Congruence Test

When all four levels are aligned — when what you say reflects how you say it, reflects your intention, reflects who you actually are — the prospect feels no gap. Their defenses don't lower because you convinced them. They lower because there was nothing to defend against. This is congruence. It's the most advanced trust signal in sales. And it cannot be scripted.

Congruent
  • Words match the delivery
  • Delivery matches the intention
  • Intention matches the identity
  • Prospect feels nothing "off"
  • Trust builds without effort
  • Pressure doesn't break the frame
Incongruent
  • Right words, hesitant voice
  • Confident tone, desperate intention
  • Clean intention, shaky identity under pressure
  • Prospect senses "something's off"
  • Can't name it — just won't move
  • Objections increase as pressure mounts
The Most Common Incongruence

Level 3/4 mismatch. The seller has the right intention going into the call — but the moment a prospect pushes back hard, the identity cracks. The need surfaces. The pendulum swings left. The prospect feels the shift and starts to pull back. The words don't change. The energy does. And they feel the energy.

What To Do With This

Level 4
This is the work between calls. Identity work. Coaching. Stacking results. Not avoiding hard conversations. Not tying your identity to any single outcome. Every closed deal builds Level 4. Every time you fold under pressure erodes it. You build this over months and years — not on a single call.
Level 3
Write your intention before every call. Not the pitch — the reason. "My job today is to find out if I can genuinely help this person. If yes, I'll tell them. If no, I'll tell them that too." Read it before you dial. When it's real, your tonality, your questions, and your silence all change automatically.
Level 2
Slow your pace by 20% on your next call. Use silence deliberately — say something true, then stop talking. Record your calls and listen back not to what you said but how you said it. Speed and tone are the most honest signals of what's actually happening at Levels 3 and 4.
Level 1
Know the framework. Know what to say when. But understand it's the last lever — and that it only lands when the other three are solid underneath it. Invest in Level 1 last, not first.

The Real Thing

The Core Principle
Who you are off your sales calls
is who you are on your sales calls.
The frameworks matter. The questions matter. The scripts matter — a little. But none of it works if the person delivering it isn't congruent across all four levels. Prospects don't buy from people who are performing. They buy from people who are real. The four levels of communication are always on. The only question is whether they're working for you or against you.
