Lead Gen — The Podcast Trojan Horse
The Gym Launch Method (Adapted for Your Niche)
JJ ran this model when he had his agency and went from 0 to 40 leads in two weeks, closed 12, and had to shut the agency down because he couldn't fulfill. The principle: people love talking about themselves. A podcast invitation gets a yes when a sales call gets ignored.
1
Cold outreach framed as a podcast invite: "Hey, we run [Podcast Name] — a show for [your niche] by [your niche]. We'd love to feature you as a guest. Would that be something you're open to?" Response rate on this is dramatically higher than a pitch.
2
The podcast host is an SDR: The "interview" is a structured discovery call. You're asking about their business, what's working, what isn't, what they've tried. All the discovery questions — but they feel like a conversation, not an interrogation.
3
End of the "episode" — soft bridge: "Before I let you go — you mentioned [pain point]. We actually have a program specifically for that. I'd love to connect you with [person/resource] if you're open to it. No pressure." Then you're in a sales conversation with a warm, trusting prospect.
4
For your niche: Service business owners / coaches / agency owners struggling with outreach and not getting responses. The "show" could be around client acquisition, cold outreach, or getting to $10K/month without a big following. Test 3-4 private episodes first.
JJ is sending you the document and a link to the podcast example so you can see the actual format and approach. Test 3-4 calls with this model before committing to scaling it.
This is complementary to the free challenge idea — not a replacement. The challenge gets volume. The podcast gets depth and trust. Try the podcast first given where you are on volume.
On the Pitch — Screen Share and Presentation
Less Is More — Don't Show What You Don't Need To
JJ's rule: no screen share unless it's genuinely impressive. If you share something that looks like a work-in-progress, a simple platform, or anything that gives the prospect visual reasons to question the value — they will. People fill in gaps positively when there's nothing concrete to pick apart.

The TikTok Pitch principle: the discovery of the DNA double helix — arguably the most important scientific finding ever — was explained to the world in five minutes. Your pitch doesn't need more time than that, and it definitely doesn't need a screen.
Talk about it. Don't show it. They'll imagine something better than whatever you build on a screen share.
On Pricing
$3K + $3K on Milestone — Right for Now
You questioned whether you're underselling yourself. The honest answer: probably, eventually. But not yet — and here's why price is more nuanced than it seems.

JJ has sold programs at $36K and $144K where the actual difference in delivery was minimal. The only variable was who was being pitched. Price is a function of perceived value, proof, and who you're in the room with — not a fixed ethical number.

Where you are now: fewer reps under your belt, less documented proof, still refining the process. The price is appropriate for that. As the proof compounds, as the results stack, as your confidence in delivery grows — the price goes up. That's not a concession. That's how this builds.
