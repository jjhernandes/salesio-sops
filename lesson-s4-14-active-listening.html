Active Listening

Repeat their life better than they told it. That's the skill. When you can do that, prospects feel more understood in 30 minutes with you than they have with most people in years.

What Active Listening Actually Is

Active listening is not nodding along. It's absorbing what someone says — including the emotion behind it — and reflecting it back with enough precision that they feel genuinely heard. Not summarized. Heard.

Most reps think they're listening when they're actually waiting. They hear enough to know when to speak again, but they're not processing what was actually said — they're processing what they're going to say next.

The prospect can feel this. It's why so many calls feel transactional even when the rep is technically asking all the right questions.

"Repeat their life better than they told it."

The Four Levels of Listening

LevelWhat You're DoingWhat They Experience
Level 1 — PassiveWaiting to talk, half-presentFeels like a sales call
Level 2 — AttentiveHearing the words correctlyFeels like a decent conversation
Level 3 — ReflectiveMirroring back what was saidFeels like you were actually listening
Level 4 — EmotionalNaming the feeling behind what was saidFeels like you understand them

Most reps operate at Level 1-2. Elite closers operate at Level 3-4. The difference in close rate between Level 2 and Level 4 is not small.

The Active Listening Protocol

  • Hear completely. Don't start formulating your response until they've fully finished. This requires you to actually be present, not planning ahead.
  • Identify the emotion. Before you respond, ask yourself: "What is the feeling underneath what they just said?" Frustration, hope, fear, pride, shame — name it internally before speaking.
  • Reflect the content AND the emotion. "So what I'm hearing is you've been grinding at this for 8 months, putting everything in, and it's not showing up in the results yet — and that's starting to feel like maybe the problem is you. Is that right?"
  • Check accuracy. End with "Is that right?" or "Am I getting that right?" This gives them permission to correct you — and when they do, they go deeper.

The Difference in Practice

VERSION 1 — Attentive but not active
ProspectYeah, I've been in sales for two years and I'm just kind of stuck. I'm making like $6k a month but I feel like I should be making more by now.
RepGot it. So what's been holding you back?
Moved on without absorbing. Zero reflection. They feel like a data point.
VERSION 2 — Active listening at Level 4
ProspectYeah, I've been in sales for two years and I'm just kind of stuck. I'm making like $6k a month but I feel like I should be making more by now.
RepSo two years in, you've been putting the work in, and the number is there — but there's this feeling like the number should be different by now given how much effort you've put in. Like there's a gap between where you expected to be and where you actually are. Is that it?
ProspectYeah, exactly. It's like... I know I'm capable of more but I can't figure out what I'm missing.
They went deeper. The reflection opened the real thing. Now you're in the conversation that matters.

Building the Habit

After every call: Write down one thing the prospect said and ask yourself — did I reflect it or did I move past it? If you moved past it, how would you have reflected it at Level 4?

The most common mistake: Reflecting the surface content without naming the emotion. "So you want to make more money" is Level 2. "So you've been working harder than most people you know and the results don't reflect that yet — and that feels like something's wrong with you, not the situation" is Level 4. Only Level 4 opens the prospect up.

Active listening is also the fastest way to handle the "but I already know what they're going to say" problem. When you actually listen instead of confirming your assumptions, prospects surprise you. And the surprises are always where the close lives.
