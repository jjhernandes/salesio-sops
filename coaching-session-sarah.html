"Is there preference between virtual backgrounds and real ones? Studies for either?"
Your Answer
Real background wins — when the environment is clean and intentional. A real space communicates authenticity, presence, and confidence. Virtual backgrounds create a subtle disconnect — the eye knows something is off, even if the brain doesn't register it consciously. The research on this is consistent: perceived credibility goes up with a real, professional environment. Her office work she mentioned is the right instinct. Clean, organized, intentional = more trust before she says a word.
"How do you do 15 mins when we're on 45 min calls?"
Your Answer
The 15-minute review is not a full call replay — it's a targeted audit of 2–3 moments. After the call, while it's fresh: write down the moment she felt the call shift (better or worse), what she said before it, and what she'd do differently. That's it. The audit log isn't a diary entry — it's a single observation and a single adjustment. The full call review (game tape) is separate and goes to the coaching team. The daily feedback loop is the micro-practice that compounds over time.
