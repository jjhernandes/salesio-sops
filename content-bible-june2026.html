Signature Stories
Ready to film as-is. Each one is a complete narrative from JJ's own interview.
The $36,000 sale he lost by going back to the script
First ten minutes she was open, emotional, vulnerable, giving him everything without him even having to ask. He got tactical — went back to his framework because he didn't trust what he was seeing — and felt it shift in real time. "The process is a floor, it's not a ceiling. When someone is already above it and you drag them back down to it, you've just de-sold them."
Kevin — the student who never once mentioned commission
Older guy, first calls on a new offer. Asked what brings him down and he didn't mention his commission once. That's genuinely rare. Most reps, when their numbers are low, feel like less of a person — Kevin could look at a bad call the way a mechanic looks at a broken engine. Curiosity instead of shame.
Spencer — the closer whose biggest skill was silence
Reviewing calls for a financial advisory team — a tech issue came up mid-call and Spencer just waited. Didn't fill it. "That silence was authority you cannot fake. You either have peace with yourself or you don't, and it shows up in the quiet moments."
The 10% closer who never ran his closing sequence — for months
Talented door-to-door rep, genuinely knew something was off, kept looking in the wrong place. He'd been running his full pitch for months without ever actually running the closing sequence. That's what happens when the problem is internal — you search for it everywhere else.
Hormozi told him to shut down B2C — he was right
Not something JJ saw coming. Rebuilding Sales.io from scratch afterward taught him more than anything he could have planned for — a story about what being inside the room teaches you that observing from outside never could.
Hot Takes — Industry Critique
JJ's contrarian, insider opinions on the sales training industry.
Scripts are the destination, not the starting point
The whole industry runs on flowcharts — if they say this, you say that. It trains reps to listen for triggers instead of listening to people. Great pattern recognition, terrible at actual conversation. The second a prospect says something they didn't prepare for, they freeze.
Accountability without diagnosis makes reps worse
"You're not closing because you're not doing enough calls" — maybe. Or maybe they're walking into every call from scarcity and no amount of volume fixes that. Nobody wants to sell identity work because you can't put it in a module with a clever name. So they sell activity instead.
Most sales trainers have never been in a real high-ticket conversation
Most people selling sales training got a few clips from one or two real $30-50K calls and built an entire brand on it. The courses are recycled — NEPQ is pacing and leading with extra steps. 40 coaches teaching the same script, calling it their proprietary method. Same thing, different colored box.
The less you try to close, the more you close
The industry is built on pressure — build momentum, overcome every objection, make them feel the pain until they move. The best sales JJ has ever made didn't feel like selling. The prospect sold themselves; his job was to reflect their reality back clearly, then get out of the way.

The Objection Reframe
"I need to think about it" / "I can't afford it" — what's actually underneath it.
What they say

The word underneath it is afford. They say it like a fact — it's not, it's a decision disguised as a math problem.

When someone says they can't afford something, what they're usually saying is: "I don't fully believe the gap between where I am and where I could be is real enough to justify this."

The money usually exists. The certainty doesn't.

What Resonance Actually Sounds Like
Proof content is landing — for social proof posts or explaining JJ's own process.
After a talk or session"I've been trying to figure out how to say that for two years and you just said it in one sentence."
Quoting it back word for word"That thing you said about the person who needs the sale the most." / "When you said you'd rather lose the sale than lose the frame."
The strongest signal"I had a call exactly like that last week." — when what you said becomes the container for someone else's experience, that's when it actually landed.
