Setter Levers

As a setter, your funnel has 4 places it can break. You're never broken in all of them at once. Find the one that's costing you the most — that's your lever.

Your 4 Levers
01
Connect Rate
Are people actually picking up?
If Low
What to fix
Dial volume — you might just not be reaching enough people. More dials, more connects.
Time of day — most people pick up in the morning or late afternoon. Are you dialing in dead windows?
Opener — if people are picking up and immediately hanging up, your first line is the problem. How you open determines if they stay.
Platform — cold call, DM, email, SMS — not all channels perform the same for all audiences. Test what the list responds to.
02
Booking Rate
Are connects turning into appointments?
If Low
What to fix
Your pitch — are you giving them a compelling reason to show up? The set is a mini-sale. Treat it like one.
Qualifying — are you booking everyone or the right people? A lower booking rate with higher show quality can still be a win.
Urgency building — if there's no reason to show up tomorrow instead of "whenever," they won't. Build a specific reason to be there.
03
No-Show Rate
Are booked calls actually happening?
If High
What to fix
Confirmation sequence — are you following up before the call? A text the night before and the morning of changes everything.
Commitment level — did they actually commit, or just say yes to get off the phone? Real commitment sounds different. Get them to verbally confirm what they're showing up for.
Rapport in the set — people cancel on strangers. They show up for people they feel connected to. The relationship starts in the set, not on the call.
04
Show-Up Quality
Are the people who show up actually qualified?
If Low
What to fix
Questions before booking — if unqualified people keep showing up, you're not filtering hard enough on the front end. Your pre-booking questions need to create a barrier, not just fill a calendar.
What you're promising in the set — if you're over-promising or under-explaining what the call is, you'll attract the wrong people. Get clear on who this call is and isn't for.
The Rule
You can only be broken in one or two of these at a time. Don't try to fix all four. Pull your last 30 days of data, find where the drop-off is largest, and go to work on that one thing. Everything else will follow.
Find Your Lever — 3 Steps
01
Pull your numbers: dials, connects, bookings, shows, no-shows. Calculate the conversion rate between each stage.
02
Find the biggest percentage drop between two consecutive stages. That's where your funnel breaks.
03
Match that stage to the lever above. That's your one thing for Phase 2. Write it down and bring it to your next session.
