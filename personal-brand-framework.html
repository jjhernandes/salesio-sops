The Core Rule

Build your brand around you — not your business

Most people post "value content" for their ideal client and wonder why nothing converts. The people who win build around who they are as a person. People follow you first, then buy what you sell.

Group A — Commodity Brand
  • Posts value content for their ideal client
  • Focuses on being informative over authentic
  • No genuine connection with the audience
  • Ends up competing on price
  • Wonders why nobody buys
Group B — Personal Brand
  • Posts a mix of authentic and authority content
  • Builds the brand around themselves, not the business
  • Audience engages and buys
  • Commands premium pricing
  • Audience follows because of who they are

The Objection Reframe & What Landing Sounds Like
The Objection Reframe

"I can't afford it"

The word underneath it is afford — said like a fact, but it's a decision disguised as a math problem. What they usually mean: "I don't fully believe the gap between where I am and where I could be is real enough to justify this." The money usually exists. The certainty doesn't.

What Landing Sounds Like

Proof it's working

Not "that was valuable." People quote it back word for word — "the person who needs the sale the most" — or bring their own story to it: "I had a call exactly like that last week." That's when it actually got in.

4 Pillars
Define these before posting anything.
Beliefs

What makes you one of one

Your values. How you see the world. The unique stories and experiences that shape how you think. This is what differentiates you at the core from every other creator online. See JJ's Voice for the concrete version.

Style

How you show up visually and verbally

How you talk. What you wear. Where you film. Camera quality, editing, captions, colors. All of it needs to be decided at the start.

Mission

Where you're going

If you want to be followed, you need to be going somewhere. What are you building? What are you trying to prove, achieve, or create? What does success look like to you?

Mechanism

How you make money

What is your offer? What is your business model? What is unique or different about how you get clients results? This is how you monetize the brand you're building.

Dream Follower vs. Ideal Client
Two different targets — know which one your content is for.

Don't make content for your ideal client. Make it for your dream follower — the larger audience who wants to become a version of who you represent. Your ideal client is a subset of that group: same person, but they also have a specific problem, know they have it, and can pay to solve it.

Dream Follower

Questions to answer

  • Who are they and what's their story?
  • What are they passionate about?
  • What are their goals and desires?
  • Who do they want to become?
  • What are their deepest fears?
  • What are their deepest struggles?
Content Ideas

The interest overlap

Content ideas live in the overlap between your interests and your dream follower's interests. The better you define both, the more clarity you have on exactly what to post. That Venn diagram center is where all the best content lives.

3 Content Types
Every piece of content is doing one of three jobs.
Top of Funnel

Audience Building

Designed to attract, not convert. Grabs attention, sparks emotion, reaches people who have never seen you. Stops the scroll. Creates curiosity. Not meant to sell.

Middle of Funnel

Brand Building

For people already following you. Positions you as the expert, deepens trust, shows your personality and values. Turns a casual follower into a loyal fan who believes in your message.

Bottom of Funnel

Client Building

Gets warm audience to take action. Comment a keyword, join your email list, DM you about your offer. This is the content that actually monetizes the brand.

2 Content Themes
Everything you post is either authenticity or authority.
Authenticity

Who you are

Your personality, values, mission, and why. The real, unpolished version of you. The person you are when no one's watching. This builds connection and likability. Lead with this.

Authority

What you've done

Your experience, results, and lifestyle. Proof of your skill set in action. The money you've made. The outcomes you've created. This builds credibility and demand. Comes after authenticity.

Diagnosing Content Problems
Match the symptom to the fix.
Problem 1

Low views

Post more audience building content. Test different formats to see what performs best with your audience, then double down on that.

Problem 2

Decent views, low engagement

Post more authenticity-based content that's highly relatable. Make people feel like "this person gets me." Engagement follows emotional connection.

Problem 3

Decent engagement, low sales

Post more authority content and more calls to action. Give your audience more opportunities to raise their hand and say they're interested. You're not asking enough.

4 Metrics That Actually Matter
Track these, not vanity views.
Tracks audience building content

Follows per 1K views

More views doesn't mean more follows. Knowing which videos drive the most follows tells you what audience building content to keep making.

Tracks brand building content

Genuine comments

Shows which videos created real emotional connection. Use this to dial in brand building content — the videos that make people feel seen.

Tracks client building content

Saves

A save means your audience found it valuable enough to come back to. Post more of these with a CTA at the end and you'll convert more clients.

Tracks both audience + brand building

Shares

A share means it resonated deeply enough that someone sent it to another person. Great for reach. Signals content worth doubling down on.
