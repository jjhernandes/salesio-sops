The 5-Point Call Review Framework

Unstructured tape review produces vague observations. This framework gives you five specific checkpoints, a scoring system, and a clear output for every call you review.

The Framework — What It Is and Why It Works

A call has five distinct phases. Each one either builds or erodes the close. Most reps have one or two that are solid and two or three that consistently let them down. The framework forces you to evaluate all five — every time — so you can't miss the phase that's actually causing your results.

Random tape review produces random insight. Structured review produces a pattern. Once you have a pattern across five calls, you know exactly what to fix.

Point Phase The Core Question
01 Opener Did you establish authority and get them talking in the first 90 seconds?
02 Discovery Did you go deep enough to find the real pain, not just the surface story?
03 Pitch Was your pitch connected to what they actually said in discovery?
04 Objection Handling Did you use questions or arguments when they pushed back?
05 Close Did you hold the frame through the end, or did you concede before being asked?

The Scoring Rubric — 1, 2, or 3

Each point gets a score of 1, 2, or 3. One number per phase. No half-points, no averages. Force yourself to choose.

Score Key
1 — Broke Down
2 — Adequate
3 — Strong

Score 1: the phase actively hurt the call. Score 2: the phase was present but not impactful. Score 3: the phase did what it was supposed to do. You're not grading on a curve.

Phase Score 1 — Broke Down Score 3 — Strong
Opener Small talk rambled. No clear authority established. Prospect controlling the direction immediately. Took the frame early. Prospect sharing within 60 seconds. Agenda clear without being stiff.
Discovery Stayed surface-level. Accepted first answer. Never got to the real pain. Got to third-layer pain. Prospect feeling understood. Clear emotional driver surfaced.
Pitch Generic pitch. Could have been sent to anyone. No direct connection to what they shared. Prospect's own words used back. Pitch landed as "exactly what I need," not "sounds interesting."
Objection Handling Argued back. Tried to logically overcome. Prospect got more entrenched. Got curious. Asked what was behind the objection. Prospect talked themselves into movement.
Close Conceded before being pushed. Rushed to the follow-up. Frame lost under minimal pressure. Held the frame. Silence comfortable. Prospect felt certain because rep was certain.

Complete this after every tape review. One worksheet per call. Keep them. The pattern across 5 worksheets tells you more than any individual call.

One Thing to Change
Based on this review, what is the single thing you are changing on your next call?
Be specific. Not "be more present" — that's too vague. "Hold the silence for 5 seconds after stating the price" — that's actionable. Write the one thing.

Using the Framework Over Time

One worksheet is insight. Five worksheets is a pattern. The framework's real power shows up when you stack reviews side by side and ask: which point am I consistently scoring 1 or 2 on? That consistent low score is your ceiling. That's where to focus all your drilling.

The Pattern Check (Every 5 Calls)

Pull your last five worksheets. Add up the scores for each point across all five calls. The lowest total score across five calls is where your current ceiling lives. That's what you drill. That's what you submit for coaching feedback. That's what you mock-call on.

"You can't fix everything at once. Find the one thing that's consistently broken and fix that. Then find the next one. That's how elite reps are built — one bottleneck at a time."

— JJ Hernandez
