The Four States

What must be active before you pitch. Pitching before all four are present creates resistance. Your job in discovery is to activate each one.

The Framework

Before a prospect can say yes to anything, four internal states must be active simultaneously. If even one is missing, you will feel it at the close. The pitch will land on resistance you cannot explain — because the resistance was built during discovery, not during the pitch.

The four states are: Dissatisfaction, Desire, Possibility, Attention.

Your job in discovery is not to ask questions for information. It is to build these four states. The information is a byproduct.

D
State One
Dissatisfaction
The prospect must feel genuinely dissatisfied with where they are right now. Not vaguely aware that things could be better. Actually dissatisfied — frustrated, uncomfortable, tired of the same result. Without this, the prospect has no real reason to change. They will listen to your pitch intellectually and then not move.
Signal it's present: "I'm just sick of it. I keep trying but nothing actually changes." Signal it's missing: "Yeah, things are fine. I just thought I'd explore options."
D
State Two
Desire
The prospect must have a clear, emotionally charged picture of where they want to be. Not just a vague hope. A specific future state they can feel. Desire is the magnet that pulls them toward the close. Without a vivid desired future, they have nothing to move toward — only something to move away from, which is weaker.
Signal it's present: "I want to be at $30K months and be able to take my family on a real vacation." Signal it's missing: "I just want to make more money, I guess."
P
State Three
Possibility
The prospect must believe that the change is actually achievable for them specifically. Not just achievable in theory. Many prospects intellectually want change but do not believe it is possible for someone in their situation. This is the hidden objection that kills closes. It never shows up as "I don't think I can do it" — it shows up as "let me think about it."
Signal it's present: "If someone with my background has actually done it, that changes things." Signal it's missing: "Yeah but those results seem like they're for people who already have an advantage."
A
State Four
Attention
The prospect must be fully present. Not distracted, not going through the motions, not half-listening while they multitask. Attention is what makes the pitch actually register. You can have the perfect pitch delivered to a mind that is elsewhere and it will produce nothing. Attention is engineered through vivid, specific, emotionally resonant questions.
Signal it's present: the prospect is asking follow-up questions, going long in their answers, leaning into the conversation. Signal it's missing: short, clipped answers, distractions audible, "yeah" as the only response.

What Happens When You Pitch Before All Four Are Active

Pitching without Dissatisfaction: The prospect sees the logic of your offer but feels no urgency to act. "That sounds interesting. Let me think about it." They are not dissatisfied enough to change anything.

Pitching without Desire: The prospect knows they want out of where they are but has no clear picture of where they are going. Your pitch is solving a problem that does not yet have a destination. Without the destination, there is nothing to anchor the decision to.

Pitching without Possibility: The prospect nods through your pitch and thinks "that sounds great but that's not for someone like me." This is the most dangerous missing state because it is invisible. The prospect will not tell you this. They will give you a surface objection instead.

Pitching without Attention: The prospect has physically been on the call but has not been mentally present. Your pitch is landing on someone who is already formulating their response or thinking about their next appointment. The words register but they do not land.

Diagnosing Which State Is Missing Mid-Call

Missing State What It Looks Like How to Activate It
Dissatisfaction Answers are flat, no emotional weight, talks about the problem without frustration Implication questions — "What has staying here cost you?" "How long has this been the pattern?"
Desire Vague statements about wanting more, cannot articulate a specific future clearly Future projection questions — "Walk me through what your life looks like at that number." "What changes first?"
Possibility Deflects to luck, background, or privilege when hearing about results — "yeah but..." Identity-matched social proof — share results of someone in their exact situation, then ask "what would it mean if that was actually possible for you?"
Attention Short answers, slow responses, background noise, checking out during questions Ask a specific, unexpected question that requires real thought. "Here's what I'm noticing — what's actually most on your mind right now?"

Signals That Tell You a Prospect Is Ready to Hear the Pitch

  • ✓
    They start asking about the offer without being prompted. "So what does the program actually look like?" This is a desire signal. They are already moving toward you.
  • ✓
    Their answers start going longer and more specific. Short answers mean low engagement. Long, emotionally specific answers mean all four states are activating. They are processing in real time.
  • ✓
    They reference other people or past experiences with negative emotion. "I tried X and it didn't work and I'm honestly frustrated." Dissatisfaction is present. They are motivated to not repeat the past.
  • ✓
    They confirm the future state unprompted. "I just know that at $30K months everything would be different." Desire is active. They have their future in their mind already.
  • ✓
    They ask "has anyone like me actually done this?" This is a possibility request. Address it directly with a specific, identity-matched example and the state will activate.

Call Exchange — Checking the States Before Pitching

Rep Checking All Four States Before Transitioning to the Pitch
Rep
"Before I share what we do — I want to make sure I'm actually understanding where you're at. You mentioned you've been stuck at this ceiling for about a year. How does that sit with you honestly — like what does it feel like to be there?"
Checking for Dissatisfaction. Inviting emotional articulation before moving forward.
Prospect
"Honestly? It's frustrating. I know I'm capable of more. I see people around me doing what I want to do and I can't figure out what's different about them."
Rep
"And if you got to the other side of that — if that version of you was actually real — what does life look like? Walk me through a specific month at that number."
Checking for Desire. Getting specific and activating the future-self picture.
Prospect
"I'd be fully remote, traveling, not stressed about whether a deal closes. My wife could stop working if she wanted to. That's what it would mean."
Rep
"One of the guys we worked with last quarter was in almost this exact spot — $8K months, stuck, had tried to figure it out alone. He hit $27K in month two. What would it mean for you if that was actually possible in your situation?"
Checking for and activating Possibility through identity-matched social proof followed by a direct possibility question.
Prospect
"If that's actually real? That changes everything. That's what I've been looking for."
All four states are now active. This is the moment to transition to the pitch. The prospect is fully prepared to hear it.
