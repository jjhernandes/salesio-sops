Feedback on Jess's Prior Roleplay

JJ rewatched Jess's earlier solo roleplay (twice — once by coincidence while prepping for an unrelated in-person fitness client, once with his fiancée, who loved her energy enough to say she'd buy from her). That reaction is exactly the risk he wanted to flag.

  • Jess's warmth and easygoing energy are authentically her — genuine strength, not something to suppress
  • But with more sophisticated buyers or at the objection stage, pure friendliness can undercut her — prospects sense the shift to a "neutral, firmer" tone as jarring if the whole call has been soft
  • Specific moment flagged: when the prospect said something like "it's been tough, I don't feel like myself," Jess laughed lightly and moved past it ("we'll totally figure it out") instead of sitting in that discomfort — that's exactly where the real emotion lives
  • The fix isn't to be cold — it's "serious" in the sense that health conversations deserve real weight, which is what creates urgency and value perception
Respect First, Likability Second
Sales is a response to authority — and authority is composure and neutrality, not domineering control. Humans are wired to follow "the most followable person" in the room. Reference point: Layla — not the friendliest on the surface, very direct, but people respect her first and then discover the warmth, which lands much harder than warmth offered upfront. Gain respect in the first few minutes, let personality and charisma show after that's established — not before.

The Sales.io Framework — Current → Future
01
Current Reality
02
Problem Aware
03
Solution Aware
04
Future Pace
  • Current Reality — grasp what's actually happening now. Watch for subjective language ("tough," "lost," "not myself") and always follow up with "what do you mean by that"
  • Problem Aware — of the 5 buyer levels (unaware → problem aware → solution aware → product aware → decision ready), get them to name the problem themselves rather than being told what it is
  • Solution Aware — establish why this coach specifically, not just that coaching in general is the answer. In a niche this personal, relatability beats generic authority — memorable wins over persuasive
  • Future Pace — make the outcome vivid and tangible (not surface-level imagination) before pitching
The Core Principle
Humans decide on emotion and justify with logic. Get to the real emotion — even shooting from the hip, off-framework, like Jess did today — and you can win the call. The framework isn't required if you can find the emotion naturally; it exists to make the outcome repeatable when you can't. Never pitch unless you're already confident they'll say yes — decisions are rooted in clarity, "no" is rooted in fear or confusion, and financing options can dissolve the fear once it's identified.
