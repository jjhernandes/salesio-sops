The Integration Mock Call

The capstone practice session. Every framework you've learned must be running simultaneously. This is not a drill for one skill — this is the whole thing at once.

What Integration Actually Means

Most reps can run frameworks one at a time. They can hold frame when they're thinking about holding frame. They can go deep in discovery when they remind themselves to go deep. They can deliver a connected pitch when that's what they're focused on.

Integration means all of it is running at the same time without deliberate effort. You're not thinking "go deeper." You're just curious. You're not thinking "hold frame." You're just grounded. The frameworks stop being techniques and become expressions of who you are on the call.

The Gap This Exposes

Most reps discover in the integration mock that they can only run two or three frameworks simultaneously before something falls apart. They get deep in discovery and lose track of frame. They pitch well and then concede on the close. They hold frame through objections and forget to use the prospect's own language.

That gap — between running frameworks one at a time and running all of them simultaneously — is the difference between a good rep and an elite one.

The Scoring Rubric — All Frameworks Present

The integration mock is reviewed against every dimension simultaneously. Coach scores each in real time during the call, then reviews with the rep afterward.

Structure — The 12 Steps
Were all 12 steps of the Sales.io call structure present?
Did the call move through every phase? Were any skipped? Which step was weakest?
Missing Partial Complete
Influence — S7 Principles Running
Were the influence principles from Section 7 present throughout the call?
Social proof, cognitive dissonance, identity-reality gap — did they appear naturally or not at all?
Absent Forced Natural
Frame — Intact Throughout
Was the frame held from opener to close, including through all objections?
Note every moment the frame wavered. At what point, if any, did the rep concede the advisor position?
Broke Down Wavered Held
State — Above 200 Throughout
Was the rep's emotional state above the line of reason (not fear, not desperation) for the whole call?
Using Dr. Hawkins' scale as reference: 200+ means courage and above. Below 200 is fear, shame, guilt, apathy — states that leak through tone and kill trust. Was the rep above 200 the whole call?
Below 200 Fluctuating Above 200
Discovery — Third Layer Reached
Did the rep reach at least the third layer of the prospect's pain before pitching?
Layer 1: surface statement. Layer 2: why that matters. Layer 3: what it costs them — emotionally, financially, relationally. Did the pitch connect to layer 3 content?
Layer 1 Only Layer 2 Layer 3+
Overall Integration Score
Across all dimensions — how integrated was this call?
Integration 3 means all frameworks ran simultaneously without the rep visibly thinking about them. Integration 1 means frameworks were isolated or absent. This is the most important single score.
1 — Not Integrated 2 — Partial 3 — Fully Integrated

What Integration Feels Like

Running Frameworks One at a Time Full Integration
You're thinking about the next step while the prospect is talking. Missing what they're saying. You're fully present. The frameworks are background. You're just in a conversation.
When you focus on discovery depth, your frame gets soft. One or the other. Deep discovery and solid frame are happening simultaneously. Not a trade-off.
Transitions feel mechanical. "Now I should go to the pitch." Transitions feel natural. The call moves because the conversation moves, not because a script says so.
You notice the prospect's emotions after the fact. Reacting late. You're reading the prospect in real time. Adjusting your approach inside the moment.
The Integration Feeling

When full integration is happening, the call feels easy in a way that's hard to explain. Not easy because the prospect isn't pushing back — easy because you're not fighting yourself anymore. You're not managing your frameworks. You're just there. The close feels natural because the whole call built toward it.

That's what you're training for. Not a better technique — a different way of being on a call.

Why This Separates Good Reps from Elite Ones

Good reps have strong frameworks. Elite reps have integrated frameworks. The difference is not technique — it's bandwidth. When you're not spending cognitive energy managing your process, that bandwidth goes to the prospect. And what the prospect feels is someone who is entirely, genuinely with them.

That's what connection feels like from the other side. Not a technique. Not a rapport tactic. Someone who is fully present and completely certain — because all the internal noise is gone.

  • !
    The integration mock is not for beginners. Run this after the individual framework mocks and Frame Attack Drill have been completed. Integration cannot happen if the component skills aren't built yet.
  • ✓
    Run one integration mock per week in the final 4 weeks of the program. This is the calibration check — all systems running together before going fully live with the skills.
  • ✓
    Record and review every integration mock. The integration score is what you're tracking. A consistent score of 3 across all dimensions means you're integrated. That's when the real calls get easy.
  • ✗
    Don't grade yourself on outcome in the integration mock. Whether you "closed" the mock prospect is irrelevant. Score the integration. The close is a byproduct of integrated execution — not the measure of it.
