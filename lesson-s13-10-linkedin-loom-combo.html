The LinkedIn + Loom Combo

The message sequence that gets replies. Personalization in message one earns the open in message two. The Loom earns the call. Three messages that most hiring managers actually respond to.

Why the Sequence Matters

Sending a Loom in your first message is a mistake. The person doesn't know you, they didn't ask for a video, and they're not going to click a link from someone they've never engaged with. You need to earn the watch before you ask for it.

The sequence earns the right to the Loom. Message 1 is human and specific — it gets a response. Message 2 delivers the Loom inside a context where they're already open to hearing from you.

The principle is identical to the sales call: Earn the right before you present. You wouldn't pitch in the first minute of a discovery call. Don't pitch in the first message of a DM sequence either.

The 3-Message Sequence

Message 1 — Connect and be specific
Hey [First name] — saw you're building out [specific thing you found on their profile / content / company]. Looks like you're going after [market or ICP they're targeting]. Love the approach.

I'm a closer who's been trained in scriptless high-ticket sales. Would love to learn more about what you're building — would a quick conversation make sense?
Keep it under 80 words. The specificity is the key — it proves you actually looked at them, not just blasted a template. No pitch. No mention of the Loom. Just genuine curiosity.
Message 2 — Deliver the Loom (after they reply)
Appreciate the response. I put together a quick 60-second video on why I think I'd be a strong fit for what you're building — rather than just send a resume, I figured you'd get more from actually hearing me.

[Loom link]

Curious what you think. Happy to jump on a call if it seems worth exploring.
The framing matters: "rather than just send a resume" — positions the Loom as a better choice, not just an outreach tactic. Keep this message short. The Loom does the work.
Message 3 — If no reply to the Loom after 2-3 days
Just wanted to make sure the video didn't get buried — [Loom link] — around 60 seconds. No pressure either way. Just think there could be something worth a quick conversation about.
Light, non-needy, confident. One follow-up on the Loom. If there's no reply after this, move to the standard follow-up protocol (see Lesson 11).

Finding the Right Person to Message

  • For smaller offers (founder-led): Message the founder directly. Search the company name on LinkedIn, look at the team, find the person with "Founder," "CEO," or "Head of Sales" in the title.
  • For larger companies: Go to "Sales Director," "VP of Sales," or "Head of Closers." Not HR — they don't know what they're looking for in a closer and they can't evaluate you.
  • Read their profile before you message. Pull one specific thing — a post they made, a result they mentioned, something about the offer. Generic first messages get ignored. The specificity is the entire reason this sequence works.

Timing and Cadence

Don't message 20 companies in one day. You can't write genuinely personalized first messages at that volume — and generic messages get ignored. Three to five genuine, specific outreaches per day is more productive than twenty templated ones. Quality compounds. Noise doesn't.

Best days to send Message 1: Tuesday through Thursday, morning. People check LinkedIn in the morning and on weekdays. Monday morning is often too busy. Friday afternoon is too close to the weekend.
