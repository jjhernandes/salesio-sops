Handoff Protocol

The handoff is where most setting-to-closing systems break down. The closer should never walk into a call cold. If they do, the setter didn't finish the job.

Where the System Breaks Down

The single most common failure point in a setter-closer system is the gap between "booked" and "briefed." The setter gets the call on the calendar and moves on. The closer shows up, opens Close CRM, sees a name with no notes, and has to start the call in the dark.

Starting a call in the dark costs 5-10 minutes of rapport-building time that should have been spent on real discovery. In a 45-minute call, that's significant. The closer is re-establishing context that the setter already has. The prospect has to repeat themselves. Nobody wins.

What a Missing Handoff Costs the Closer

The closer's first 60 seconds on a call sets the tone for the entire conversation. If they spend those 60 seconds asking the prospect to re-explain who they are and why they booked, they've started from zero and the prospect feels like they're talking to someone who doesn't know them.

A proper handoff means the closer can open with something specific: "Marcus, I heard you've been closing in the health coaching space for about a year and you're working through the 'I need to think about it' objection — that's actually a really fixable thing." That opener takes 10 seconds and builds instant connection. It's only possible if the setter left a real note.

What the Closer Needs Before Every Call

  • ✓
    Full name and how they found us. LinkedIn outreach, cold call, referral, inbound. Context on how they came in shapes how the closer frames their approach.
  • ✓
    Current situation summary. What do they do, how long have they been doing it, what are the key numbers (close rate, income, offer type, niche). This is what the closer uses to open the call with specificity.
  • ✓
    What was said in the set. The specific pain they revealed. The exact words they used, if memorable. This is the anchor the closer comes back to throughout the call.
  • ✓
    Qualification level. Did they confirm budget capacity? Is there a second decision maker? What's the urgency level? Any objections that came up during the set and how they were handled?
  • ✓
    Energy and tone of the conversation. Were they excited, cautious, skeptical, or just curious? Was there resistance or did it flow easily? The closer's emotional read on the prospect before the call starts directly affects how they open.
  • ✗
    What does NOT count as a handoff: A name in Close with no notes. "He seemed interested." A generic "qualified lead" tag with no context. These give the closer nothing to work with.

The Handoff Format — Close CRM Note

The Standard

Every booked call gets a structured note in Close CRM before the call starts. No exceptions. This is entered by the setter as soon as the call is confirmed — not the day of, not an hour before. As soon as it's booked.

Close CRM Handoff Note — Template
NAME
Marcus Thompson
SOURCE
LinkedIn DM outreach — setter initiated
SITUATION
Remote closer, health coaching niche, 9 months in. Currently at 18% close rate. Has a consistent income (~$5-7K/mo based on conversation). Not pre-revenue.
PAIN POINT (THEIR WORDS)
"I fold whenever they say they need to think about it. My manager says I'm leaving 40% of my closes on the table."
QUALIFICATION
Budget: confirmed willing to invest. Decision maker: solo. Timeline: actively looking now. Past programs: tried one generic program, didn't work because "all theory, no real coaching."
ENERGY / TONE
Self-aware, a little skeptical due to past program experience, but genuinely motivated. Warmed up through the conversation. Came in cautious, left curious.
SETTER NOTES
Address the past program skepticism early — he's worried about getting theory instead of real coaching. Differentiate on the call review and active coaches angle. Don't oversell early.

What a Bad Handoff Does to the First 60 Seconds

A closer who walks in cold has to use the opening of the call to gather information the setter already had. This means the prospect has to repeat themselves, the closer loses the opportunity to open with something personal and specific, and the relationship starts from scratch instead of from where the setter left it.

No Handoff Note Complete Handoff Note
"Hey Marcus, thanks for hopping on. So tell me a bit about yourself and what you're looking to work on." "Marcus — I heard you've been in the health coaching space for about a year, closing around 18%, and that the main thing is what happens when prospects say they need to think about it. That right?"
Prospect spends 5 minutes re-explaining their situation. Feels like a cold call, not a warm referral. Prospect says "yeah exactly." Feels heard before the call even really starts. Trust is already building.
Closer has no angle, no anchor, no specific pain to return to. Call is generic. Closer knows the pain, the skepticism about past programs, and the right angle to differentiate. Call is targeted from the first minute.

Exchange — The Handoff That Changes the First 60 Seconds

SCENARIO — Closer Opening with a Complete Handoff Note
Closer
"Marcus — appreciate you jumping on. I got some context from Jalen before this. You've been closing in the health coaching space for about nine months, running around 18%, and the main thing breaking down is when prospects say they need more time to think — that right?"
Opens with everything from the handoff note. No "tell me about yourself." The prospect immediately feels heard — and didn't have to say a word yet.
Prospect
"Yeah, exactly. That's the biggest thing. I just — I don't know how to handle it without feeling like I'm being pushy."
Already in the real conversation. 45 seconds in. Discovery didn't waste 10 minutes getting to this point.
Closer
"The pushiness feeling is actually where we need to start — because that feeling is not about what you're saying. It's about what you believe about yourself when you're asking. Tell me more about what happens in your head when they say that."
The handoff enabled a precise, high-trust opening. The closer is already working the real problem. The call will close at a higher rate because the first minute didn't get wasted on logistics.
