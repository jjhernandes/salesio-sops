Before You Dial

Pre-Call Prep

Do this before every presentation call. Walking in prepared is what separates a consultant from a salesperson.

The single most important thing you can do before this call: build the financial case for their specific situation. Take their current setup — capital sitting in BTL or cash — and show them side by side what this deal does instead. Make the maths unavoidable.
Step 1 · 3–5 min

Reconnect & Recap

Start by showing you listened. Mirror their situation back before you show them anything. This earns you the room.

3 – 5 minutes
Opening

"[Name], good to be back. Before I walk you through what I have put together — I want to make sure nothing has shifted since we last spoke. When we talked you mentioned [their main frustration], and the core goal was [their stated goal — income / capital protection / tax / legacy]. Is that still the right frame for today, or has anything changed?"

Why this works: It re-anchors them on the problem they described in their own words before they see the solution. The contrast between where they are and where they want to be is what creates genuine interest — not the pitch.
Discovery Personalisation Prompts
If they had BTL frustrations → reference the specific ones they mentioned (tenant issues, Renters Rights Act, yield drop)
If they mentioned tax as a concern → flag that the first thing you want to cover is structuring
If they have capital sitting idle → acknowledge it: "You mentioned [£X] is ready to move. I want to make sure we look at the right opportunity for that amount."
If their adviser is on the call → acknowledge them directly and confirm their role

Step 6 · 5–8 min

The Risk Question

Raise this before they do. Analytical investors will have this question. Addressing it proactively builds more trust than waiting for them to ask.

5 – 8 minutes
Raise it first

"Before we go further, I want to address the question I know you are thinking about. What happens if the housing association goes bust? It is a fair question and I would rather you asked it now than thought about it later. The answer is more robust than most people expect."

The Legal Protection

The Housing and Planning Act 2016 (Part 4, Chapter 5) was built specifically for this scenario. A housing association cannot be wound up without 28 days notice to the Regulator of Social Housing. The Regulator must consent before any winding up. A housing administrator can be appointed whose sole job is protecting income and residents. The Secretary of State can provide grants and guarantees to ensure continuity.

Who Steps In: MYSHON

MYSHON manages over 11,000 homes across the UK with 150 local authority partnerships. When One CIC went into administration in August 2024, MYSHON stepped in within 72 hours. Services continued. Income kept flowing. The system worked exactly as the legislation intended.

Three Real Cases That Prove the System Works
One CIC (2024) — into administration, MYSHON stepped in day three, zero income interruption
Cosmopolitan Housing Group (2012) — £100M+ liabilities, Regulator intervened, Sanctuary Group stepped in, zero residents lost their home. Triggered the Housing Administration Regime.
Ujima Housing Association (2007) — only housing association in English history to fully collapse. Government stepped in, stock transferred to L&Q Group, all 5,000+ families protected. Triggered the Housing and Regeneration Act 2008.
The institutional signal: Canada Life, Aviva, BlackRock, Henley Investment Management, and the Universities Superannuation Scheme have all committed hundreds of millions to this sector. USS completed a £405M deal in 2024 — the largest pension-backed affordable housing transaction in UK history. These are the most risk-averse institutions in the world. They have looked at this sector and committed.

Step 9 · 5–10 min

The Close

Anchor on value before asking. Then ask once, clearly, and wait. Never end the call without a decision or a specific committed next step.

5 – 10 minutes
Anchor before asking

"Before I walk you through what the next step looks like — based on everything we have covered today, and what you told me about [their goal], does this look like the kind of investment that makes sense for your position?"

Wait for their answer. Do not fill the silence. Whatever they say next is the real conversation.
If yes

"The next step is to reserve the asset. That takes it off the market while we complete the structuring review and instruct solicitors. I can get that moving today — shall I send over what you need to review?"

If maybe — find the real blocker

"What is the one thing that needs to be answered before you can move forward? I want to make sure I am not leaving you with an unanswered question."

If they need to involve their adviser

"That is exactly the right approach at this level. Would it make sense to get 30 minutes on the calendar with the three of us so I can answer their questions directly? That usually moves things faster and means nothing gets lost in the handoff. When are they typically available?"

Never leave without a committed next step. Either a decision, a booked three-way call with their adviser, or a specific date to reconnect — with a reason and a calendar invite before they leave the Zoom.
Creating real urgency

"I want to be straight with you — this specific asset has had interest from two other investors who have been through a similar process. I cannot hold it indefinitely without a reservation in place. I am not saying that to pressure you — I am saying it because I would rather you had the full picture."
