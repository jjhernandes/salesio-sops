Lesson 1

Map of Consciousness

Framework — David Hawkins
Where you operate determines what you're capable of
Source: Letting Go by David Hawkins — referenced by JJ
Shame / Guilt
Lowest. Usually tied to deep identity wounds, dishonesty, hiding.
Where liars and people lacking integrity typically operate
Apathy / Fear
Stuck. Afraid of not growing, not landing a role, not being enough. Paralysis.
Common entry state for struggling reps
Desire
Push mechanism. Wanting more money, wanting to be better than everyone. Can drive results but collapses at the top.
Can reach 30–50K/mo, then loses the why
Anger
Also a push mechanism. Fuel, but burns dirty. Similar ceiling to desire — sustainable only so long.
Most sales reps cycle here
Pride
JJ: "Genuinely the worst." Blocks all coaching. Person is right even when they're wrong. JJ has cut students operating here.
Heavy, low energy. Coachability = zero
Courage
First level above the line. Willingness to act, to be wrong, to try.
Where growth becomes possible
Neutrality
Things happen, you process them without spiraling. Not numb — grounded.
Reason ← Kevin
Kevin's self-identified baseline. Lost a sale? There's a reason. Bad day? A reason. Nothing becomes purely emotional — it becomes logical and traceable.
Kevin's default mode — JJ confirmed this
Acceptance
Also Kevin's range. Things are as they are. No need to fight or inflate.
Love ← JJ / Jalen
Genuine care for the person in front of you. Felt energetically, not performed. What makes prospects say "this feels different."
JJ's target operating state on calls
Kevin's profile: Rare. 99% of sales reps operate at Pride, Anger, Desire, or Fear. Kevin is already at Reason and Acceptance — which means he loses sales without emotional spiraling, processes feedback without defensiveness, and is naturally coachable. His biggest down-trigger is dishonesty from others (brings him from Reason toward Anger) — and crucially, he does not tie his self-worth to his commission numbers. JJ flagged this as one of the most common failure patterns in reps he coaches.
Coaching Moments

What was worked through

01
Don't tie your value to your commissions
JJ asked what brings Kevin up and down on the map. Kevin didn't mention his numbers once. JJ noticed this and called it out — because most reps do the opposite: low numbers = low self-worth = low energy = worse calls = lower numbers.
"We cannot attribute our value to our commissions and the number in our bank account. Even when I was broke, I wasn't much different than the man I am now."
JJ Hernandez
JJ's point: money reveals and amplifies, it doesn't create. The things that make him laugh, how he connects with people — that didn't change with income. The peace comes from not needing to keep up with the Joneses. Kevin already lives this way. That's a real edge.
02
Environment shapes your operating level
JJ's office: he walked into the first (smaller, cheaper) space and immediately said "I'm not working here." Paid more for windows and better energy. The $2K/month upgrade was worth it — zero distractions, no TV, no desk yet (still on a stool), but the energy is right.
For Kevin, who has worked remotely his whole career and is comfortable in his setup: the bigger environmental shift comes from the Sales.io community itself — coaching calls, people on the same path, connection. That replaces the office energy for someone working from home.
"If you come here and you're inspired by what's happening around you, your energy's always going to be higher. Every single time."
JJ Hernandez
Kevin's home environment is coming back up after some personal house-related things. Dogs, wife, kids on calls — all elevate him. The community is the next layer.
03
Who you are off the call is who you are on the call
Kevin's time and attention is focused professionally — follows through on work tasks, has had that discipline even working from home with young kids. Personally, lower-urgency items (like moving furniture) take a while. JJ flagged the connection:
"Who you are on your sales calls is who you are off your sales calls."
JJ Hernandez
Not a criticism — just awareness. The discipline Kevin has for follow-up and professional work is the same discipline that makes him reliable on calls. The personal drift is something to watch as it can bleed in at the margins.
04
Script-based offer — how Kevin's handling it
Kevin's new offer runs off a very strict script — close to fully read. He's figured out the setup: script minimized on the monitor behind Zoom so it's not visible. He's getting inflection and tonality in despite reading.
JJ asked to review the mock call Kevin did with the offer owner (2 parts). The goal: confirm whether the reading is detectable and whether the script is being executed at the level the offer requires. JJ will run through it.
Kevin started call tracking from Day 1 — which JJ praised. Being at call one and already tracking is the right move.
