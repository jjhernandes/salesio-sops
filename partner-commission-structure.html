How to Pay a Rep Who Closes a Referral Partner

When a salesperson lands a partnership like SimilarWeb — not a direct customer, but a source of referred businesses — standard commission math breaks down. Here's how to structure it.

The Problem

Three reasons you can't pay standard rate on partner deals

01 The price is already discounted. Agency and partner deals come in at a reduced rate — the partner marks up to their clients and keeps margin. If you're netting $3,500 on a deal that normally closes at $5,000, paying 10% of $5,000 is paying 14% of what you actually received.
02 The rep didn't close the end customer. They closed the partnership — a one-time act. Every business that comes through after that is mostly passive inbound. That's a different contribution than working a direct sale from cold to close.
03 Perpetuity exposure is real. A single good partnership could pay out for years. If SimilarWeb sends 15 businesses a month at 10% commission, you've created a liability that grows with volume and never stops.
Why the Numbers Change

Direct sale vs. partner-referred deal

Using a $5,000 offer as the example. Partner discount of 30% (agency pricing). Standard commission of 10%.

Direct Sale
Deal price $5,000
Your revenue $5,000
Commission (10%) $500
Your net $4,500
Partner-Referred Deal
Deal price to partner $3,500
Your revenue $3,500
Commission at 10% $350 — still too high
Commission at 3% $105
Your net at 3% $3,395
The Principle
Commission should be calculated on revenue received, not the end-user price. On a discounted partner deal, paying 3% of what you actually collected is the right baseline — it's still meaningful to the rep, and it doesn't erode your margins on an already-discounted deal.
Recommended Structure

Two-part payout: signing bonus + micro-commission

Separate the act of closing the partnership from the ongoing credit for referrals. They're different contributions and should be compensated differently.

Part 1 — Partnership Signing Bonus One-time
When paid When the agreement is signed and the first referral converts — not on signing alone
Amount Flat $250 – $1,000 based on partner size. SimilarWeb (20K users) warrants the high end
Why flat No revenue at signing to % against. Flat fee rewards the close without creating perpetuity
Validation Requiring first referral to convert protects against dead partnerships that never produce
Part 2 — Referral Stream Commission Capped + time-limited
Rate 2 – 3% of revenue received per closed referral (not the end-user price, not your list price)
Time cap Commissions expire 12 months from the date the partnership goes live
Volume cap Max $3,000 – $5,000 total earned from any single partner source, regardless of time remaining
Whichever first Time cap OR volume cap — whichever is hit first ends the payout
Cap Mechanics

How to protect against runaway payouts

Time Cap
12-month window
Partnership commissions expire one year after the first referral converts. After that, the stream is house revenue. Simple to track, easy to communicate upfront.
Volume Cap
$3–5K per partner
No matter how productive the partnership gets, the rep's total payout is capped. Especially important for high-volume partners like SimilarWeb — protects you from a single deal becoming a six-figure liability.
Worked Example — SimilarWeb
Rep closes SimilarWeb. Signing bonus: $750 (paid when first business converts). Over the next 12 months, SimilarWeb refers 40 businesses that close at $3,500 each. At 3% commission = $105/deal × 40 deals = $4,200. Volume cap of $4,000 kicks in — rep earns $4,000, not $4,200. Total rep payout: $750 + $4,000 = $4,750. Your gross from the same 40 deals: $140,000. That's 3.4% total cost of the partnership channel — fair for both sides.
Rules to Set in the Agreement

What to put in writing before the rep goes to work

  • Commission is calculated on revenue received — never the end-user price or list price
  • Partner deals and direct deals are separate commission tiers. Make this explicit from day one
  • The signing bonus requires first-referral conversion — not paid on the partnership agreement alone
  • Time and volume caps both apply. Whichever is hit first ends the payout stream
  • If the same rep later closes a referred business directly on a upsell or renewal, that closes under the direct commission table — not the referral rate
  • If a referred business is also an existing direct prospect in the pipeline, the sourcing credit goes to whoever touched them first — define this before it becomes a dispute
Bottom Line

The short version

A flat signing bonus ($500–$1,000) when the partnership actually produces, plus 2–3% on referred revenue for 12 months, capped at $3,000–$5,000 total per partner. Not perpetuity. Not full rate. Not on discounted price. Rep still gets meaningful credit for building the channel — you keep the upside as volume scales.

The simplest version: if deal size is consistent, just do a flat $75–$150 fee per converted referral with a monthly cap of $500. No percentages, no math disputes.
