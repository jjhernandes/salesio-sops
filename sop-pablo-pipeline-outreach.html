Pipeline Outreach SOP · v1.0

Contact These Leads.
In This Order. This Way.

You have warm leads sitting in 4 pipelines. Most of them have had zero follow-up. This document tells you exactly who to contact, when, how, and what to say.

Pipeline Priority
Priority 1
Coaching Dropoffs
108 leads
Filled out a full survey showing high intent for coaching. Got to the purchase page and fell off. Zero follow-up has gone out. These are the most qualified leads you have and nobody has talked to them.
Priority 2
Webinar Attendees
Fresh list
Just attended a live webinar — they are warm right now. The window closes fast. Call them today or tomorrow while the content is still fresh in their mind.
Priority 3
Ticket Abandonments
29 leads
Filled out info for the June event and didn't buy. June is approaching — time pressure is already built in. Urgency messaging will convert a chunk of these fast.
Priority 4
E-book Buyers
Active list
They already paid money — they're buyers. Email automations are running. Add SMS and use this list to bridge toward coaching or the event. Lower urgency but easy upsell path.
⚠
Start with Priority 1 every single day until the 108 coaching dropoffs are worked through. Do not skip ahead to easier pipelines because the volume is smaller. The coaching dropoffs have the highest intent.
Contact Sequences

This is your first touch. Lead is cold to you but warm from intent. Be human, not salesy. Goal is to get them on a conversation — not to pitch.
Day

Email
Send a short, personal follow-up email.
Reference that they filled out the form. Keep it 3-4 lines. No long pitches. Subject line should feel like a reply, not a marketing email.
Day
4
Call SMS
Second call attempt + follow-up SMS if no answer.
Different time of day than Day 1. If you called at 10am on Day 1, try 3pm today. Reference your previous message in the SMS.
Day
7
SMS Email
Final touch — breakup message.
Let them know this is your last reach out. Give them a clear and easy way to respond yes or no. If still no response after this, move to nurture sequence.

0–1

Reference the webinar directly. "Hey [name], just wanted to reach out after today's session — did you get a chance to see it?" Speed is everything here.

Mention one specific thing from the webinar content. Shows you're paying attention, not blasting a template.

After 4 days with no response from a webinar attendee, they're going cold. One more clear outreach, then move on.
Priority 3 Ticket Abandonments 29 leads · June event · Time pressure built in
Day
1
SMS Call
SMS first, then call. Lead with the event deadline.
They already showed interest in the event — they filled out the form. Remind them the event is coming and spots are limited. Don't make it aggressive. Make it helpful.
Day
3
Email SMS
Urgency email + SMS with social proof if available.
Mention that spots are filling. If you have a number ("only X tickets left") use it. If not, keep it simple: "Event is in [X] weeks and I want to make sure you have your spot."
Day
6
SMS Call
Last touch — final call + SMS. Release the pressure.
Tell them this is the last time you'll reach out about it and that you'll remove them from the list if they're not interested. Sometimes the release creates the conversion.
Priority 4 E-book Buyers Existing buyers · Email automation running · Add SMS + bridge to offer
Week
1
SMS
Add SMS to the existing email sequence. Send a welcome SMS within 24 hours of e-book purchase.
They're already in an email funnel. Don't interrupt it — add SMS alongside it. The SMS should feel personal, not automated.
Week
2
SMS Call
Follow-up SMS asking if they've applied anything from the e-book. Call the ones who respond.
The goal is to move them from e-book buyer to coaching or event prospect. Get a conversation started. Let the call do the qualifying.
Week
3
Email SMS
Bridge offer — invite them to the event or a free strategy call.
The ask here is low commitment: a conversation or a discounted event ticket. They're already buyers. Don't jump straight to a high-ticket pitch.
Scripts
Never Do This
"Hey [name], I saw you were interested in the coaching program! We have an amazing offer right now and I just wanted to reach out and tell you more about it. Let me know if you have any questions!"
Do This Instead
"Hey [name], noticed you looked at the coaching program a little while back. Wanted to reach out and see if you had any questions or if the timing just wasn't right. Either way — no pressure."
SMS Coaching Dropoffs · Day 1 First Touch
Hey [First Name] — this is [Your Name] reaching out on behalf of [Client Name]. Saw you looked into the blueprint coaching a little while back. Just wanted to check in — did you have any questions or did life just get in the way? No pressure either way.
Keep it short. One question at the end. Do not explain the offer, do not pitch. Wait for a reply before saying anything else.
SMS Coaching Dropoffs · Day 7 Final Touch
Hey [First Name] — I've tried to reach out a couple times and I don't want to keep bothering you. I'll leave you alone after this. If the coaching ever feels like the right move, feel free to reach back out. Wishing you the best either way.
This is the breakup message. Sometimes this converts more than any other touch because it releases pressure. After this, move them to a nurture list — don't delete.
Voicemail All Pipelines · First Attempt
Hey [First Name], this is [Your Name] with [Client Name]. I'm reaching out because you showed some interest in [coaching / the event / the program] and I just wanted to connect briefly — I have a couple of questions for you. My number is [Number]. I'll also send you a quick text. Talk soon.
Under 20 seconds. No pitching in the voicemail. The job of the voicemail is to get them to check the SMS. That's it.
Call Live Answer · Opening
"Hey, is this [First Name]?"

[They say yes]

"Hey, this is [Your Name] — I'm reaching out from [Client Name]'s team. I'm not going to take up a lot of your time — I just had a quick question for you. Is now an okay time for like two minutes?"

[They say yes]

"Perfect. So I saw you looked into [coaching / the event / the program] not too long ago and I just wanted to reach out and see — what made you look into it in the first place?"
That last question is the most important. Stop talking after you ask it. Let them answer. Their answer tells you everything about how to run the rest of the conversation.
SMS Ticket Abandonments · Day 1
Hey [First Name] — saw you grabbed your info for the [Event Name] in June but didn't lock in your spot yet. Just wanted to make sure you didn't miss it — we're getting close on spots. Is the event still something you're interested in?
The event deadline is your best friend here. Use it honestly — don't fake urgency. If there really are limited spots, say so. If not, just reference the date.
SMS E-book Buyers · Welcome SMS
Hey [First Name] — this is [Your Name] from [Client Name]'s team. Just wanted to personally reach out since you grabbed the e-book. Hope it's helpful. If you have any questions as you go through it, feel free to text me here. I'm around.
This is not a sales message — it's a relationship opener. The goal is to make them feel like a human, not a transaction. The sale comes later when you follow up and ask how it's going.
Daily Workflow

Run through this checklist every day before moving on to anything else.

First 30 min: Work the Priority 1 coaching dropoffs. Pull your list for the day, call through them in order. 10-20 calls minimum.
For every no-answer: Drop voicemail + send SMS within 5 minutes. Log the attempt in GHL immediately.
Check SMS replies: Anyone who responded overnight or in the morning gets a reply before you start your call block. Don't let replies sit.
Webinar attendees (if fresh): These get worked immediately after your P1 block while they're still warm. Call through the full list same day or next day.
Ticket abandonments: Send your follow-up SMS or email per the sequence timeline. Check who's on Day 3 or Day 6 and action them accordingly.
End of day: Update all pipeline stages in GHL. Every contact you touched should have a note on what happened. No exceptions.
EOD report: Log your numbers — calls made, connects, responses, calls booked. Track these daily so you can see what's working.
CRM Logging Standard

Every contact must be logged in GHL. If it's not in the CRM, it didn't happen.

After a call
Log: connected or no-answer. If connected — what did they say? What's their situation? What's the next step?
After SMS
Log the message sent and whether they replied. Move the pipeline stage if they responded.
Note format
Date · Channel (call/SMS/email) · What happened · Next step and when. Example: "Apr 22 · Called, no answer. Left VM, sent SMS. Follow up Day 4."
Stage movement
Move the stage immediately when status changes. Don't leave them in "New" after you've already called twice.
Dead leads
After all sequence steps complete with no response, move to "Nurture." Do not delete. These people may convert in 60-90 days.
Non-Negotiables
Respond to incoming messages within 5 minutes
The lead reached back out to you. That is the best signal you can get. Drop everything and respond. Every minute you wait, the interest cools.
Never pitch on the first touch
Your only goal on the first call or SMS is to start a conversation and find out where they're at. The pitch comes after you understand their situation. Not before.
Always follow voicemail with an SMS
Most people don't check voicemails. The SMS is what gets read. Voicemail + SMS together is the standard. One without the other is half the effort for half the result.
Add SMS automation to every pipeline in GHL this week
Right now SMS is missing from most of your pipelines. Email alone is not enough. GHL supports SMS natively — set this up before you do anything else on the automation side.
