Your First 30 Days on a New Offer

How you ramp determines your ceiling on the offer. Most reps try to close before they're ready and blow their trust with the team. Here's how to ramp fast, the right way.

The Principle Behind Ramping Fast

Ramping fast doesn't mean getting on calls immediately and hoping. It means front-loading the inputs so that when you're on calls, you're already operating from internalized knowledge — not guessing.

The fastest ramp is the one where you've done enough pre-work that the first call doesn't feel like a learning experience. It feels like a deployment of what you've already prepared.

The top question to answer before call 1: Can you explain the offer as if you were one of their best customers describing how it changed your life? If yes, you're ready. If not, more product immersion needed.

Week by Week

Days 1-7 Product immersion — become the customer before you sell to them
Know the product story: Every module, every framework, every transformation. Know what it actually does for the people who use it.
Know the customer avatar: Read every testimonial. Who are the people who buy? What was their life before? What changed? The more specifically you know this, the more precisely you can speak to a prospect who matches.
Know the objections: What are the top 5 reasons people don't buy? What are the best responses? Ask the team. Review past call recordings if they're available.
Know the numbers: Average close rate, average deal size, split pay prevalence, chargeback rate. You need these to set your own targets and to answer prospect questions confidently.
Days 8-14 Shadow and practice — watch, then drill
Shadow at least 2 calls from the top closer if possible. Watch how they run discovery. Notice the specific questions they ask and what they do with the answers.
Run at least 3 mock calls with another rep or your coach. Escalating difficulty — first an easy prospect, then a difficult one, then a frame attack drill.
Know the pitch cold. Not scripted — internalized. You should be able to pitch the offer from any angle the prospect's discovery has revealed.
Days 15-21 First real calls — debrief everything
Do a 5-minute debrief after every call. What happened, what worked, what broke. Write it down. Don't move to the next call without doing this.
Record every call and listen back to at least one per day. Look for the moment the call shifted — good or bad — and identify what you did at that moment.
Don't chase close rate yet. Your job in week 3 is to gather data about the offer's specific conversational patterns. Closures will come as you adjust to the offer's specific buyer.
Days 22-30 First ceiling — find it and name it
Track your data. By day 30 you should have enough calls to see your initial close rate on this offer. Compare it to your historical rate.
Identify the first ceiling. What's the consistent pattern in your losses? This is your primary focus for month 2.
Submit a call for coaching review. Use the S12 call submission protocol. One call where you want specific feedback — not "what do you think," but one specific question.

The Mindset for Month 1

Month 1 is not about closing rate. It's about learning rate. The rep who prioritizes data collection in month 1 will have a higher close rate in month 3 than the rep who chases the commission in week 2 and learns nothing in the process.

  • Be patient with yourself. Every offer has its own buyer psychology. Adjustment takes time.
  • Ask for help early. The fastest ramp is the one where you get coaching on the specific gaps early, not after 3 months of repeating the same mistakes.
  • Bring your Sales.io training with you. The frameworks don't change per offer. The specific language and proof points do. Apply the same discovery depth, frame mastery, and state management regardless of what you're selling.
