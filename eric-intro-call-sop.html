Sales.io — Eric's Framework

Intro Call SOP

This is the discovery call — 15 minutes, no pitch. Your job is to find the problem, qualify the person, and make them want to show up to the close call.

15
Minutes max — keep it tight
45
Minute strategy/close call is the goal
2
Call close process — book call 2 here
Before You Dial

Frame of Mind

Your One Job

See if this person has a problem you can solve, they seem serious, they're qualified — and therefore worth taking 45 minutes out of your day to get them on a closing call.

The Three Questions You're Answering

  • What is the problem? — Get them to name it clearly.
  • How bad is it? — Quantify it. Numbers, time, consequences.
  • How big of a priority is it to solve now? — No urgency means no show rate on call 2.

Why People No-Show the Second Call

They do not see value in showing up. Your discovery was not good enough. They do not feel like you can actually help them — or they think it is going to be a sales pitch. Fix this on call 1, not after.

Standards

Do's & Don'ts

Do
  • Qualify with BANT — Budget, Authority, Need, Timing
  • Keep it to 15 minutes max
  • Ask specific questions to get specific answers
  • Find out how much money they're making
  • Focus on the problem and the pain
  • Frame the call upfront
  • Tie them down to wanting the second meeting
  • Confirm a time that 100% works — double tie down
  • Confirm they won't be driving and know how to use Zoom
  • Send the homework video (VSL, case study, or Loom)
  • Figure out what they actually want — position call 2 as the gameplan
  • Plan what you're sending after the Loom to keep them warm
Don't
  • Have an agenda for the call — listen, don't present
  • Over-explain your offer
  • Over-talk — silence is a tool
  • Assume you know their problem before they tell you
  • Type notes — write them by hand
  • Ask a lot of logistical or background questions
  • Go too deep into emotional pain on this call — keep it logical
  • Be distracted or scrolling
  • Drop the price unless it contextually makes sense (they sound broke)
Opening

Energy & Intro

Your Energy on Every Call

  • Positive, upbeat, grounded, calm, friendly, confident
  • You are reading their energy too — disinterested? Humble? Serious? Open? Guarded? Nervous? Distracted? Trying to control the call?
  • Match their pace — mirror energy, do not force yours on them
01
0:00 — 0:30

The Opener

Warm, casual, no pressure. Confirm you have their attention before moving forward.

Script

"Hey [Name]? How's it going, it's Eric here from Instagram. [Wait for response.] Yeah — I had a few minutes here and wanted to reach out. Did I catch you at a bad time?"

Call Structure

Frame the Call

02
0:30 — 1:30

Set the Frame

Take control immediately. Tell them exactly what this call is and what it is not. Remove the sales call energy before you ask a single question.

Script

"So I've got about 15 minutes here before my next meeting. Are you ready to jump in? Awesome. So the goal of this call is simple — I want to understand where you're at in the business, what you might be looking for help with, and from there see if I can help or worst case, point you in a better direction. Does that sound good?"

03
1:30 — 3:00

Opening Question

Reference the DM conversation briefly, then hand the conversation to them. You are listening for the real problem — not the surface answer.

Script

"So I've got a few details from our DM convo and I'm curious — in your opinion, what is the biggest reason you're looking into marketing and growing your brand on Instagram right now?"

Listen for These — Always

The problem. The pain. The cost. The consequences. The implications. Every word they say after your opening question is material. Do not interrupt. Do not jump to your next question before they are done.

Core Skill

Find the Problem

04
3:00 — 7:00

Dig Into the Real Problem

Their first answer is rarely the real problem. Listen for what they say that sounds painful, then pull on that thread. The pain point and the objection often live in the same sentence.

Example

"We're doing SEO and Google ads, and right now it's going pretty well but it's also up and down, and we're looking into other alternatives..."

Pain point: "up and down"  |  Hidden objection: "it's going pretty well"

05
7:00 — 8:00

Summarize and Confirm the Problem

Feed their own words back to them. Make them say yes. This locks in the problem before you go deeper on data.

Script — Confirm the Pain

"Got it — so it sounds like you're wanting to scale but the problem is you're not able to do that through [current method] alone. Is that accurate?"

Script — Layer It

"Okay, so the business is up and down, and [current channels] aren't generating enough consistency in your revenue. Is that right?"

Qualification

Data Gathering

Why You're Gathering Data

You need numbers to close on call 2. The specific metrics you collect now are what make the second call feel personalized and urgent. Gather everything here — you will not get another clean shot at it.

06
8:00 — 11:00

Money — Quantify Everything

  • How are they generating leads right now?
  • How much are they spending on ads — or how much time if it's organic?
  • How many leads this week or month?
  • How many appointments booked?
  • How many closed? At what price point?
  • Do they have other offers, upsells, or a backend?
  • Last month's new revenue — and is that total or net?
  • Total profit or margins?
  • How does that compare to the two months before?
  • Have they ever had higher months?
  • Personal take-home? Other income sources?
07
Weave in naturally

Time & Team

  • How many hours did they work last week or month?
  • Do they have any team members?
08
Weave in naturally

Goals & Qualitative Pain

This is where you get the emotional context — but keep it light on this call. Stay logical and numbers-based. Save the deeper emotional work for call 2.

  • What are other problems related to the numbers?
  • What are they being prevented from doing?
  • What's that costing them personally?
  • What do they want their life to look like?
09
End of data section

Stretch the Pain & Create Urgency

Once you have the data, make the problem feel bigger by attaching it to time.

Stretch

"How long have you been stuck at [X revenue]?"  /  "How long has this been a problem for?"

Urgency

"How quickly are you looking to fix that?"  /  "If you found a way to [solve X], how soon would you want to implement that?"  /  "What's got you wanting to solve this sooner rather than later?"

Budget Check — Use This When You Need To

If they are showing red flags around money, ask early rather than waste the close call:

Script

"Do you have at least $500 a month to put into ads right now? If everything made sense, would you be ready to start right away — or would you need more time?"

If they need time: "How much more time? Any particular reason why?"

Pre-Close

Tie Downs

You Cannot Book the Second Call Without These

Every tie down below must be confirmed before you pitch the second meeting. If you skip one, your show rate suffers.

  • Confirmed need to change. They have a painful problem, you know how bad it is, and they have a reason to act now.
  • Urgency is real. They have answered "how quickly are you looking to solve this?" with something specific — not "whenever."
  • Decision makers identified. Know if anyone else is involved. If yes, they need to be on the second call.
  • Budget confirmed. If red flags exist, ask directly before booking: "How much do you have available to invest in solving this?"
  • They want the second meeting. Tie them down that they actually want the longer call — do not assume a booked slot equals genuine buy-in.
  • Time is locked and double confirmed. "Does [day/time] 100% work for you?" Get a hard yes — not a "should be fine."
  • They will not be driving. Confirm: Zoom or in person. If Zoom, confirm they have used it before.
  • Homework is sent and framed. Send the VSL, case study, or Loom before call 2. Frame it as giving them context so the next call goes faster.
Closing the Intro Call

Transition & Book

10
12:00 — 13:00

Summarize Their Problem Back to Them

Before you pitch anything, mirror their exact situation back using their words. They should be nodding before you say another word.

Script

"So it sounds like the main issue is your business is going through an income rollercoaster because you don't have control over your lead flow. That's causing you to work longer hours than you want to and creating uncertainty around your ability to scale. All of this is coming down to the fact that you don't have a consistent marketing system. Is that a fair summary?"

11
13:00 — 15:00

Pitch the Second Call

Position call 2 as a custom gameplan — not a sales call. The more it feels like you are building something for them, the higher your show rate.

Script

"So I've got another call coming up in a few minutes — but it sounds like this is something I can help with. Here's what I'm thinking: why don't we book a longer meeting where we can go deeper and make sure there's real alignment between what you want and what I can do. From there, if there is, we can build out a custom gameplan on what it would look like to get you to [their goal]. And of course if you want help executing the plan, we can talk about options. How does that sound?"

12
After booking

Send the Homework Video

Frame the VSL or Loom as something that helps them — not as something that helps you sell.

Script

"Before our next call, I'm going to send you a short video — it'll give you context on how we work and help me skip the basics so we can get straight to building your gameplan. Takes about [X] minutes. Can you commit to watching it before we talk?"

Handling Resistance

Objection Handling

Objection
"How much is this going to cost me?"
Response

"Everything is custom — so on the next call we would put together a proposal and go over a few options that actually make sense for where you're at. That's what the strategy session is for."

Objection
"What do you actually do?"
Response

"In short — I help local service businesses get leads from Instagram by building a personal brand and running hyper-targeted local ads. I build the system and then hand it off to you or your team with full training. What that looks like for your business specifically might be entirely different — which is why the next call is fully customized."

Objections on This Call = You Went Too Deep

If you are getting hard objections on the intro call, you probably over-explained the offer or stayed too long. Keep this call about them. Save the pitch for call 2.

When They Ghost

No-Show SOP

A No-Show Is Not the End — Work the Protocol

Most no-shows pick up if you follow the right sequence. Do not send one text and give up. Be persistent without being desperate.

On the call

Wait 5 minutes. Send a quick message immediately.

"Hey [Name], I'm on Zoom now — still good to connect today?"
At 15 min

They are not coming. Start the call-back sequence. Double dial each attempt.

Call 2–3 times total over the next few hours. Space calls 2–4 hours apart. Try FaceTime Audio — higher pickup rates than a regular call.

24 hours

One more outreach attempt. If no response after this, close the loop.

"Hey [Name], I haven't heard back from you so I'm going to go ahead and cancel our meeting. Let me know if you'd like to reschedule in the future."
No response

Move to Nurture. Set a 30-day follow-up task. Do not chase beyond this.

Show Rate Optimization

Keeping Them Warm

Show Rate Starts on the Intro Call

The best reminder sequence in the world cannot fix a bad intro call. They need to leave call 1 feeling like you genuinely understood them and that the second call will be worth their time. Get that right first.

After booking

Send the homework video immediately. Frame it as giving them context, not as a sales tool.

Morning of call

Confirm the time and check if they watched the video.

"Hey [Name], just popping in to make sure you're all set for our meeting today at [time]. Let me know if you need me to re-send that video or if you're all set. Talk soon. Eric"
1–2 hrs later (no response)
"Did you see this?"
Another hour (no response)
"[Name]?"
1–2 hrs before call (still nothing)
"Hey — I didn't hear back from you yet. Did something come up? Let me know if you're still good for [time] or not."

What Actually Drives Show Rate

  • They left the intro call feeling genuinely understood — not pitched
  • They watched the homework video and got curious
  • They confirmed a time that 100% works — not a maybe
  • All decision makers are already committed to attending
  • The second call was framed as a custom gameplan — not a sales presentation
