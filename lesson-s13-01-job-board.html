The Sales.io Job Board

4-6 vetted postings drop every day. Most reps glance and move on. The ones who move on it same day are the ones who land roles.

What the Job Board Is

The Sales.io job board is not a job aggregator. It is not LinkedIn. It is not Indeed. Every company posted has been through our vetting process before it appears. That means you are not starting from scratch every time you see a new listing. The floor has already been raised.

4-6 new vetted postings per day. Live roles, active pipelines, companies that are currently hiring closers and setters right now.

What This Replaces

Most reps waste 80% of their job-search time applying to roles on platforms with zero quality filter. They end up at companies that don't pay on time, have no real pipeline, or lie about close rate data. That is not a rep problem. That is a sourcing problem. The job board solves sourcing so you can spend your time on execution.

Who Posts Here

Operators and offer owners in B2C, B2B, coaching, agency, and software verticals. The common thread: proven revenue, real pipeline, and a defined commission structure. The specifics of each criterion are covered in lesson 2.

What's in Each Posting

Every listing includes the same core data points. Know what to look for before you read a single word of the description.

  • ✓
    Offer vertical and product type. B2C, B2B, SaaS, coaching, agency. Know which vertical you perform best in. Start there.
  • ✓
    Deal size and commission structure. What is the average deal? What is the commission percentage or flat fee? If these are missing or vague, that is worth noting before you apply.
  • ✓
    Current close rate (when available). Some operators share it. A 15%+ close rate on warm inbound is solid. A 2% close rate deserves serious questions before you commit.
  • ✓
    Role type: closer, setter, or both. Some postings are pure closing. Some are hybrid. Know what you are applying for before you reach out.
  • ✓
    Lead source and volume. Where do leads come from? How many calls per week? This tells you the actual earning potential. Commission rate means nothing without lead volume behind it.

The Daily Job Board Routine

01
Every Morning
Check the Board First
Not every other day. Not when you feel like it. Every morning. New postings drop daily and roles fill fast. The rep who checks on day 3 is applying to a role already filled by the rep who checked on day 1.
02
Evaluate
Shortlist 2-3 Opportunities Max
You are not applying to everything. Read the posting, check the data, decide if this is a realistic fit. Shortlist 2-3. Anything more dilutes your effort and the quality of your Loom drops immediately.
03
Execute
Move on It Same Day
This is the one that separates reps who get discovery calls from reps who don't. Good operators are actively hiring. If they get a sharp Loom from another rep today and yours arrives four days later, they are already deep into interviews. Same day. Every time.

"You're not applying for a job. You're evaluating a business partner. The rep who interviews the company gets the offer. The rep who begs for the offer doesn't."

The Competitive Advantage

Every rep in this program sees the same board. That is the level playing field. What is not level is execution speed. Most reps check the board, think "I'll come back to that later," and never move on it. Or they move three days late. Or they apply with a generic text message and wonder why nobody responds.

The rep who moves same day, with a targeted Loom, citing something specific about that company, wins. Not because they are more talented. Because they show up differently than everyone else on that list.

Common Mistakes

Applying to roles outside your current level. If you are new, applying to roles that require 18 months of remote high-ticket experience is not hustle. It is bad targeting. The board has roles for reps at every level. Apply to roles where you are a realistic candidate, then grow from there.

Treating the board like a passive resource. The board is not something you check when you feel like it. It is a live feed of your pipeline for the next 30 days. Your income depends on the offer you are on. Treat this with the same seriousness you treat your close rate.

Sending the same Loom to every company. A generic Loom is just a text application with your face on it. Every Loom needs at least one thing specific to that company. More on this in lessons 7 and 8.
