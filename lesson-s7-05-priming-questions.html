Priming Questions

Questions don't just gather information. They activate emotional states. The right question sequence gets the prospect into the buying state before you've said anything about your offer.

What Priming Questions Do

The Mechanism

Priming in psychology refers to the activation of specific associations in memory — where exposure to one stimulus influences the response to a subsequent stimulus. In a sales context, questions prime by directing the prospect's attention toward specific emotional states, memories, or identities.

When you ask someone "what would your life look like if this problem was completely solved?" — you've activated the part of their mind associated with relief, possibility, and desire. That emotional state is now active when you present your solution. Questions are the mechanism that opens the door.

The buying state isn't a place the prospect arrives naturally. You engineer it through question sequence. Pain vivid. Future vivid. Gap named. Solution clearly the bridge. That sequence doesn't happen by accident.

What the Buying State Actually Is

Four Components

The buying state is a specific emotional configuration. All four elements need to be present before the pitch lands the way it should.

Component What It Means Priming Question That Activates It
Pain Vivid They're feeling the cost of the current situation, not just describing it "How long have you been dealing with this? What's that actually cost you over that time?"
Future Vivid They can see and feel the desired future state in present tense "Describe what your career looks like 12 months from now if this is completely handled."
Gap Acknowledged They see clearly that they can't get to the future from here without something changing "What's been the main thing that's kept you from closing that gap yourself?"
Open to Solution They're ready to hear what can bridge the gap, not skeptical or defended "If there was a way to compress that 3-year timeline into 90 days, what would that be worth to you?"

The Worst Priming Question

"Are you ready to get started?"

This is the worst closing/priming question in sales and it gets asked constantly. Here's why it fails: it puts the prospect in an evaluation mode. They now have to consciously decide if they're "ready" — which activates System 2, creates resistance, and invites objections. It also signals that the rep wants the sale, which collapses the frame.

What to use instead: Any question that invites them to see themselves already in the future. "What would your first 30 days look like if you were in this program?" gets them thinking from inside the decision — not evaluating it from outside.

Priming Question Sequences

Sequence 1 — Pain Activation

"How long has this been going on?" → "What have you tried that hasn't worked?" → "What's it cost you to stay in this spot — in income, in confidence, in time?" → "At this pace, where do you see yourself in 12 months?"

This sequence takes someone from mild dissatisfaction to acute awareness of the cost of staying put. Use it when the prospect isn't fully feeling the pain.

Sequence 2 — Future Activation

"What does your ideal version of this career look like?" → "What would you need to be doing differently to get there?" → "If you were completely on track 6 months from now, what would that look like specifically?" → "What's the number — in income, per month — that would feel like you've broken through?"

This sequence activates aspiration and specificity. Use it when the prospect is emotionally flat or hasn't articulated what they actually want.

Sequence 3 — Pre-Close Priming

"If everything about this program is exactly what you said you needed, what would be the reason not to start today?" → "What would have to be true for this to be the right move?" → "Is there anything that would need to happen before you could move forward?"

This sequence surfaces and pre-empts objections before the close. It also primes the prospect to articulate the conditions under which they'd say yes — which often means they're already thinking about what yes looks like.

Call Exchange — Priming Sequence in Discovery

"How long have you been in this spot — where you feel like you're capable of more but it's not showing up in the numbers?"

The buying state is now active. Pain acknowledged. Future vivid. The next thing the rep says will land on warm ground.

Priming Questions Checklist

  • ✓
    Activate pain with duration. "How long" makes the cost cumulative. It turns a problem into a pattern.
  • ✓
    Ask about failed attempts before you pitch solutions. It establishes that the problem isn't simple, and that what they've tried hasn't worked. Your solution is framed as different.
  • ✓
    Pivot to future before the pitch. Get them speaking from their ambitious self before they evaluate your offer. Aspirational state = open, not defended.
  • ✗
    Don't prime with closed questions. "Are you happy with your current numbers?" gets a yes/no and closes the state rather than opening it.
  • ✗
    Don't rush past the pain. If the prospect says "yeah I'm not hitting my numbers," most reps move on. The elite rep stays: "Tell me more about that. What does that actually feel like on a week-to-week basis?"
