Lesson 3 · Section 3

Step 2 — Current Reality

Surface to depth inquiry. Most reps accept the first answer. Elite reps know the first answer is never the real one.

Surface Reality vs. Actual Reality

What Prospects Say First

When you ask someone about their situation, they give you the version they've rehearsed. The answer they give to everyone. "I'm making $6K a month but I know I can do more." "I'm in sales but I'm not closing consistently." "I want to level up." These are surface answers — true, but not the real picture.

The actual reality lives one or two questions deeper. It's specific. It's embarrassing to some degree. It's the thing they haven't said out loud to many people. That's what you're after.

Your job in Step 2 is not to collect information. It's to understand the specific situation this specific person is actually in right now — not the summary version, the real version.

When you get to the actual reality, two things happen: the prospect feels deeply understood, and you have the raw material to build everything else in the call on.

Most Reps Accept the First Answer

Here's what average looks like: rep asks "What's your situation?" prospect gives a surface answer, rep nods and moves on. The rep now believes they understand the current reality. They don't. They understand the prospect's first-draft version of it.

Accepting the first answer is the single most common discovery mistake. Everything built on top of it — the pitch, the close, the objection handling — is now slightly off. Not catastrophically, but enough to miss.

The Discipline

Before moving to the next question, ask yourself: "Do I actually know where this person is right now — specifically?" If you can't answer that with a concrete, specific picture, you haven't gone deep enough. Go one more question in.

The Question Sequence — Surface to Depth

Q1
Opening Move
The Situation Question
Get the overview. Where are they, what are they doing, what does the landscape look like right now.
"Walk me through where you are right now — what does your month look like in terms of income and activity?"
Q2
Specificity
The Clarifying Question
Take one part of their answer and make it concrete. If they say "it's been inconsistent," find out what inconsistent actually means in numbers.
"When you say inconsistent — what's the range? Like what's a good month versus a bad month look like for you?"
Q3
Depth
The "How Long" Question
Duration creates stakes. Find out how long this has been the situation. A problem that's been happening for 6 weeks is different from one that's been happening for 18 months.
"How long has it been looking like this? Has it always been this way or is this something that's shifted recently?"
Q4
Emotional Truth
The Experience Question
Move from facts to how it feels to be in this situation. This is the bridge into Step 3. You're not asking about the problem yet — you're asking what it's like to be living with it.
"What's that been like? Like when you have a bad month — what does that actually feel like for you?"

What "Current Reality" Means in Context

Not Just the Numbers

Current reality is not just their income. It's the whole picture: what they're doing, what's working, what isn't, how long they've been in this position, and what it feels like to be there. Income is one data point. The full context is what matters.

The Specific Picture

By the end of Step 2, you should be able to describe their situation in their own words, with specific numbers, specific durations, and some emotional texture. If all you have is "rep making $5K wanting more" — you haven't done Step 2. You've gotten a headline.

Don't rush to Step 3 until you have the specific picture. The depth of your discovery determines the accuracy of your pitch. Thin current reality means a thin, generic pitch later — no matter how good your presentation skills are.

Call Exchange — Surface vs. Depth

VERSION 1 — Rep Who Accepts the Surface
Prospect
"Yeah I'm making about $5K a month in sales. I feel like I should be doing more."
Rep
"Totally, yeah. So tell me — what do you feel like is holding you back?"
Jumped straight to problem awareness. Doesn't know what they're actually selling, how many calls they're running, what the variance looks like, or how long this has been happening. The discovery is already thin.
VERSION 2 — Rep Who Goes One Deeper
Prospect
"Yeah I'm making about $5K a month in sales. I feel like I should be doing more."
Rep
"Walk me through what the day-to-day looks like. What are you actually selling, and how many calls are you taking?"
Prospect
"I'm closing for a solar company. About 6-8 calls a day, close rate's probably around 10%."
Rep
"And how long has $5K been the number? Like is that where you've been for a while or is that recent?"
Prospect
"Like nine months. I thought it would improve on its own but it hasn't."
Rep
"Nine months at the same number — what does that feel like? Like when you look at that, what comes up for you?"
Three questions in and now you have real material: the product, the volume, the duration, and the emotional weight. Step 3 is going to land with precision because Step 2 was complete.
